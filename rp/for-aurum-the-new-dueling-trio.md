*~Seven Days After the Quasi Incident~*

The air near the outskirts of the Quasi still felt wrong; it was unsettling, as if the land itself were traumatized. Sol stood at the edge of the fractured terrain, boots planted in ash-covered grass. Cracks veined the earth ahead of him, faintly glowing with residual magic that pulsed and faded like a dying heartbeat. The ruins of the Quasi loomed before him, collapsed inward, its impossible geometry finally stilled.

He held a bouquet in his arms. He had gathered wildflowers from the far edges of campus: sun-bright yellows, soft whites, a few pale blues. He hadn’t arranged them magically; Aurum deserved the courtesy of him doing it by hand.

Sol knelt near the boundary where the earth had split deepest. He swallowed, throat tight, and carefully set the flowers down against the cracked stone.

“Hey,” he said quietly, voice barely carrying over the wind. “I don’t really know how this is supposed to go.” His fingers curled against his knees, knuckles pale.

“I keep thinking you’re going to reappear in a sparkling burst of magic and yell *SURPRISE* or make some cheesy joke; you were always good at the theatrics.” A weak, breathless huff escaped him; it was almost a laugh, but it broke halfway through.

Sol stared at the flowers in front of him. “Thank you,” he said, firmer now. “For everything you’ve done. For helping us fight the lich. For not giving up when things got terrifying. For standing there when the rest of us were barely holding ourselves together.”

He pause.

“And for me,” he added more softly. “For pushing me. For helping me understand what I was feeling instead of just drowning in it. I wouldn’t be able to read emotions as well as I do without you.”

The wind stirred, brushing his hair back. Sol blinked hard. “You were part of my family,” he said. “Whether you realized it or not. And I’m really going to miss you.”

His voice wavered despite his effort to keep it steady. A tear slipped free, tracking down his cheek before he could stop it. He reached forward and adjusted the flowers, setting them more securely against the stone.

“I hope you’re okay,” he whispered. “Wherever you are.”

He stayed there for a few long moments after that, kneeling at the edge of the ruined Quasi, breathing in the quiet and letting the hurt flow.

### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Dec 20, 2025 &middot; 03:21 AM UTC</sub>

Where Sol may have thought he was there alone, it was close to half an hour until that illusion faded and another soul entered his orbit. Soft footsteps approached closer until he’d turn and see Vornakir at his left side, looking very well off compared to how he did shortly after the Quasi collapsed. 

By far he was one of the worst conditioned ones by the time the fight ended, especially considering how many times he actually fell in battle. But today, after a week of resting he looked very well kept. His hair was brushed back and pulled into a half ponytail prominently displaying various strands of the golden undertones.

Just as surprising as his arrival was perhaps the fact he was dressed in his dueling garb. A clean presentable vest dyed a deep gray with silver trim inlayed into the carved designs. Underneath, a black button up shirt with a charm bracelet worn every day since they finished their fight in the Quasi.  Over his left shoulder was the cloak that Sol had crafted him for his Sponsorship Attire, affixed instead to this dueling gear. The rest was unimpressive, just simple tight breeches padded with dyed leather for protection and boots strapped fashionably to tie it all together.

“How long have you been out here? I’d ask if you’ve seen or heard anything, but clearly you wouldn’t be sitting here if you had. Was on my way to the practice field to keep sharp for when the league picks back up and I couldn’t help but notice you sitting out here.”

He didn’t lower himself to sit at his side, but he followed Sol’s stare and let the rest of his thoughts fade into silence to experience whatever it was Sol was.


### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Dec 21, 2025 &middot; 12:29 AM UTC</sub>

Sol was briefly startled when he realized he wasn’t alone anymore. He lifted a hand to his face and brushed the heel of his palm beneath his eyes in a quick motion before turning his head toward the footsteps. Whatever tears lingered didn’t vanish entirely, but he steadied himself as his gaze found Vornakir at his side.

An appreciative smile tugged at his mouth as his eyes traced the polished lines of Vornakir’s dueling attire: the vest, the silver trim, the familiar cape resting over his shoulder. 

“Well,” Sol said, voice warm despite the weight still sitting in his chest, “you look nice.”

He glanced pointedly at the cloak, then back up at Vornakir. “Good to see you’re taking care of my designs. You wear them better than most.” He grinned. “You should give Cas a tip or two on clothing maintenance. I’ve already had to fix his dueling outfit once.” A soft laugh slipped out of him.

Sol remained seated, but he shifted his weight so Vornakir could see him more fully. He wore an off-white cotton shirt, dipped low at the sternum to define and draw attention to his chest; its sleeves billowed before cinching neatly at the cuffs, and it had brown autumnal embroidery catching the light along the collar and shoulders. He wore a tawny sash draped at his waist that echoed the same autumn patterns, its ends stirring faintly in the breeze. His hair was half up, half down as usual, but today it was tied back with a slim golden ribbon that gleamed softly in the afternoon light.

“I haven’t been out here that long,” Sol said after a moment, following Vornakir’s gaze back toward the ruined Quasi. “Maybe an hour? I just wanted to say a proper goodbye to Aurum.”

His eyes flicked briefly to the flowers resting against the stone. “And take a second to listen to nature,” he added quietly. “It’s always talking out here, you just have to give it time.”

He patted the ground beside him in invitation. “You’re welcome to join me if you wish.”

A pause stretched between them as Vornakir spoke about dueling. “I honestly haven’t thought much about dueling lately,” Sol admitted. “Everything else going on kind of drowned it out, but I think I’ll enjoy it again when the league opens back up.”

He tilted his head, glancing sideways at Vornaki, a spark of playful challenge returning to his eyes. “Fair warning, though,” he added, “if you’re not careful, I might surpass your rank sooner than you expect.”

The breeze stirred again, carrying the scent of ash and wildflowers, as Sol settled back into the quiet.

### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Dec 23, 2025 &middot; 06:55 PM UTC</sub>

Sol paid close attention to Vornakir as he spoke. When the comment about Caspian landed, he laughed.

“Yeah,” Sol admitted, smiling. “That’s fair. I think I’ll have to design *around* your work styles.” He glanced up at Vornakir, eyes bright, “I’ll remember it for your next outfits; Cas gets something built to survive being dragged through the nine hells, and you get something that survives scrutiny down to the last stitch.”

His gaze followed Vornakir’s to the flowers, listening as he spoke about the Barricade. Sol’s expression softened, thoughtfulness coloring his features.

“Everything can be beautiful,” Sol said quietly. 

He lifted one hand and prismatic magic danced between his fingers. With a flick of his wrist, the magic shot off into the air, and a chaotic spray of light motes burst into existence around them. As the colored lights jittered, they created a clashing cacophony of hues that shouldn’t have worked together by any rules of art theory.

Sol tilted his head. “But sometimes,” he continued, “all you just have to do is look at things from a new perspective.”

Sol lifted his index finger and gave it a twirl; the motes twisted and spiraled inward, their discord smoothing into harmony. They arced upward like fireworks, brilliant streaks refracting every color of the rainbow across their faces before bursting overhead. The lights bled together, stretching and weaving until a luminous aurora unfurled above them, bathing the Quasi ruins in color.

“They’ll always be beauty in the Barricade,” Sol said, smiling. “You just have to look at what is typically considered drab, monotonous, or dull, differently.”

With a final flick of his fingers, the spell dissipated and collapsed into a gentle shower of golden magic that drifted down around them, catching briefly in their hair and clothes before fading away.

When Vornakir spoke of training and the way he, Sol, and Caspian complemented each other, Sol nodded thoughtfully.

“I agree,” he said. “We match each other really well. And yeah, I *have* been wanting to learn more combat magic. Auri’s been helping me lately.” He shrugged lightly. “But there’s no such thing as too much practice.”

Then he paused. Sol’s brow creased, and he tilted his head as something brushed against his senses: determination, hesitation, questioning. His lips curved, and he let out a quiet laugh.

“I felt that,” he said, amused. “Your emotions are kind of loud when you’re thinking hard, Vornakir,” he said in jest. “If you have a question, you can just ask me, it’s not like I’m going to hurl a lightning bolt at you or anything.” He gave his friend a joking wink, clearly making light of their previous quarrel.

Sol’s smile widened, that spark of excitement in his eyes sharpening. “I mean, think about it,” he said, gesturing between the three of them. “Vornakir with his overwhelming long-range spells, Caspian holding opponents back at close range with his defenses and raw power, and me weaving trickery around you both.” His eyes flickered with quiet confidence. “The battlefield would be controlled from every angle,” Sol added, smiling with his hands held behind his head. “We’d be a pretty unstoppable.”

Sol inhaled slowly, then, with deliberate care, he reached up and untied the golden ribbon from his hair. His hair spilled free, catching the light as it fell to his shoulders. He wrapped the ribbon twice around his wrist, anchoring himself, then extended the remaining ends outward towards Vornakir and Caspian.
