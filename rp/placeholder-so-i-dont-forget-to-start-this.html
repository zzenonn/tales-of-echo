Early that morning, Ancient Star Second, Vornakir arose and began his preparations. He'd already spent the prior week setting the stage by passing out information and flyers to inform everyone where this little 'gala' party was to take place and when; He'd pulled some strings to get permission to throw a party in the Quasi. It took a little bit of favor work for a couple of his professors but once he'd gotten his permission it was all just a game of throwing a party. And gods, was he a picture perfect host.

The Quasi itself was able to conjure up the decorations - Gold, everywhere of course. Tables with black and gold cloths draped over then, streamers and banners, all the proper fixings for a nice little get together. He was, of course as pretentious as ever but it was part of his charm. At least, he liked to think so. The drinks and finger foods he prepared himself and carted them in since the Quasi couldn't produce those. Sandwiches, crackers and cheeses, miscellaneous Hors d'oeuvres and of course several glasses of wine, water, fragrant fruity juices and alcoholic beverages. He went all out to ensure all his guests could have things to indulge in.

Vornakir the host greeted each person himself as they arrived, offering them a drink upon their entry and gesturing them inside or leading particular people to their friends as they arrived. It wasn't quite on the scale of something grand like he was likely used to, but it was a slice of his life back home. This was a taste of royalty, and of course Vornakir wore a princely crown to indicate just who was the man of the hour in case anyone had forgotten. More so, he wore the outfit that Sol had designed him for Sponsorship day as well.

After the guests who RSVP'd arrived, and everyone was settled with a drink and their snacks it was time to socialize and he was just as much the expert at that; Seamlessly weaving in and out of conversations with half a dozen people as he made the rounds. He lingered longest speaking to Kael and Ysolde, and Nimbus with Pyrrah, and an Orcish girl he'd beaten in a duel once then befriended later named Irelia. But all the names they'd grown used to were present and some he wasn't quite acquainted with.

Naturally, he would eventually gravitate toward the rest of his dormmates, casting a gaze over his shoulder - Ysolde and Pyrrah both nearby where his gaze remained. To whom he lingered those auric eyes on, it was anyone's guess. But any of his friends might capture that it was infatuation at the very least that gleamed in his eyes.

Sol/Sunni

The doors of the Quasi parted with a crackle of magic, announcing Sol's arrival. In his arms, he held several enchanted lanterns; their warm gold light spilled across the decorations already in place, catching the banners and streamers and making the entire hall glow that much brighter.

“Sorry I’m late, had to steal these away from a supply closet,” he called out, half joking.

Sol flicked his fingers, and the lanterns soared toward the ceiling, snapping into a coordinated constellation-like arrangement. With a second gesture, he summoned a sweeping arc of color: a lightning-laced aurora unfurling across the upper dome of the Quasi, shimmering in gold and soft storm-blue hues. It crackled faintly, just enough to echo the same motifs Sol had embroidered into Vornakir’s Sponsorship Day attire.

He turned toward Caspian and Vornakir, and immediately, his eyes fixed on the prince’s outfit.

“Oh. Absolutely not.”

Sol strode closer, sweeping his hands down towards Vornakir’s torso with the same intensity as someone examining a crime scene. “I cannot have you looking like a seven when you’re a ten on your birthday. Don’t worry, I can fix this.”

A quick snap sent golden, silver, and bronze sparks dancing across the drow’s outfit. The storm patterns ignited in soft motion, shifting like real storm clouds. The scales along the outfit shone brighter and caught the aurora’s glow, refracted it, and made Vornakir look like a storm incarnate.

“There,” Sol said, stepping back and dusting off his hands, clearly satisfied. “Now you look like you own the place.”

He shot Caspian a grin, bumping their shoulders together before elbow-nudging him meaningfully.

“So…” Sol said, eyes flicking knowingly toward Ysolde and Pyrrah across the room, “am I playing wingman for our prince tonight?”

He wiggled his eyebrows at Vornakir.

“I saw that look earlier. I can orchestrate a cute meeting so smooth it’ll feel like fate.”

Before Vornakir could answer, Sol turned the same mischievous gaze onto Caspian.

“And you, Cas.” Another elbow. Sharper this time. “Do I need to wingman for you, too? Because if you’re still entertaining that witch—”

His entire face wrinkled like he’d tasted something sour.

“—I will personally hex you until your taste in women improves.

He crossed his arms, mock-stern.

“There’s plenty of gorgeous women here tonight. Let me shove you toward one that actually has a soul…and maybe some manners. Please. My sanity depends on it.”
