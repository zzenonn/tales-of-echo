# Basking in the Sunset

### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Jan 31, 2026 &middot; 07:29 AM UTC</sub>

After a long an arduous day of training, Alden steps out from the barracks building and begins to walk down a stone path leading to an overlook. As he walks, he hears the sounds of wyverns screeching in the distance, and eventually catches glimpse of his own, flying with its fellow kin. After a few minutes of walking, Alden finally arrives to his secret spot: A small, tucked away overlook that faces towards the west - a beautiful spot to catch the sunset.

The stone terrace is still warm beneath Alden’s knees when he finally slows his breathing. His arms ache, burning in that deep, honest way that only hours of rein holding, wind, and flight can bring. Somewhere above, wyverns cry out as they’re beginning to settle in for the night -  Solkara, his personal wyvern, calls out - her voice cutting through the others like a blade of light. He smiles faintly at that. Alden bows his head, the sigil of the Morninglord resting against his chest.

“Lathander…
Dawnfather.  Morninglord. Bringer of first light and second chances.”

The wind tugs at his cloak as he speaks, carrying the scent of cloud.

“Today tested me.”
“My grip faltered. My balance failed. I doubted myself when the sky felt too wide and my strength too small.”

He exhales slowly.

“But I rose again. Every fall ended in life, not death. Every mistake became a lesson.”
“For that mercy, I thank you.”

A quiet pause.

“Watch over Solkara tonight. Let her wings rest easy. Let her know trust where I still learn it.”

He presses his palm lightly to the keepsake he carries, then to his heart.

“And when the dawn comes… let me meet it worthy.”

When Alden rises, he doesn’t go back inside.

Instead, he continues to walk.

Beyond spires laced with banners flapping in the wind - past rope bridges glowing faintly with golden hour light. Until Skyweave opens into a high overlook, there remains nothing but air, horizon, and the long fall of the world below.

The sunset catches the city just right.

Gold spills across stone, turning the clouds into fire and the wyverns into living silhouettes of legend. Solkara perches nearby, her wings folding as amber light traces through the veins like molten glass.

Alden leans on the railing, eyes soft.

“You paint even the ending of the day with hope,” he murmurs.
“As if to remind us that light never truly leaves… it just waits its turn.”

He closes his eyes, but not to pray this time, but simply to feel.

The wind.
The height.
The fragile, breathtaking truth that he is alive. Learning. Becoming.

“Thank you,” he whispers.
Not to the sky, but to the life within it.

Alden remains at the overlook long after the city begins to quiet. The sky deepens from gold to ember, then to a soft rose that bleeds into violet. Light drapes itself across Skyweave’s spires like a benediction, catching on banners, stone, and scale alike. Even the wind seems to slow, as though unwilling to disturb the moment.

He lets out a soft breath, almost a laugh.

“Only you would make the day’s ending feel like a promise,” he murmurs.

His gaze follows the sun as it dips below the horizon, its final rays igniting the clouds in one last radiant display.

“If this is how you mark the close of a day,” Alden says quietly,
“then tomorrow must be something worth waking for.”

He rests a hand over his heart, warmth lingering there long after the light fades.

“Thank you, Lathander… for beauty where there could have been only exhaustion.”

And for a few precious moments, Alden simply stands—
bathed in afterglow,
content to witness a miracle meant for anyone willing to look.


### &#x270D; Calarel
<sub>Feb 02, 2026 &middot; 02:35 AM UTC</sub>

Normally, Calarel didn’t interrupt Alden during his quiet walks or the moments he spent paying respect to his god. She understood devotion once, enough to know when not to intrude on it. Tonight was different. After the difficult training that day, she needed the sky, and this was the only hour when she didn’t mind the color gold.
 
She approached without a sound. The last of the sun glanced off her ice-blue skin, pale as winter light. Elven grace sharpened her features— the taper of her ears, the clean lines of her face— but her orcish blood revealed itself through the small tusks resting at her lower lip. Dark hair hung loose at her shoulders, and her lavender eyes stayed fixed on the horizon. 

Silence stretched after she stopped beside him. Her halberd rested across her back out of habit. Then, without looking at him, she said, “You lost your balance earlier.” It wasn’t meant to be hurtful, just stating the facts. “But you fixed it.” Her eyes flicked to him briefly, sharp and assessing, then returned to the sunset. “I’m glad you’re safe.”


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 02, 2026 &middot; 03:53 AM UTC</sub>

Alden doesn’t startle at her presence. He had felt it - someone stepping into the quiet with care rather than intrusion.
At her words, he exhales a soft, regretful breath, the corner of his mouth lifting just slightly.

“Yeah,” he admits. “For a moment there… I thought the sky had finally decided I didn’t belong in it.”

He shifts his weight unconsciously, as if remembering the sway of Solkara’s wings beneath him.

“But she trusted me,” he continues, eyes still on the horizon. “And I remembered to trust myself.”
A pause. Then, quieter, “And my god.”

When Calarel says she’s glad he’s safe, Alden finally turns his head toward her. The sunset paints gold across her features, catching against ice-blue skin, and for a second he looks almost struck by it.

“Thank you,” he says simply, no bravado, no polish. Just honesty.

After a moment, he gestures gently toward the burning sky before them.

“Funny thing,” he adds. “After a day like today, I expected exhaustion to be all I could feel. But then the light does this…”
He smiles, soft and reverent.
“…and it feels like Lathander reminding me that falling isn’t failure. It’s just part of learning how to rise.”

He looks back to the sunset, standing beside her now - not alone, not crowded.

“Glad you found your way up here too.”


<sub>Feb 02, 2026 &middot; 04:07 AM UTC</sub>

Alden lets the quiet return after his words, the wind filling the space where conversation doesn’t need to rush.

“For me,” he adds after a moment, voice low and thoughtful, “this is the hour that feels closest to him. Not dawn, not prayer… but this.”
He nods toward the horizon, where gold fades into deeper hues.
“The light hasn’t left yet, it’s just choosing where to rest.”

He shifts slightly at the railing, giving her space rather than taking it.

“I come up here when I need reminding that the world is still beautiful, even after it’s wrung you dry,” he says. “That effort doesn’t erase wonder.”

His gaze drifts back to her, with a gentle curiosity rather than pressing.

“What about you, Calarel? What brings you to the sky when the day is finally done?”


### &#x270D; Calarel
<sub>Feb 04, 2026 &middot; 07:40 PM UTC</sub>

Calarel didn’t answer Alden’s question right away. She studied the gold slipping into violet and the last warmth bleeding out of the day. For a moment, her expression was undreadable. She shifted her weight and rested one forearm on the railing. Lavender eyes flicked to him briefly, then away again. 

“It’s the one time the world doesn’t ask anything of me,” she said. “I can just…exist. It’s harder to feel bitter when the light is still trying its best to be so beautiful.”


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 06:40 AM UTC</sub>

Alden listens without interrupting, his expression softening as her words settle.

“That makes sense,” he says quietly. “Light like this doesn’t demand anything... It doesn't bark orders at us, it doesn't request us to complete any tasks, it doesn't ask us to study... It just... offers itself.”

He watches the last trace of gold fade from the clouds, voice calm and sincere.

“I think that’s why I thank Lathander for moments like this,” he adds. “Not because he asks us to be better here… but because he lets us rest in the trying. I apologize if I speak too much of my lord - I just... Have so much to thank him for. He serves as the light that guides me when the fog becomes too thick.”

A brief pause.

"Sometimes, I wonder if we were brought here for a reason - some grandeur purpose maybe. I try and ask Lathander what that purpose could be but it always seems as if my words fall upon deaf ears... Never once have I received an answer." 

A puzzled look appears upon Alden's face.

 "It's made me wonder... if maybe I'm not to know until the end..."

Alden takes a deep breath, and then releases a sigh of frustration - but then straightens up, and a smirk begins to slide across his face.

“I’m glad you found a place where you don’t have to carry everything,” he says, eyes still on the horizon.


### &#x270D; Calarel
<sub>Feb 06, 2026 &middot; 03:24 AM UTC</sub>

The sky dimmed to a bruised violet now, the last edge of gold thinning along the clouds like an ember refusing to die. Wind slid over the overlook, tugging at the loose fall of her hair. She watched Alden while he spoke. The way he held himself when he mentioned Lathander— the ease, the certainty. The absence of any doubt, even when he admitted to unanswered prayers. 

When he finished, Calarel shifted her weight and rested both forearms on the railing, posture relaxed but alert, as if to be ready to move at a moment’s notice. Amethyst eyes followed the horizon for another beat before finally settling on him again. 

“What made you decide to trust him like that? Your god?” she asked, unflinching. “Is it a faith you were raised to believe, or did it find you in some other way?”


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 06, 2026 &middot; 10:51 PM UTC</sub>

The question settles into him, deeper than the wind, deeper than the fading light. His gaze lingers on the horizon, watching as the sky darkens and the faintest stars begin to shine.

“I wasn’t born to it,” he says at last, voice calm but surrounded with something quieter....

“Some of my village believed in the Morninglord, sure. Offerings at festivals, prayers when children were born, that sort of thing. But belief…” He shakes his head slightly. “Belief is easy when life is kind.”

His hands rest against the stone railing, fingers tracing the weathered grooves left by years of wind.

“When sickness came, it didn’t feel like dawn,” he continues. “It felt like the sun forgot where we were.”
A brief pause follows.

“I prayed then out of fear. Out of anger too, if I’m being truthful. I asked why light would exist at all if it could leave people in that kind of dark.”

The wind flows softly through Alden's white hair as he exhales.

“I didn’t get an answer. Not words. Not a miracle that saved everyone.” His eyes soften, reflecting the darkening sky. “What I got was morning… still coming. Every single day. Even when I didn’t want to see it. Even when I thought it had no right to rise again.”

He turns his head slightly toward her, expression steady but deeply sincere.

“That’s when it changed for me. *Faith* stopped being something that prevents suffering… and became something that refuses to let suffering be the final chapter.”

His gaze drifts upward where the first faint stars begin to prick through the violet sky.

“Lathander, to me, isn’t perfection. He’s persistence. The promise that light returns whether we think we deserve it or not… whether we’re ready for it or not.”

Alden’s voice lowers slightly, reverent but not performative - simply true.

“I trust him because he never promised a world without night. Only that dawn will always follow.”

He lets the silence breathe between them for a moment before adding softly,

“And that was enough to keep me standing when I might have chosen not to.”


### &#x270D; Calarel
<sub>Feb 09, 2026 &middot; 03:31 AM UTC</sub>

The stars were coming in now. Faint, stubborn points of light pressing into the dark. The wind lifted loose strands of her dark hair, brushing against her ice-blue cheek, and she didn’t bother to still them. The city below had gone quieter, the world holding its breath between day and night.
When she finally spoke, her voice was quieter than before. 

“...Persistence,” she repeated, testing the word and turning it over like a blade in her hand.

The tight line of her mouth eased slightly. Her expression shifted into something less guarded and more honest.

“I used to trust in the gods when I was small,” she said simply. “Now I trust in myself.” Her hand rested against the railing. “The stars may vanish, and the sun may fail to shine. The world may be eclipsed by darkness.” She lifted her chin just a fraction, eyes reflecting starlight. “But I will keep getting back up,” she continued. “I will not be silent when the innocent call for help.” 

She turned to look at him, her gaze cool but sincere. “And I believe you wouldn’t either.”


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 15, 2026 &middot; 10:25 PM UTC</sub>

Alden doesn’t answer right away. He watches the stars with her, those quiet points of defiance against the dark, and something in his chest settles.

“I don’t think we’re so different,” he says softly. “You trust your strength. I trust the light that helps me to use mine."

He turns to her, meeting her gaze without flinching.

“If the stars vanished,” Alden continues, “I’d still rise. And if the dawn failed… I’d try to be it, in whatever small way I could.” A faint, earnest smile touches his mouth. “Faith doesn’t make me passive. It makes me accountable, present... Not alone.”

He nods once, slow and certain.

“And you’re right,” he says. “I wouldn’t be silent either. You have a good soul Calarel - I can see it. Whether you believe it or not, I believe we were all brought here for a reason. Reasons that we will figure out ourselves... I just pray that we make the right choice when the time comes.”
