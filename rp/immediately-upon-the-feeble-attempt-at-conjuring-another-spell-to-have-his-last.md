# Immediately upon the feeble attempt at conjuring another spell to have his last ...

### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 05:53 AM UTC</sub>

Immediately upon the feeble attempt at conjuring another spell to have his last ditch to win in this somewhat close duel with such a massive gap of power, Vornakir's breathing hitched and darkness began to cloud the edge of his vision. The spell sputtered out in his hand as burnout flared in his chest and caused extreme fatigue to wreck his mind. Where previously he was so confident in being able to win, now he was so...humanized. Fallible. In a way, it made him seem so much less prodigal to see how frail he appeared after falling unconscious. Normally so proud, and standing firm in his convictions. Now simply a one armed man pushed to his limit so thoroughly that he fainted, laying face down in the dirt after a loss. 

It seemed he wasn't capable of getting up, not just because of the arm but fully due to the fact that he lost all grasp on consciousness from how far he pushed himself and was subsequently forced to be carried to the infirmary for observation and care. Those who cared for him after the Incident™ would have a small flash back to the night they carried him back to their dorm, seeing him lay frail in bed without an arm and his fine clothes soiled by the ground he landed on. 

Visions swirled in his minds palace, far from dreaming. Echoes of a cavern haunting his thoughts, twisting and writhing deep beneath the surface in the Barricade. A growl that rumbled like a thousand feet-across engine turning over slowly in the dead of winter careened through bouncing off the walls and giving the illusion that it was disembodied. Pebbles on the stone floor quaked, small loose rocks tumbled from the walls as it carried in a whisper that was for his ears only thousands of miles away; He'd heard these whispers, all of the Scaled Ones do. Even able to speak the Draconic tongue, he could never decipher the words as if they were a long dead ancient dialect never taught. 

But somehow now, some of the words found purchase. Some of them struck and resonated within him, and he *understood them*. It shook him to his core as those words echoed in his mind and bounced off the inner sanctum walls just as it had the cavern. 

*"Pride...Bloodline...Gift..."* Some burning rage billowed in his chest, causing him to thrash in his unrest until a sharp gasp tore through his lips and he sat up in the bed suddenly. 

It took but several short moments for him to realize where he was...And what had happened. He didn't even turn to face whomever it was sitting with him, he was no fool. He knew *one of them* were there, surely. "Please send for Kael," His voice barely scratched out of his throat as shame bore down on his shoulders and his ochre eyes lost their luster as they stared downtrodden at the sheets over his legs. "I have a promise to keep, and I will not be shamed anymore than I already have by not upholding my word."

### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 06:09 AM UTC</sub>

Zašia looked up from the leaf of parchment she'd been writing on, sudden and startled. Her quill scratched across the surface, the tip breaking and ink smearing over her immaculate handwriting. Quickly, she set her homework aside and stood to lean over the bed.

"Hey," the aasimar called to him, one hand reaching out to touch his face and turn it toward her. "Hey." She cracked a shaky little smile. "All that worry you put me through and the first thing to grace your lips is another woman's name?" A breath of laughter was even shakier than that. "Not a chance."

Rather than expect him to lay down again, she grabbed a pillow she'd had leaning against the side of the bed and set it behind him so that he could sit propped up comfortably. With more seriousness, she insisted, "Not yet. Everybody needs to make sure you're okay first. Then, yeah. Sure. I'll go and fetch Kael myself if you want."

He had to know — even if potentially only distantly — how mortifying that had the potential to be for her.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 06:23 AM UTC</sub>

He frowned over at Zašia and his brows knit together in mild frustration but more so at himself than anything else. His voice was a bit on the hollow side as he murmured back to her, "I assumed it wouldn't be you in here with me. I wasn't sure you'd want to after you put your faith in me and I..." his lips formed a tight line and he tried to look away. This may very well be the first time she'd ever seen him so sullen, so fragile. It wasn't just the fact that he lost, it wasn't just the fact that he'd failed. It was in front of so many people that held such *heavy* expectations on him, including the Professor that put him to the task in the first place - The very same that insinuated if he worked hard he may become the next Cynthia. To fail in front of them, to fail in front of her after she made a public display of herself for him. To fail after wanting so badly to win, not just for his pride and not just for his image but to *make a friend that wasn't obligated* the same way his previous comrades had become his friends. 

All of this coming just days after he lost his arm, just days after he had to come to the realization that he was fallible. It was almost the worst way a perfect storm could brew against him. He leaned forward to let her put the pillow behind him. He shook his head at her though, "No, not you. I don't want to put you out more than you've already been on my behalf lately. When someone else worthwhile comes by, just don't let me forget to send for them please. I told her I would raise her hand myself." He said, sure of himself that he needed to do this. He gave his word.


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 06:35 AM UTC</sub>

"And when you have more strength, you will," Zašia promised. Carefully, she smoothed a strand of dark hair away from his face and tucked it behind his ear.

"I don't care that you lost," she insisted, her voice kept quiet. "I told you, I'm not out for your power or what it represents or what opportunities it would afford me to be attached to it." For that, she'd rather have her own. "I didn't cheer for Lord Vanori, I cheered for Vornakir. And he made an incredible showing despite having to re-learn even his walking gait, against a foe that outranked him by more than one degree."

She held his face in both hands so he would understand just how insistent she was when she told him: "You were magnificent."

Then she cleared her throat and let her hands fall away, leaning back again. Suddenly self-conscious. Zašia set herself to tidying up her discarded homework while she waited for the blaze beneath the skin of her face to die down.


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 06:41 AM UTC</sub>

The door creaked as Redd slipped inside, pausing when he caught Zašia’s voice, steady and sure in the hush of the infirmary. He hesitated a half-step, as though expecting the healers to shoo him back out, then caught sight of Zašia perched beside the bed. His smirk came too quickly.

“Guess I’m late to the pep talk,” he said, though his voice didn’t carry its usual bite, eyes flicking over Vornakir. The sight of him upright and breathing steadier made Redd’s shoulders drop before he even realized they’d been tense. “Looks better than when I had to scrape him off the arena floor. You should’ve seen the professors trying to keep me from carrying him myself.”

When he stopped at the edge of the bed, he leaned a hand against the post, fingers tapping restlessly. The smirk slipped, just for a moment. “You can’t do this to us so soon, Scales,” he murmured, softer now, the words landing heavier than he meant them to. “Not after everything.”

For a moment, he let the silence hang between them. Then, with a faint shake of his head, he added, “You had her rattled, you know,” Redd went on, quieter now. “Half the stands thought you were about to turn it around. I thought you were about to turn it around.” His shoulders shifted, restless, before he let out a sharp breath. “You were damned impressive out there. More than you probably realize.”


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 07:51 AM UTC</sub>

As his face was held between her hands, that's where he found the most assurance. His eyes fluttered closed and he exhaled a breath so heavy he wasn't sure how he managed to hold it for so long without realizing. The words didn't fall on deaf ears, but he was still beyond consoling for the time being - At least to be his normal cocky, cheerful self. He was fragile right now in more ways than one. His voice was still scratchy, above a murmur. "I appreciate you, honestly. You do more for me than you realize." Immediately his head snapped to the side as he heard movement. 

And as if matters couldn't be worse it was entirely obvious he intended to make some motion with his left arm as his body shifted that way before his shoulders slumped and his eyes hollowed more. Redd's own pep talk came, and he lifted his head to fall back with eyes held on the ceiling. "What happened after...I did it..." He asked, remembering finally that he'd used the power that he wasn't *supposed* to in public.


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 11:14 AM UTC</sub>

Redd shifted where he sat, eyes flicking away for a beat before meeting Vornakir’s again. “After? The whole damn school saw it. No hiding that part.” He exhaled through his nose, forcing a crooked grin. “But I told them it was just your draconic sorcery blood getting tangled with… well, with the mess of losing your arm. That the magic just flared ugly because of it.”

He shrugged, trying to keep his tone light. “Not my best story, but I sold it, enough that nobody came running asking questions right then and there.” His voice dipped lower. “Truth is, I don’t know if they all believed me. Some probably did, some are probably whispering already. Velvyn has definitely been sniffing around for sure. But for now? You’ve got breathing room. And I’ll keep it that way as long as I can.”

### &#x270D; Vornakir

He groaned and wiped his hand down the front of his face, pausing to pinch the bridge of his nose as he angled his face forward again. "I don't know what came over me." He drawled out, his voice starting to regain its timbre the longer he was conscious. "I just got so frustrated, and I knew I was on my last leg - I knew if I didn't do something I wouldn't be able to hold a chance. I just had to...really hope it would've won me the battle. After I failed, it took all my energy to use the last spell I stole." He muttered at the end. 

He focused over to the Aasimar and formed a tight line with his lips again, "I'm sorry if...I damaged the scarf. I know she kept flinging a heavy eruption spell around me."


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Aug 24, 2025 &middot; 11:18 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian's tall frame filled the doorway into the recovery room. His clothes were rumpled and his hair was no longer neatly styled. After Vornakir passed out on the field he'd done his best to work with Redd to keep away the nosy group of students who tried to get to Vornakir after the duel. 

He looked to Redd seeming a little ticked off, "I figured you'd snuck off here. I was in the middle of trying to convince Professor Hallowforge to give Vornakir a few hours to recover and I turned around and you had disappeared." He then shivered involuntarily, "Then I leave to come over here and of course who else do I run into other than Diana and Miv of all people..."

He finally notices Vornakir is awake and with only a slight pause of surprise adds, "Good to see you awake already Scales."

Addressing everyone he finishes, "I've got some good news and bad news. Good news, no one seems to care that Scales is missing an arm. Bad news, its because the rumor that he used some kind of *secret* or *illegal* magic in the duel has been far more popular."

### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Aug 26, 2025 &middot; 06:41 AM UTC</sub>

He closed his eyes again, and tried to keep himself centered whilst everyone had conflicting points of view of the situation. Once Zašia finished giving her practiced words, he nodded along and exhaled another heavy breath. "I definitely would rather it be said that my magic is illegal or forbidden or anything *other* than mismanaged or lashing out. I think I can smooth things over with the professors, as Zašia has said several times - People will talk and spread their own version of the truth whether the true version or not is present. The students I could care less about, if anything it'll make me more interesting to some." He said with a loft of his only good shoulder. 

He didn't make eye contact with Caspian, but his lips formed their tight line again and he stared toward his blanketed legs while addressing him. "It appears the training didn't stick in my mind well enough. I failed. I assure you I won't shame your teaching by doing so more than this. I'll just...try harder next time so it doesn't reflect poorly."


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Aug 26, 2025 &middot; 11:47 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian nodded in agreement with Zašia's explanation. "Anyone who saw the display would agree it was too controlled and directed to be a wild surge of magic." He finds an empty seat in the room and falls into it lazily. 

"My concern is the professors, they won't be satisfied with a vague response or simple assurances it won't happen again. Just don't underestimate them Scales, Hallowforge could stop you from future duels." It isn't an admonishment, just a reminder the stakes are high given the circumstances.

When Vornakir spoke though he listened thoughtfully. Before replying he paused to think through his words. "Vornakir..." He begins, dropping the casual nickname to emphasize his next words.

"I understand what you're saying, but it's not necessary." Caspian sat up straight looking directly at the drow and spoke candidly. "I invited you to train with me because you're my friend who needed some help. The only way you could shame my 'teaching' is by giving up and I don't think I have to worry about that."
