> *When the river runs dry,*

The Spark Saga · Vornakir
September 16, 2025
Vornakir

When the river runs dry,
And the curtain is called
How will I know if I can't see the bottom?
Come up for air, and choke on it all
No one else knows that I've got a problem.
What if I can't get up and stand tall?
What if the diamond days are all gone, and
Who will I be when the empire falls?
Wake up alone, and I'll be forgotten.

"Not good enough. Not good enough. You aren't good enough." Those words kept echoing in his mind, searing into the walls of his skull. Just because he presented himself as this prodigal son that excelled at everything and offered the facade of someone who never let anything get to him, it didn't mean his self doubt didn't exist. "I can’t keep failing. I simply can’t. It’s shameful, it’s gods damned pitiful. It hasn’t failed yet, every single time there’s enemies to fight I falter. What good have I been? I got careless and nearly got Zašia killed for it and lost an arm as a result. Gods, I haven’t been the same since then. It’s been downhill ever since…"

”It was spiraling long before that.” the voice in his head chimed in. Gods how he ached to fight it, to prove himself wrong but what evidence did he have? No friends, outside those present in obligation and happenstance. Failing in the dueling league. Barely garnering the attention of one of the only women he's fostered feelings for, though he still wasn't fully convinced she wasn't doing this out of obligation.

Nobody could see past the mask. Almost nobody even knew it was there. He excelled at everything he attempted, that much was true but most unfortunate in this case was how excessively potent he was at his masking. A picture of elegance and regality. Destined for greatness. Picture perfect with almost everything he attempted. But nobody saw how hard he worked at that. It wasn't that life gave him everything. He worked for all of these talents, tirelessly. Did they know the countless hours he spent practicing sewing while his mage hand held a book on cooking in front of him? How many times he burned himself while cooking as he read a book on dancing? How many times he stumbled on his step routines as he danced reading a book on jewel cutting? How many times he was nearly killed for forgetting to lock his doors?

"Fools gold. That's what you are, child. A pitiful excuse for Scaleborn. You know how many people want you dead, you know how they seethe with envy at the mere sight of you." His tutor had scolded in the dead of the night as he sat poised with one knee pivoted into Vornakir's bed and the other standing off the side of the bed, pointed tip of a serrated dagger at his throat as he suddenly jolted the poor sleeping youthful Scaleborn to the world of the living. "You forgot to lock your door again. If I had been anyone else, you'd be dead." He hissed, sending a vicious backhand with the hilted end of the dagger pummeling into his jaw - Almost breaking the bone from the force. A dozen and a dozen more times over the next month, he would forget to lock his doors and the same assault would happen. In the bathing chambers, in his bed chambers, using the restroom. Anywhere he could be assassinated, if he let his guard down he was attacked by his tutor or a hired 'fake assassin'. In one particularly bad case, he'd recall being so frightened he actually nearly killed his assailant with a burst of magic. This made his tutor proud...after he was beaten once again for failing to prevent it. He'd been flogged so many times for this mistake, his body's natural armor grew thicker near his neck to prevent being killed by a dagger as if instinctively making up for his mistake.

Nobody truly understood why he was so paranoid. How many times had he had his wrist nearly cut for being too careless shaking hands? How many times were actual assassins sent to bring his head back before being dispatched once his magic properly blossomed? How many times had he been applauded and praised for hiding the pain it brought him to have to keep everyone he met at mage-arm's length. They know, but they don't understand. How often had he made a friend only to find out they were an assassin? How many times had he found a girl attractive but for her to only want him for his station, or once again being sent for his head? If betrayal were power, he'd be a god. If treachery were subjects, he'd be a king. If trust were friends, he'd be...no different than he is now.

His foot falls echoed through the tunnel leading into the dueling arena, one of them anyway set aside for training. Just because he were suspended it didn't mean he couldn't train. It was too early for Caspian to be here, he celebrated their victory in the inverse layer a little too much to wake up this early he'd assumed. Regardless, arriving alone he surveyed the expanse of the arena. His eyes closed, and opened again - only this time, he envisioned a dozen enemies surrounding him. Everything that nearly killed him over these last few weeks. And the one that did.

An inhale, sharp and sounding of a blade being drawn from its sheathe. Every single action was controlled, from the way he breathed to the way he stood. Did anyone realize that either? He turned, effortless it would seem to those on the outside but in his mind all he could think was his lessons. Feet just far enough apart, shoulder length, that you're sturdy but able to retreat. Stricken with wooden staves when he failed. Spine straight, chin raised and eyes down as if looking down on the world around him - demanding an apology for being born into his world. Shoulders square, nostrils flaring as he took in the final huff of air into his lungs and expelling it through his lips just as controlled as every other movement he made.

His blood pumped, pounding through his body faster and faster as a trail of auric ethereal arcana followed behind his every move; Everything moving twice as fast in his eyes but half the speed for everyone else (Metamagic: Quicken Spell). He twisted on his heel to look behind him, slashing his hand through the air after a draconic word growled past his lips in an incantation. "Flamgra'in!" From each of his fingers a smoldering beam of energy clawed through the air and sent its rays of pure conflagration into the sand one destructive burst at a time (2nd Level Scorching Ray as Bonus Action). In that same motion, he twisted on his heel again and thrust his hand outward - "Vir'ednit!" Pure and potent raw sorcerous energy roiling in his palm and expelling forth in a sudden eruption of sharded ice in every direction. (Cantrip Sorcerous Burst as Action). He could feel the dichotomy of this magic pumping through his veins, the searing heat from the first spell followed by the immediate shift to crystalline blood congealing in his palm for the second.

Every breath came out ragged but he didn't stop there. He twisted his hand through the air and hissed out again, "Tonashss!" as the incantation flowed past his lips, he suddenly felt a surge of bile in his throat, burning as an acidic orb of unfiltered magic surged outward and erupted. Ash and snowflakes, sand and glinting pieces of glass rained from the sky around him as he continued to sling spell after spell out, the more he cast the more unhinged he became. His stance was no longer controlled and proper, his knees bent and his torso forward like an animal cornered and wounded. He wasn't even speaking his incantations anymore, just throwing every spell in his arsenal at the ground - explosion after explosion of color and arcana echoed through the arena.

He couldn't even feel his fingers anymore. At first they burned, then they chilled to the point of hypothermia fears. Then they ached and went numb, they felt broken. Every muscle in his arm screamed in protest, his chest tightened and his throat closed. His lungs felt like they were filled with glass as his mouth went agape and he roared. Much like Zasia before with his spell, he erupted raw, discordant magic past his lips from the depths of his throat into the sand around him. This time it took the form of golden energy congealing with a coiling serpent of electricity striking the ground as if it were sent from the heavens above instead of his chest.

That was it. He was tapped out. Sweat beaded at his brow. His vision was blurry, blackened at the edges. His torso bent somewhat forward, his arm limp at his side - Vornakir was spent. Empty and hollow of magic of any great feat until he rested. But he couldn't stop. His body cried and begged for it, but he couldn't. The last time he felt like this was the duel. Still, he felt the tug into the weave of arcana. He heard the roar of that unseen beast deep in the caverns in his mind's palace. It boiled up, his lungs expanding again as the razor air was sucked into them and felt like it cut him up from the inside out. Every molecule of air in his lungs sparked to life with arcana and as it traveled back through his throat it hit that magical organ that grew at the top of his mouth when this spell was cast, the pinhole igniting the magic with another spark of arcana to erupt the air in the form of Dragon's Breath.

The roar came, and it didn't stop as he angled his face down and sent an absolute pillar of auriferous flames at the ground. It recoiled back and licked up the length of his body - his scales protecting him from the brunt of it but gods did it burn. Not just his skin, not just his clothes. Every cell in his body roared in defiance, and soon pain replaced the blood that pumped through his veins, turning the roar into a scream that still poured ochre magic from his throat. His vision began to turn black, his breath gradually lost and his roaring scream turned into a hoarse raspy sputter. The ground came rushing to meet him as he collapsed, still conscious. But barely. His finger lazily, weakly clawed at the dirt as his eyelids fluttered. Sweat caked his face in the sand beneath him that stuck to him.

"Fools Gold. Pah, I don't know why I bother with you. ~You~ were blessed by the blood of the dragon, yet you lack the wings to soar? How are you going to carry the weight and pride of your name on your shoulders when ~you're~ beneath everyone right now?" He was asked before a sharp kick shattered one of the scales that protected his ribs. "Why don't I just do the others a favor and put you down myself? You're not going to win the trial in this state anyway. Your mother would be better off handing the Crown to one of the others, and let the Vanori name fade into obscurity." A flash of silver broke into his peripheral. This was it. He was going to die. He was born with the scales, he survived the first couple years. And he couldn't even survive the training that followed to ensure he could lead with an iron fist and ruthless pride. All he could do was cry, and scream in abject horror as his life would come to its end. But the sound that rattled the very foundations of the subterranean chambers he was subject to for these training sessions wasn't a scream. It was a roar that made his throat ache so bad it bled for half an hour after the burning ceased. The putrid scent of burning flesh cut through the brilliant resplendent light of flames that blinded him. It took almost ten minutes of coughing for all the ash to leave his throat in clumps of blood. All he could do on all fours, sobbing and hacking up everything that remained in his lungs, was keep himself from crumbling to the ground. Only when he finally stood, legs shaking and vision blackened at the edges did he hear three single echoing claps. And his Mother's voice in the distance. "My darling boy has finally hatched into a beautiful dragon, and learned his first lesson after all this time. A dragon does not bow to anyone."
