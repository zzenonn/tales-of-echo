Vornakir

The morning came brilliantly, the shimmers of worry ceasing from Vornakir after the night prior. He woke early, earlier than the rest of his dorm-mates for a purpose. He stepped into the kitchen and took a heavy breath; He hadn't done anything like this since the night he prepared desserts for Redd for his date. And he sensed a hint of tension between the others, but aside from that his previous sessions in counselling had helped bolster himself and carving away some of the shames he'd faced in the past weeks. He stood prideful there in the kitchen and with a flourish of his hand, a spark of ochre arcana flared and formed itself into an ethereal hand. It moved with a life of its own, by his will as if it were attached to him and he'd never lost his left arm. He reach back to pull his hair back into a tight, distinguished ponytail and cracked his neck to the side before he waved his hand and willed the mage hand to gather what was needed.

It brought back various ingredients and he took what was left and brought it from the cabinets. Extra-virgin olive oil, a pound of sweet sausage, an onion and some ciabatta bread, a jar of tomato sauce with herbs (marinara), some provolone cheese, some Parmesan, fresh basil, five of the largest eggs they had and freshly ground pepper as well as salt. The hand brought forward an apron to drape over him, and he reach back to tie it in place appropriately. A snap of his fingers ignited a magical flame on the stove and he poured the oil in a cast-iron skillet to allow it to begin heating. He sliced down the sausage and tossed in bits at a time, stirring it occasionally until all of the meat was in the pan and fairly browned. While it simmered, he chopped up the onion and scraped the sliced vegetables into the pan until they went golden.

The ciabatta was diced up into small cubes and scraped into the mixture next to allow it to toast, and the sauce followed with a couple of cups of water until it too began to simmer. He pantomimed turning a dial with his fingers, the heat from his magical flame lowering whilst his mage hand dumped in and began to stir the provolone and parmesan cheese as well as about half of the basil, letting everything get to know one another in the pan.

He pursed his lips, canting his head to the side as he watched it cook. The thought occurred to him to try something a little different, and he flipped the back of his spoon around to make some shallow wells in the mixture within the pan. His mage hand fluttered closer and cracked an egg into each of those five wells, then he covered the pan and let the dish cook until the eggs whites began to set and brown whilst the yolk was still runny. After about ten minutes, he sprinkled the rest of the parmesan and basil on top, and ground up some pepper to top it all off with a generous pinch of salt.

At last, he waved off the fire and grasped the skillet with a cloth so he could carry it over to the table - Placing it on a special serving platter so the skillet wouldn't burn the table. He sat out several plates and silverware appropriately, as if this were fine dining and carefully carved out the contents to make perfect portions for all of those who would like to come eat - one for each, but whomever didn't want a portion would mean more for someone else. And then he sat down, taking out his own portion to put on the plate and he sat himself down for breakfast alone.
