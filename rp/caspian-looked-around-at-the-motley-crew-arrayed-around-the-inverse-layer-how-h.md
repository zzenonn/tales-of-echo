### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 08, 2025 &middot; 11:38 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian looked around at the motley crew arrayed around the inverse layer. How had this become his life so quickly? 

Only a few weeks ago he had been bracing for social isolation and never-ending academic struggles. Now he stood about to attempt a magical trial to earn access to powerful draconic magic with an assortment of people who, barring the fact that they lived together, may have never given each other a second glance. That wasn’t to mention the murder and conspiracy that entangled the very institution that they were enrolled in nor the enigmatic being they had found locked “underneath” the school.

He put his hand on one of the large arcing stones that made up the astrolabe-like architecture at the center of the inverse layer as if to brace himself. It helped considering he still felt the tell tale lightheadedness of being buzzed from his several glasses of wine earlier. That seemed to be the root of his wandering thoughts if he was being honest with himself. 

He silently pleaded that Aurum needed plenty more time to prepare for the trial. After a slight groan as he dragged himself away from the comfort of the stone he caught the attention of everyone else waiting for Aurum. 

“I think I may have overindulged just a bit back in the dorms. Does anyone know any ways to sober up fast? I’ve fought drunk before but it isn’t pretty and I would really like to pass this test.” 

He looked to Sol and Vornakir “Any home remedies you know of?” 

Then he turned to Redd and Zašia, “What about you, any tips or tricks?”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Sep 08, 2025 &middot; 11:53 PM UTC</sub>

Redd’s smirk curled the moment Caspian groaned. “First you eat the cafeteria dry, now you can’t hold your wine? I’m starting to think your stomach’s the real monster we ought to be worried about.

"Best you’ll get out here is something salty if you packed it, cold water if you trust whatever Aurum gives you, and . . . anything that gets the blood pumping.” His grin widened, wicked and unrepentant. “Though my usual method’s a bit too scandalous to pull off in public. Unless, you’re into that sort of thing.” He rummages through his bag and hands him some salt crackers.


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 12:26 AM UTC</sub>

Zašia produced a flask of chilled water, holding it out. A little smirk played on her lips at Redd's commentary, holding back a chuckle.

A different thought prompted her to do it anyway. "Chin up, long spine..." As she spoke, Zašia demonstrated the posture herself. At her short stature of only 5' 3", she didn't often look impressive. This, however, did lend her some air of authority. She looked poised, confident.

One hand gestured out in front of her as though she were just casually throwing the thought out into the world. Not quite carelessly, but effortlessly.

"Think *conqueror*, and walk." 

Long strides carried her forward a few steps to show off the notion of being aloof and sure of herself, as conquerors must be. She pivoted neatly on the ball of one foot to turn back to Caspian, her brows raised as though imperiously waiting for him to follow this example she (jokingly) set. Eventually, she had to bite the inside of her lip to keep herself from grinning and spoiling this illusion she's painted of herself. If he blushed, then she succeeded in her efforts to get his blood pumping, more subtly than Redd's methods.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 12:38 AM UTC</sub>

Vornakir’s demeanor was, mostly, different than he normally presented himself to be. Ever since the conversation before leaving, something clearly lingered in his head but he was an expert at masking. It’s something his people prided in. It wasn’t his facial expressions or even his body language that betrayed the shift in personality but the way he spoke. It still had the bite of someone who thought he was better than every other creature that exists, that he saw himself the pinnacle of all things. The bite, it didn’t lack. The venom, it did lack. So when he spoke, it almost echoed a bit more hollow and airy than he intended.

“Mm. Other than an array of magic means to purge the body that I wouldn’t waste on something so paltry, I wouldn’t know. We don’t often get drunk where I’m from— especially not enough to be impaired in any way.” Though the tone lacked his normal inflection, the truth of his upbringing, life and his future was still woven into the fabric of his words. It couldn’t take much thought to connect impairment to weakness, and weakness to being able to be killed easier given his track record.

Notably, his gaze remained averted from everyone present. Not in shame, but his ochre stare settled on the stonework while he tossed his idle words. 

“Perhaps choke on something? A finger, a stick, whatever it may take to induce vomiting.”


### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 01:19 AM UTC</sub>

Sol glanced at Caspian, lips quirking in a half-smile as he tugged the rolled sleeves of his crimson coat higher up his forearms. “My brother used to swear by water, bread, and a walk in the cold. Said the air did half the work of clearing the head. Not sure if that’s an old wives’ tale or not, but…” He tilted his chin toward Zašia, amused. “Seems you’ve got the walking part covered.”

For a moment his golden-silver eyes softened, catching the glow of the street lamp above. “If I’m being honest, Caspisn, I don’t think there’s any remedy that makes this place less dizzying. First week on campus and I’ve heard of a murdered professor, a creepy security system that stares through your soul, and a headmaster who thinks I should keep tabs on all of you.” He huffed a quiet laugh. “Compared to that, a hangover feels almost… normal.”

His smile returned, easier this time, teasing as he nudged Caspian with an elbow. “But if nothing else works, I’ll paint you a sunrise so bright it’ll feel like the sun itself is dragging you out of bed.”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 01:54 AM UTC</sub>

Redd’s smirk brightened into something close to admiration as Zašia demonstrated her imperious stride. “Now that,” he drawled, eyes sweeping her up and down with obvious approval, “is how it’s done. Caspian, take notes, if Zašia's methods don't sober you up, she’ll have walking like you own everything by morning.” He gave her a small bow of his head, a theatrical flourish that managed to feel sincere beneath the playfulness.

When Vornakir offered his grim advice, Redd snorted and pressed his lips together. He fought to keep a laugh locked behind his teeth. For once, he said nothing on choking.

His gaze turned to Sol then, the warmth in it a touch surprising. “Bread, cold air… and painting the sky for a hungover fool? That’s almost charming.” A sly smile tugged at his lips. “If that’s your cure, Sol, I’ll happily volunteer to be your next patient.”

Finally, he leaned back against the stone, folding his arms, eyes flicking back to Caspian. “Between conquerors, grim sages, poets, and me? You’re spoiled for choice, Caspian. Pick one cure before Aurum shows up.”


### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 02:23 AM UTC</sub>

Sol laughed, tossing a glance Redd’s way. “Volunteer patients, huh? Careful, once I get started, you’ll be walking around covered in suns and moons for a week. My art has a habit of sticking.” He let the grin linger before adding with a wink, “Though… a little starlight might suit you.”


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 03:53 AM UTC</sub>

Caspian took each instruction in kind hearing them out before replying. 

When Redd produced some crackers he swiped them without a second thought. He ate them very quickly his aforementioned stomach not caring in what form the food took. 

“For the record Redd, I’m no lush I just believe in appreciating fine wine in ample quantities.” He said as he wiped away a few crumbs.

He gladly drained much of Zašia’s flask of water when it was offered. This time he took the conqueror comment with a little more grace than the dorms and managed to not choke on this beverage. While he turned a bit red it was as much from a good natured laugh at her imperial march as the callback to his all too revealing comment earlier.

“Long spine?! What kinda nickname is that? You can do better Silverspan.” He called out as she rounded back to the group. “I can do that walk!” he insisted afterwards and began his own attempt.

His imitation was terrible, yet comedic in an embarrassing way. 

When Vornakir spoke he turned mid-stride and looked at the drow before he smiled. “Let’s hope it doesn’t come to that for all your sakes Scales. I’m terrible to be around when I’m sick.”

He twisted in place in an attempt to spin on his foot and  nearly lost his balance. He keeled over before recovering last second with his arms outstretched. When he made it back he stopped in front of Sol.

“Alright, water… check! Bread… check! A walk… check! It does seem to be working as I’m feeling a growing sense of dread as I realize how stupid I looked while I tried to do that walk. Think that covers everything except the sunrise right?”

He pauses before adding with sincerity, “You do get used to it just a bit more, at the very least you’ll never be bored here at Spark Oath.”


### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 05:45 AM UTC</sub>

Sol clapped slowly, dragging it out exaggeratedly as his grin broke wide. “That was… breathtaking, Caspian. Truly. If you keep marching like that, half the academy will drop to their knees…out of sheer disbelief.” His laughter warmly spilled, catching in the air like sunlight breaking through clouds.

When Caspian’s words grew more sincere, so did Sol’s expression. His eyes lingered on the stones beneath their feet. “Never bored, huh? I can live with that. I worried I’d come here and vanish in the shuffle, but maybe a little chaos is exactly what I needed.” There was a softness there, quick and unguarded, before it was replaced with a crooked grin.

He gave Caspian a light bump with his shoulder, his voice carrying that teasing warmth he wrapped around everyone. “Your sunrise is still pending. After that performance, you’ve more than earned it, Twinkletoes.”


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 02:55 PM UTC</sub>

Zašia sighed with an exaggerated roll of her eyes. "It wasn't a nickname, it was an *instruction.*" Come to think of it, had anyone heard her use a nickname for anyone in their cadre yet? Other than the ones they introduced themselves by. She'd not jumped on board calling Vornakir *Scales* as the others had.

"Long spine, you know? It's not quite 'back straight,' because while that is something, thinking about standing *taller* than that is what really lends it the air of authority." She gestured to Vornakir, whos advice had caused her to wrinkle her nose, but pass over without comment. "Watch him when he's dealing with people outside our circle. Especially the professors." She's noticed.

Likewise, she'd noticed Redd's assessment and his praise elicited a little smile from her. "Thank you," she murmured demurely. For all that she gave an impressive demonstration, none of them had seen her carry herself that way. Maybe during the first few days of classes, but her spirit seemed to have been crushed pretty easily. Witnessing the death of the professor had rattled her, certainly, but it had to go deeper than that.

She sighed again, circling back to the previous comment, "And *all* men are horrid while they're sick. I'm telling you all right now, I refuse to be your nursemaid when a cold inevitably rolls through campus."

Although, with what most of them knew about Zašia by then? She almost certainly would drop everything to take care of any one of them. When Vornakir was recovery, the others had to force her to rest and let someone else have a shift. Her devotion to the task hadn't simply been because it was *him*, or the fact that he'd saved her life.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 05:57 PM UTC</sub>

Vornakir crinkled his nose a little, and as she gave an indication toward him he merely offered a curt nod followed by a sigh. With a wave of his hand, he spoke airily outward toward the group vaguely again. "She's right. I don't quite know what we call it, Long Spine makes sense but I suppose a good term for it would be 'posturing'. In order to look down on someone, *they have to be beneath you.* The trend likely began because of a short man with the complex and authority of a larger man so he needed to make himself seem taller, in truth. But it holds true to today - And is now a lesson taught to many in the world of nobility. The amount of times I've been struck because my posture laxed should have left scars had it not been for the scales." That last part was muttered mostly to himself. 

He canted his head to the side this way, and that thoughtfully. "If you wish to rid yourself of the alcohol, I can induce vomiting with a cantrip and then clean you - and the subsequent mess - up without fault or strain." He offered before taking a leisurely stroll around the group while busying his eyes anywhere else. 

"I would delight in seeing your magic, sometime Sol. I haven't been able to mingle with another painter in some time, and I'm sure it's quite a show to see magic take breath in paint." His off hand compliment and offer would also serve to reveal to his companions who hadn't known until now, *apparently Vornakir could paint*. Of course he could; although he obviously showed traits all along that he'd excelled at many things, he never made it long without bragging about a talent. So that this one wasn't one he shared before was odd.


### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 06:39 PM UTC</sub>

Sol’s wings gave an eager flutter, lifting him a few inches off the ground before he even realized it. Sol’s eyes lit up, brows climbing as he leaned toward Vornakir with an wide grin. “You paint? And here I thought you’d already boasted about every talent under the sun. Hells, Vornakir, you’re slipping.” His tone was teasing but warm, the kind reserved for friends.

Then the grin softened into something more earnest, his voice picking up with a spark of excitement. “But seriously, I’d love to see it. It’s not every day I get to experience art with someone who understands the craft. You’ll have to show me what you’ve been hiding before I start thinking you’re ashamed of it.” He chuckled, giving him an easy smile. “Who knows, maybe we’ll even learn a few tricks from each other.”


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 08:04 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian took a short bow at the “applause” from Sol. He felt good and it wasn’t just the wine causing it. It had been a long time since he had a moment to goof off with friends and forget their troubles for a minute.

“Twinkletoes?” Caspian considered the nickname aloud before he shrugged. It was better than the names a few of his stricter weapons instructors had given him. “Works for me, I’m still figuring out your nickname though, it’ll come to me.”

As Zašia called out his misunderstanding he cocked his head. “Oh that does make more sense. Don’t worry Zašia when I get sick I’ll make it the whole schools misfortune not just yours.” He joked .

He listened to her and Vornakir’s explanation on posture with genuine interest.

“Oh I see what you mean. Let me give it a shot for real this time…” 

Caspian straightened from his typical relaxed manner. He took this task a bit more seriously and tried to imitate the confidence and pride Vornakir presented. Old habits kicked in and some aspects of a battle stance stood out in his recreation. 

Not quite a proud noble but closer to a noble warrior. For any who had seen his spell duels they would recognize it as how he held himself in the moments before one of his matches began.

He held it for a few moments before slackening back to his normal self. 

“I did skip most of my etiquette lessons growing up, maybe I did miss out on some helpful tips. Though I didn’t realize they slapped you around in those too.” He admitted.

“Please don’t make me lose my lunch Scales. But I appreciate the offer, thankfully the food and drink has hit the spot.” He added quickly after.


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 09, 2025 &middot; 10:04 PM UTC</sub>

Zašia fell silent and her gaze unfocused, shifting away from the group as though the way the lights played off the stone might have captured her interest. Her eyes seemed to roam the expanse in front of her, but the expression on her face was too blank to denote interest in anything at all.

None of the group's conversation or antics seemed to register her at all. With her right hand, she reached across to take her bracelet delicately between her thumb and middle finger, turning it in a slow orbit around her wrist. She felt each setting, the facets of the stones, the slither of the gold links across her skin. Those things kept her tethered to her body, but not to the moment.


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 12, 2025 &middot; 02:39 AM UTC</sub>

Caspian paused and noticed his friend’s silence. Zašia had just been having a good time a few minutes ago, so what had changed? 

He sidled up to her, trying not to make a big deal out of it. In classic Caspian fashion, he relied on jokes to defuse his awkwardness. He pointed at one of the glyphs carved into the stones around the inverse layer.

“That one kinda looks like a duck. What do you think?” He tried to say casually, but it came off far too forced.

He rolled his eyes at himself and shook his head before restarting. 

“Sorry, bad habit. What I meant to ask was if you’re alright? Seems like something’s bothering you,” he said quietly.


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 12, 2025 &middot; 02:21 PM UTC</sub>

She missed his drawing closer, but lifted her head quickly when he spoke. "Huh?"  Zašia blinked. "Oh." She studied the rune for a moment before he revealed his intent wasn't simply to be (awkwardly) goofy.

The perplexity on her face softened and he could see the sullen girl she'd been poorly hiding under that indifferent, detached façade. "I'm fine." Her voice quieted in an attempt to keep her response just for him.

Goddess, she envied the way Vornakir could just speak into someone's mind. "Just..." She smiled disingenuously, trying to mask from the others how she was really feeling as she answered Caspian. "You know, feeling the sting of being painfully unimpressive."


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 13, 2025 &middot; 02:21 AM UTC</sub>

A frown appeared on Caspian's face. Zašia could really be her own worst enemy sometimes. He knew that feeling though, the feeling of falling short of your own expectations. Out of respect he kept his tone hushed to match hers.

"We'll have to agree to disagree on that statement. Unimpressive people don't kill beholders."

From prior experience he knew she would probably try to contradict him so he didn't give her the chance. He continued speaking quietly even though it might become obvious to the others that they were whispering to each other at this point.

"Everyone here would agree with me by the way. Especially that one" He points out Vornakir, poised as ever, speaking with Sol. 

"He doesn't bother with unimpressive."

To cement the point he added, "And I don't lie to my friends, and frankly your the best one I've got here 'Z'."  

He gave her a playful nudge and nodded his head towards the others. "Let's have a little more fun before whatever Aurum has in store, sound good?"


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 13, 2025 &middot; 03:03 AM UTC</sub>

She hadn't expected him to bring up the Beholder. The accomplishment there had been, she felt, eclipsed by the failure to dispatch it sooner and the consequences that had for *that one.* She didn't argue the point, however. It *had* been a feat to manage to put the thing down.

Zašia rewarded his efforts with a little smile. "Thanks..." Her gaze trailed to Vornakir. The argument made sense. (*He is* fascinated *with you.*) Still, self-doubt continued to linger, but she shoved it aside to be dealt with approximately never.

With a breath that saw her standing a little straighter, she looked up at Caspian and nudged him right back. "Alright, *C*." A little huff of laughter went along with a shake of her head. They were going to have to get more creative than *that* on the nickname front. Give it time.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 13, 2025 &middot; 05:27 AM UTC</sub>

Vornakir lifted a brow over in their direction shortly after it had become fairly obvious the two were in a private conversation. He only observed for a few moments before he caught sight of the smile, of the small laugh, of the body language she presented. It read to him like she was at least okay in the moment, and being okay was better than anything negative. He shifted his ever lustrous golden stare onto Sol again.

“Mm? Worry not, you’ll come to learn there are very scarce few things I feel shame about, and if I do it’s most often highly temporary. I wouldn’t mind showing you sometime. To be honest, I’m quite shocked nobody’s asked me until now.” He pursed his lips but kept his ‘long spine’ posture, his eyes rolling upward a little to observe skyward in case Aurum was hiding somewhere about to drop something on him. 

“Granted I don’t often talk about it. Many people lump in my passion with other things I excel at and just assume I enjoy it because I’m good at it. I’ve taken to studying a few other culture’s variations on brushstrokes as of late. Many artists don’t realize the difference it makes when you use varied strokes in your piece, how some forms could make things feel far more Alive or warm or contemporary.”
