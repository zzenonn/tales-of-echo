It was never a small task or an easy feat for Vornakir to admit when he was wron...

The Spark Saga · Vornakir Caspian Redd
August 03, 2025 – August 11, 2025
Vornakir

It was never a small task or an easy feat for Vornakir to admit when he was wrong, or when he made a mistake. It was near impossible for him for him to conceive he’s not completely and entirely on the right path always.

Today was no different. At least, not in a conventional way. He didn’t think this was a mistake, he didn’t think this was something that painted him in the wrong. He fully intended to die, last night. He fully intended to sacrifice his life for someone else, to set aside his pride for once and just be a decent person. It would have been a mistake to let Zašia take the attack. It would have been wrong to not intervene with the plans the gods had and prove to her that he meant what he said. That his pride may be important to him but it didn’t always come first.

So why then was the taste of Death’s Sweet Tea — the finest wine he’d let wet his tongue and a late night kiss that never left his mind — he had experienced that previously washed down the taste of his own blood and disintegrated ash now replaced by shame and sourness? Why did guilt flood him? For but a fleeting moment in the silence that hung in his room that afternoon, it was because deep down he felt that he may become a burden. Sure he could still very efficiently cast his spells, they didn’t require obtuse somatic components or weaving. Sure, in a duel it wouldn’t drag him down - in fact it was statistically less surface area to take an attack and a thinner silhouette.

But a man who feels shame at his core for the magic he’s so very often praised for, that he’s called a prodigy for, that he swore couldn’t possibly define him but only bolstered what was already there would become…a life line. Anything that required two hands to do for the rest of his life, he’d have to rely on a conjured mage hand. Sometimes he’d need help sitting up in bed if he was especially sore where normally he could use his own hands. Some foods would be more difficult to eat. Getting dressed. The mundane things, that’s where his shame and guilt would lie. And gods, the pity that people would have on him. The way they’d look down at him and consider him lesser…

More than ever, somehow he knew he needed to prove himself. More than he ever needed to before, he needed to show everyone that he is the Blood of the Dragon. And a dragon missing a leg is still a dragon.

He cast his golden eyes to the side of the room where a full length body mirror was - he could barely see his new form. More scales than before, covering the left side of his body where he had been torn asunder. No arm. His torso bare and this time his hair was a mess down to his shoulders, not even put in its normal lazy bun when he was meant to be casual. Just loose rivers of black and gold hanging to his shoulders and down his back.

“Gods I gotta get out of this room.” It’s only been less than twenty-four hours. He rolled out of bed, and immediately walked toward the door — still dressed in his pants from the night before and his boots, his top was singed away. Hopefully he could slip out before anyone noticed to nag him about taking care of himself. Maybe a duel would make him feel better…

Of course that hope was almost definitely dashed, when he stepped out of the room and was immediately met with —
