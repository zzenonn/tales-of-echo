The Spark Saga · Auri Caspian
January 23, 2026 – January 27, 2026
Auri

The kitchen had settled into its post-dinner quiet, the air still faintly smelling like spices. Auri occupied the small table like she’d been there for ours. One leg was tucked beneath her, the other hooked around the chair rung, tail draped over the side and occasionally flicking when she got particularly annoyed at her own notes.

In front of her sat an open textbook. The pages turned themselves with polite, almost apologetic flips, pausing whenever Auri muttered something like, “No, go back…—there.”

Beside it, a ceramic mug of instant coffee stirred itself in slow, lazy circles. Every few rotations, Auri would lift it and take a sip, her eyes never leaving the pages.

An unseen servant worked tirelessly in the background, washing dishes, stacking plates, and wiping down counters. “Thank you, you’re doing great,” Auri added without looking up before resuming her furious scribbling.

Her notebook was already half-filled with tight, precise handwriting. She cross-referenced three sources at once, eyes flicking between book, notes, and loose pages covered in calculations.
She leaned back for half a second, stretching, tail curling around the chair leg as if to anchor herself, then immediately leaned forward again, pulled back into her work like gravity had claimed her.

Jan 23, 2026 · 05:33 PM UTC
Caspian

Caspian exited his room, the door closed behind him with a soft click. He entered the common area to find Auri. The very person he’d been looking for. The Archmage’s advice had him thinking the last few days and he may have found the solution.

He sat down across from Auri and gently tapped her book to get her attention. He smiled and waved when her gaze met his.

“Sorry to disturb but can I interrupt. It has to do with the archmage’s advice.”

He sighed and leaned into his chair trying to get comfortable. A lot was riding on this conversation.

“Auri, your mentor. The… uh undead wizard. Given his undeath, it’s probably fair to assume he’s a necromancer right? Or at least the closest to one we might find.”

Caspian knew Auri was protective of her tower and adopted family. Now it was a matter of navigating Auri helping him and mitigating the risk of contacting a wizard was increasingly concerned was a lich with unknown motivations.

“The archmage said a necromancer could help me understand the terror elemental. I wanted to get your opinion on reaching out to Artorius, that was his name right?”

Jan 23, 2026 · 05:58 PM UTC
Auri

Auri’s attention snapped fully to Caspian the moment by the time he said archmage. The book in front of her froze mid-page, hovering in the air. Her coffee, already a crime against good taste, gave one lazy swirl before going still. Even the unseen servant paused, a plate suspended in midair as she listened.

“Yes,” she confirmed. “That’s his name.”

She leaned back, fingers wrapping around her mug. “And…you’re not wrong,” she continued, choosing her words with care. “Whatever happened to the tower, and whatever made him and the others what they are…it was necromantic in nature. That much is obvious.” Her gaze drifted briefly to the side. “I don’t know the specifics. I tried asking, but it was always a sore subject he preferred to avoid.” Her mouth tightened just a little.

Her tail curled around her legs as she thought, then slowly loosened. “That said,” she went on, looking back at Caspian, “I know Artorius won’t hurt us, and unlike some other necromancers we’ve had the misfortune of meeting…” A faint edge entered her tone, “his magic never felt cruel.” She hesitated, then added more softly, “I think there’s a chance he’d be willing to share what he knows to help someone I care about, so it’s worth trying. We don’t have time to travel to the tower, but I could research spells or alternate methods of contact.”

After a brief pause, she added, “What do you think?”

Jan 23, 2026 · 07:54 PM UTC
Caspian

When she asked him what he thought he grimaced. As far as the terror elemental was concerned everything was difficult to figure out. It was becoming more and more clear that this was all mad science. Seeking a potential outcome more than any real understanding.

“What I think is that this thing is bad news. My concern is that while Artorius seems to have your best interests at heart, that doesn’t mean he’ll treat me the same way.”

He winced, it was a difficult admission but given his experiences lately it paid to be skeptical. This was his life on the line and he had to be sure that not only would they look out for him but also not give them something dangerous by showing them the terror elemental. The wrong person would only try to replicate what they found.

“I don’t mean to be harsh I just need to be certain. But I guess he’s the only lead we have so I don’t have much of a choice.”

To Auri he would look determined but concerned. Whatever answers Artorius might provide would certainly lead to further dilemmas.

Jan 27, 2026 · 03:32 PM UTC
Auri

“That’s fair,” Auri said quietly, nodding once in understanding. “You’re right to be cautious when you’ve never met him.” Her fingers tightened slightly around her mug, then relaxed. “But the archmage told you to understand what inhabits you, and this…” she gestured vaguely toward Caspian’s chest, “isn’t something we can afford to stay ignorant about.”

She leaned forward then, forearms resting on the table, meeting his gaze directly. “So here’s where I stand,” Auri said. “I’ll research a way to contact my mentor. I’ll share only what you are comfortable sharing, and if at any point you want me to stop, I will.” Her tail flicked once, sharp and decisive. “If you want answers, this is the path forward. I won’t force you into it, but that’s what I think.”
