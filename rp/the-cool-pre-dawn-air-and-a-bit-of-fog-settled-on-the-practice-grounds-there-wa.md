### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Aug 11, 2025 &middot; 11:42 PM UTC</sub>

The cool pre-dawn air and a bit of fog settled on the practice grounds. There was little sound, only birds chirping and the occasional footsteps of students dragging themselves to their dorms to get some sleep for whatever time remained before starting the cycle of classes and studying over again. 

In this early hour Caspian strode slowly onto the most secluded of the practice areas and began to set up. He was prepared for a day of training, a bag of equipment slung over one shoulder and wearing clothes meant to be moved in. He set up a few racks and placed his weapons on them neatly, a battle axe, a long sword, a longbow, and a new addition. A well crafted glaive he had purchased from a weapon smith the day before at the market.

He wasn’t tired waking this early, this was a normal ritual for him. Spending years learning from a sword master with military experience would do that to someone. He began stretching before running a few laps around the sandy arena.  His teacher had always emphasized the importance of preparation. The other repeated lessons all echoed in Caspian’s head as he ran.

Finally he finished warming up and grabbed the new glaive from the rack. He began to drill, slowly at first before speeding up. The motions were precise and fluid at the same time. The glaive spun and twirled, cutting through empty air as Caspian’s footwork marked the sand that covered the ground where he moved. Soon the distinction between weapon and master seemed to disappear as the drills repeated and Caspian began to move without active thought.

Some people practiced meditation but Caspian did this. For this short time each day reality fell away and there was just the rhythmic cadence of his drills. Eventually he noticed the presence of someone other than himself. He stopped his motions and snapped the glaive into a relaxed stance. He turned to face whoever had arrived…
