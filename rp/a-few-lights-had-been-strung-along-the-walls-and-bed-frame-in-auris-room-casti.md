The door opened with a soft creak as Sol slipped inside. He looked comfortable, not in a careless, thrown-on kind of way, but in his distinct, curated-with-ease way. He was wearing a black sweatshirt that was cropped just above his belly button; teal letters spelling *CVS Harmacy* were embroidered on the front, catching the light with a soft sheen, and a hand-stitched golden dragon curled across the fabric, as if it might come alive. Frayed denim pants hung loose on his legs, and socks whispered against the floor as he stepped in.

Behind him, suspended in the air by threads of gold magic, a tray floated steadily, a pot of steaming soup resting at its center. The scent of leeks and potatoes wafted from the pot, filling the room. Beside it were two bowls and a small loaf of warm sourdough bread.

He gave Auri an easy smile when his eyes landed on her. “Hi, *Prof*,” he greeted softly. “I brought food in case the snacks weren’t enough. I hope you like potato soup.” 

The tray drifted forward with a gentle flick of his hand, lowering itself carefully in front of her.

“I’m not as great a cook as Vornakir,” he added with a small laugh, “but I tried my best! Plus, Adrien helped too…after I almost sliced my hand open trying to cut up those damn potatoes.” A quiet, breathy laugh followed.

He settled beside her as he reached for the velvet hair tie around his wrist, gathering his dark curls up into a loose bun. A few strands slipped free at his temples, catching the light like fine threads of sunlight.

He glanced around at what she’d set up: the cushions, the blankets, the tea, the lights, and his expression softened.

“You did all this?” he murmured, gaze flicking back to her, a small smile pulling at his lips. “It’s really pretty.” A beat of quiet passed. “It feels nice in here.”
