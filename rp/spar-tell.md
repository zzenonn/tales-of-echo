### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Oct 28, 2025 &middot; 02:21 AM UTC</sub>

The practice fields were wrapped in morning mist, the kind that made you feel half-asleep. Pale sunlight clung stubbornly to the horizon but hadn’t quite committed to shining yet, just like Sol.

He trudged across the dew-soaked grass, jacket hood up and a cup of tea in his hands. Steam curled above the cup, hitting his face like it was mocking him for being awake this early.

“Gods help me,” he muttered under his breath. Sol found great irony in the fact that the name his parents had given him literally meant *Sun King*, but he despised anything to do with the morning.

As he approached the dueling grounds, he heard faint metallic echoes and the rhythmic *thud* of training dummies being struck. He had finally found Caspian; of course, he had been here all along. As Sol got closer, he saw Caspian’s sword gleaming in the early morning light as he moved with an unshakable precision.

Sol slowed, watching for a moment before sighing dramatically. “You know, Cas, some people start their mornings with coffee. Why must you start yours with violence?” He yawned, the sound half-silenced behind his hand. “Do you ever sleep, or are you just… constantly preparing for a fight?”

He took a long sip from his cup before continuing, tone half-mocking, half-genuine. “Anyway, I come bearing a noble request. I need to practice for spell duels. Professor Veilbright said casting illusionary monsters outside Diana’s room would be a waste of my talents and a *‘a hazard to public safety.’*” He added air quotes around the phrase, eyes squinting at the memory.

He gestured lazily toward the field. “So. Spar with me? Please? I need the practice.”
