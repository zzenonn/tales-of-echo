### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Feb 17, 2026 &middot; 04:29 AM UTC</sub>

The late afternoon sun spilled gold across the quad of Spark-Oath, catching in drifting motes of light.
Sol walked beneath the arching trees, melodically humming a catchy tune, a slight skip in his steps.

In front of him, a notebook hovered open, pages flipping as Sol swiped his hand. A golden pen wrapped in shimmering gold light scribbled neatly within the lines as Sol listed things off.

“Deck of cards, check.” He flicked one of his fingers, and a small, glowing tick mark appeared. “Drinks, entrées, and snacks, check.”

He turned slightly, peering over his shoulder to look at a small velvet bag cloaked in golden magic floating obediently behind him. Inside the bag were prizes galore: spell scrolls, potions, and a small orb Sol had enchanted to play dramatic applause.

“And finally, prizes.” Sol rubbed his chin, eyeing the bag. “Hmm, these don’t seem sufficient; we need something *worthy* of a game night hosted by the Sparkian Protectors.”

A soft *ding* sounded in his mind as inspiration struck. “I’ve got it!”

With a snap of his fingers, one of the blank pages in his notebook tore free and was immediately swallowed by prismatic light. The colors folded and condensed, refracting like a living kaleidoscope, until the glow faded to reveal a rainbow-hued ticket hovering in the air; *‘Sol Outfit Voucher’*, was inscribed in elegant script on both sides. He flicked his wrist, and the ticket slipped neatly into the velvet bag with the rest of the prizes.

Sol laughed to himself, clearly pleased. “Awesome prizes everyone will love? Double check!” The bag gave a tiny shimmer, as if it were agreeing with him.

“All right,” he murmured, tapping the notebook. “All that’s left is the guest list.”

He slowed as he looked over to Caspian, the notebook drifting aside to hover near Sol’s shoulder.

“So,” Sol began brightly, matching Caspian’s step cadence, “Everyone in the dorm is *obviously* attending.” He made a decisive slicing gesture in the air with his index finger, and five glowing stars blinked into existence, each labeled with a name.

“Adrien said he’ll join after he finishes his literature paper; apparently, there’s symbolism to a pomegranate that far exceeds what my mind is capable of understanding,” he added with a confused shrug.

He tilted his head, thinking. “Should we invite Zurr’ka?” A soft, thoughtful *hhhhmmmmm* escaped him as he twirled one finger, causing the floating star with Zurr’ka’s name on it to spin lazily. “Maybe not,” he decided after a beat. “I don’t know, he seems very peeved at Redd and Auri right now, and I refuse to let my meticulously curated good vibes be trampled by a man who can’t overcome toxic masculinity.” With a snap of his fingers, the Zurr’ka star burst into a spray of golden glitter.

“Kael can come,” he added lightly, “and if Vornakir wants, so can Miv. I feel like she would absolutely demolish us at strategy games and then be annoyingly humble about it.”

He paused, then his expression darkened just a fraction. “And that bitch Diana is not allowed within ten square miles of this party,” he said irritably, the notebook snapping shut in emphasis.

Then the irritation melted, replaced by a teasing expression. The gold stars around Sol flickered pink for a brief moment; he was clearly thinking something mischievous. Sol turned toward Caspian and started walking backward so he could fully face him.

“And,” he added, lowering his voice into a playful rhythm, “*you can even bring Briar*,” he said in a singsong.

He bumped his shoulder lightly against Caspian’s, and a tiny illusory rose bloomed between them before popping into glittering hearts.

“What do you think?” Sol smiled.

### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Mar 02, 2026 &middot; 04:45 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian was enjoying the fresh air good weather and had gotten lost in it for a moment as Sol was recounting planning details. Content to let the aasimar make the big decisions and happy to serve as a sounding board. He quickly paid attention as Briar’s name came up. 

Trying to backfill using context he sheepishly seemed like he’d missed a few key details. 

“Of course! I’d be happy to have her come. And the same goes for everyone else you said, invite them all! The more the merrier.”

Then he admitted.

“Sorry I only caught bits and pieces. You shouldn’t have suggested we do this outside on such a nice day it’s so distracting. Anyways that’s a mighty good prize, a free outfit is a lot of work though. You’ve said you’re pretty tired lately don’t stretch yourself too thin. That’s why we planned this in the first place, remember?”

Caspian gave a look that could only be described as brotherly. It was concerned but also unsurprised at Sol’s relaxation attempt turning into more work. 

“Just don’t want to see you learn what your limits are by going past them.”
