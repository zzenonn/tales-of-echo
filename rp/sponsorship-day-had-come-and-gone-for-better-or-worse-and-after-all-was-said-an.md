Sponsorship day had come and gone for better or worse, and after all was said and done everyone was now left with the consequences or benefits of that day - Whatever it may have been for each individual. Earlier in the day, after everyone had finished their showings and retired to the dorm for the evening Vornakir had pulled Zašia to the side. "I need a night to myself, after everything I feel like it's best for me to...think the day over, analyze it from a perspective in this frame of mind. I'll still come find you in the morning and we can have breakfast together." He promised, so she wouldn't assume anything was amiss. 

Caspian, Vornakir and Sol had their training just a few short days before Sponsorship Day - And Sol and Vornakir subsequently had their spat in that time frame as well. But that wasn't important. Something Caspian had said really settled in with Vornakir, it really drove a nail in to home. And since then, he'd been finding a way to make it up to him. Tonight was the night he could finally say it was ready. 

He approached his ally's door at an hour he was familiar with Caspian being awake during, and with his mage hand knuckle a gentle tap came thrice in his practiced pattern. *Tap, Ta-tap*

If Caspian were to answer the door, there he'd see Vornakir's mage hand fading into a shower of dull golden sparkles and his real one toting a bag that was rather large and square shaped. Last time he'd seen one of those, it was a painting. 

"Could I come in, if it's not too much a bother? I wanted to...talk to you, in private. And I've brought a gift - I realized I never properly thanked you for the help you gave me when I lost my arm, and the continued support you've offered me." He looked a little worn ragged - Everyone had a taxing day, some more than others and some for completely different reasons. Perhaps it finally settled in on him that he struck his sister square in the chest with his most powerful spell, and it wore his emotions a bit thin.
