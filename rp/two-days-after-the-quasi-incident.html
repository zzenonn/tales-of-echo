~Two Days After the Quasi Incident~

The overcast skies did not break after the the Quasi Incident. The melancholy of all the students and faculty alike felt mirrored over their heads as the clouds made everything feel cast in shades of grey and brown. Interrogations and interviews had begun that morning in full force, already rumors were running rampant across the campus regarding what occurred. Even with classes cancelled and the High Courts swarming the ruins of the Quasi, students seemed to dart from building to building like bogeymen roamed the campus waiting to snatch up the unfortunate.

Caspian had been spending much of his time in solitude. He hadn't been wearing his uniform the last two days and instead wore a more casual tunic and pants. Around his neck was now a cord from which Aurum's key hung, he hadn't removed it even while he slept the last two nights. This morning he had a pair of riding boots on and had a pair of saddlebags slung over one shoulder. A few bottles of wine poked out of one of them. He gathered the last of his belongings before exiting his room. He started down the hall as if to leave before pausing at the end of the hall.

He backtracked, a few steps and knocked on the door of Vornakir Vanori. It was just an impulsive choice, he'd originally intended to go and get piss drunk by himself, but figured that he wouldn't mind some company. Especially that of someone who he knew probably wouldn't try to dissuade him from his binge drinking plans. He'd appreciated that of his friends Vornakir wasn't inclined to judge Caspian for choices the others would typically deem bad ones. For example, he'd barely been phased by the Diana situation.

As he waited for the drow to answer his door he did consider if the drow even had experience horseback riding. Horses didn't really thrive underground he assumed, and everyone had mentioned the chariot his cousin arrived in was drawn by giant spiders. Did the residents of the barricade ride spiders like above ground kingdoms rode horses? He shrugged to himself and took a swig of a small flask with some stronger liquor in it. He wasn't kidding around about getting drunk today.

Caspian knocked a second time, a little impatient and spoke,

"Hey Scales, apparently the stables are still open. I'm going riding wanna join?"

He paused before adding,

"I also have a ton of booze and your welcome to have some if you tag along."

Vornakir

Habit had Vornakir awake far earlier these days than he was accustomed to back home. He was already wide awake, half dressed and lounging on his bed with a book held aloft by his mage hand. The spine title was written in elegant elvish script, and the pages were littered with the same writing.

He muttered out loud to himself as he read along, “Seriously, you expect me to believe that’s how you crafted the arcana weave and somatics for that spell variation? Gods above and below, Soravaen you’re a damned fool…” as if he were speaking to the author himself.

The gentle thuds of boots outside in the hall immediately caught his attention, always acutely aware of his surroundings. His ears flicked twice at the foreign sound and his ochre eyes snapped to the door. Book laden mage hand shifted to the side to let him have a clear shot at the door in case something were to happen out of nowhere.

It was Caspian’s voice that made him relax considerably. He cleared his throat, pausing long enough to carefully figure out exactly what he wanted to do. Stables, to Caspian, likely meant horses not any of the Barricade Spiders that were with the entourage that remained after his cousin left.

“Allow me a moment to dress, I would love to get out for a bit.” He called back out to his friend. “I don’t imagine it’s too much different from spiders, no?” He asked half in jest. But the question did linger in his mind. He figured he could handle it, spiders were likely worse than horses.

Casual wear came to mind easier than his well noble dress. A simple long sleeve shirt and an opulent vest with intricate scrawling over it - the shirt a sleek black with the vest gleaming its delightful silver. This was as casual as he actually had besides dorm or sleep clothes. Simple enough.

He arrived at the door, opening it to slip out into the hallway and locking all of his various locks both magical and not. Each click firmly latched in place, he turned to nod at Caspian firmly.

“What are we drinking today, my friend? More wine or dare I ask if you’ve got something a bit stronger this morning?” His smile was plastered over the mask but Caspian would definitely know Vornakir needed a good drink and distraction as much as he did.

Well, maybe not as much. But a lot.

A smirk crossed Caspian’s face as he saw Vornakir’s reaction. The moonshine was an acquired taste, an old soldier’s drink he’d learned from the old winemaker who’d lived a short walk from the Veilbright’s countryside cottage.

“It’s certainly an experience, some people do flavor it with mint and other herbs but this is more traditional.”

As Vornakir gave his lesson on spider riding technique he made sure they passed the flask back and forth. He listened intently at first but with each scientific term a little enthusiasm waned. Learning the specifics had dampened a bit of the excitement the concept of riding a spider had originally entertained. He nodded and made a mental note that sometimes things were more exciting because he didn’t know the answers.

Caspian wasn’t a natural with horses but he understood the basics. So when he saw the pause of what the next step was from Vornakir he jumped in to help.

“So first you typically approach from the side or shoulder, never from behind or in front, those legs will crack your skull like an egg if you spook them. Reach out a had so he can have a sniff and learn your scent. Then if he’s not aggressive give him a pat and you can start to saddle him.”

Each step was demonstrated with Freya as he explained. She was patient, experienced with riders. Caspian appreciated that in a steed, going as fast as possible was great but a reliable horse would always carry you farther.

Once the saddle was in place on the stallion Caspian gave Vornakir a carrot to feed the horse.

“It’s always good bonding to make sure they have a snack from you. Horses like children often accept bribes for good behavior. And remember when you pull yourself up make sure to use your momentum to throw a leg over his back. Don’t worry if you can’t get it at first Scales it’s a tough skill.”

Caspian demonstrated stepping into the stirrup and hoisting himself onto his horses back. Once in the saddle he adjusted how he sat and checked his straps twice.

Once mounted he tapped his heels to get her to walk forward toward the barn exit before he warmed her up in the field outside.

“You might want to check the name on his stall, they’re smart enough to recognize their names when called. Let me know if you need some practice time before we hit the road. Generally squeeze with your legs to speed up and lean backs a bit and ease up to slow down.”

He had Freya exit the barn and worked on getting her up to pace for a long ride. He wanted to put her through her paces today but didn’t want her to get injured in the process.
