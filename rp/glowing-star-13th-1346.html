Glowing Star 13th, 1346

Dear Diary,

I have a problem; a boy problem. I’ve only been at school for a few days, and he’s already captured my thoughts in a silk net.

I first saw him in illusion class, and I haven’t been able to stop thinking about him since. I have days filled with lectures, and nights erupting with homework, and still somehow managed to retain nothing except the fact that he exists.

He has dark hair I’d love to run my hands through, he’s tall, and has the most dreamy eyes you could swim in forever. He’s handsome, but not in a loud or flashy way; it's like the calm after a storm, like he knows exactly who he is and doesn’t feel the need to announce it to the world.

What really captured my attention was his energy. I don’t know how to explain it yet, but his aura doesn’t flare or shimmer the way most people’s do. I didn’t see it spike with emotion or shift colors at the edges. It was just a soft, muted blue. If I had to describe it, it looked like the depths of the ocean surrounded him.

Being near him felt grounding. I could feel my thoughts slowing down rather than racing ahead. I liked how that felt—a lot.

I overheard him and Kael talking during class, something about illusion anchors. I'm not entirely sure if that was actually the subject of conversation. I was admittedly a little bit distracted, but that’s when I caught his name.

Adrien.

Gods, I really want to talk to him, but courage decides it’s a bad time to show up whenever I’m around him; so instead, all I’ve managed so far are a few shy smiles when we pass each other between classes or on the quad.

I think he’s noticed my attempts to be acknowledged; he hasn’t looked away or seemed annoyed yet, so that’s promising, right?

It’s probably for the best that we haven’t connected, though; Father would never approve of me entertaining thoughts like this, not when I’m supposed to be focused on studying.

I can already hear him chiding me in my ears: “Romance is a distraction, Sol’Régem, and distractions lead to mistakes, and mistakes won’t be tolerated.”

But that doesn’t mean I can’t continue to admire him from afar.

I hope I get to see him again tomorrow.

Glowing Star 18th, 1346

Dear Diary,

I did it! I ACTUALLY talked to him today! I don’t think my heart’s come down from my throat since Illusion class, and if I combust into a glorious star soon, please know I died happy.

I didn’t even realize we were paired together in class today at first; I was too busy daydreaming to pay attention. Professor Veilwright just said our names, and the next thing I know, Adrien is standing right next to me. I stood there and tried very hard not to forget how breathing works.

We were practicing the Minor Illusion spell, specifically to manipulate light and learn control. This is stuff Adrien is really good at, which is why I was surprised when he struggled today.

His spell kept fizzling out, and he asked if I could help him. He asked me diary!

At first, I thought I misheard him because Adrien doesn’t usually need help with any classes; he’s brilliant, precise, and quite an adept caster, maybe more so than me, so why would he need my help? Of course, I said yes, I’m not stupid enough to let a chance like this pass me by!

I watched him cast again and noticed his somatic form was barely off; his wrist was angled a bit too high, and his fingers weren’t quite anchoring the shape correctly. Again, diary, it’s so weird to see him struggle with somatics when I know he’s so precise.

Anyways, I took his hands in mine and showed him the proper technique; I guided his fingers and helped adjust his wrist.

Diary. His hands were so warm; they felt like I was warming my hands near the hearth in my father’s study back at home. They felt safe and steady, and they were so soft!

We cast the spell together, and the illusion bloomed into these tiny little fireworks in the air. They were so beautiful: soft gold, blue, and violet sparks drifted into the air and faded away like miniature stars.

It was so perfect, diary, minus the fact that when he looked at me after, I’m pretty sure I looked as red as a tomato, signaled by the heat in my face and my heart doing Caspian-level acrobatics in my chest. But at the same time, I felt this wave of calm roll through me like a gentle stream. I swear I wasn’t trying to read him; it felt like his emotions just leaked into me. I felt his focus, his ease, his determination. It was nice.

He thanked me afterwards and said: “Now I know which illusionist to go to for help next time,” with that dreamy voice of his! I don’t think I’ll ever emotionally recover from this.

And then, diary, because apparently today the spirit of bravery possessed me: at the end of class, I used the momentum from earlier and asked if he wanted to hang out later today.

I was sooooo casual about it, and totally didn’t stutter over my world like a babbling baboon!

Guess what, diary? He said YES!

He told me to meet him at the greenhouse, and we could walk around campus and talk!

I can’t write for much longer because I have to figure out what to wear! Maybe I’ll wear the music-embroidered shirt I made last week, or maybe Vornakir has something nice I can borrow; he always looks so polished.

Wish me luck!

Glowing Star 18th, 1346 — Midnight

Dear Diary,

I am back in my dorm room and alive, just barely. Diary, I had the best time today; it’s been the most fun I’ve had in a while ~~, ever since Lucien…~~.

He met me at the greenhouse like he said he would, and he flashed me that handsome smile of his when he saw me; I think that moment alone shaved at least five years off my lifespan.

We walked around campus for a while, talking about anything and everything; it didn’t feel forced or awkward or like I was scrambling for things to say, like I thought it would. I actually felt pretty natural and comfortable around him, like we’d been doing this kind of thing for years instead of hours.

We started by talking about our favorite color: mine, ironically, is sunrise yellow, even though I hate the morning; he found that rather amusing. His is purple, but not the vibrant shades; he likes deep purple, like an amethyst.

We also talked about books, and I found out that he loves literature of all kinds: classic epics, fantasy novels, historical accounts. The way his eyes light up when he talks about his favorite stories brought a smile to my face. He asked what my favorite book was, and I had to awkwardly tell him I wasn’t a huge reader. I did tell him about my favorite book as a kid though; it was about a princess who longed to be a knight. He wasn’t mean about me not reading a lot, and he actually gave me a few book recommendations he thought I’d like! One of them was called The Celestial Trials.

I even got to tell him about my fashion designs. I was nervous at first, and my palms got super sweaty because talking about my passion hasn’t exactly gone well in the past. But he didn’t laugh at me or say it was silly; he even said he’d love to see my portfolio someday, and I think that caused my soul to leave my body briefly.

Our walk eventually drifted past the College of Illusion and into the forest behind the building. He led me down this path dense with foliage, and I was confused about why he brought me there, but then Adrien parted some of the greenery and showed me this large, hidden pond tucked between the trees.

Diary, it was so beautiful. The water was crystal clear, reflecting the stars above like a giant mirror, and everything around it was so quiet. It was like the world was holding its breath: there was no shouting, no crowds, no pressure; it was just me, Adrien, the soft rustling of the wind, and the occasional croaking of frogs.

We sat along the edge for a while and kept talking, mostly about school and Sponsorship Day, and then Diary, AND THEN, the most amazing thing happened!

Adrien asked me if I wanted to go on a date with him this weekend. A DATE WITH THE ADRIEN THALSON! I’m certain my heart stopped beating, and then restarted at a full sprint.

I somehow managed to say yes without screaming, crying, or throwing up, which I personally consider a huge achievement. I asked what we’d be doing, and he just winked at me! He said it was a surprise, and I’d have to wait and see.

He said he wanted to make sure I got back safely, so he walked me back to my dorm afterward. He told me goodnight when we returned, and then just walked away like he hadn’t just lit a stick of dynamite in my head.

I waited to explode until he was out of earshot, then I absolutely lost my mind. I might’ve spun in a circle, squealed, and excitedly punched the air.

Unfortunately, Diana witnessed my small celebration. Usually, I would’ve verbally assaulted her and told her to mind her own damn business, but I was too giddy to let that bitch ruin my night, so I simply walked inside with my heart in orbit.

Diary, I’m going on a date! I can’t stop smiling or thinking about him, and I can’t wait until the weekend!

Glowing Star 21st, 1346

Dear Diary,

I don’t think I’ve touched the ground since I got back; if I start flying around the dorm in my sleep, just let it happen.

Today was perfect; I don’t think I have a better word to describe it.

Adrien met me outside my dorm, and I almost tripped down the steps when I saw him. Very humiliating for me, but also very on brand.

I wore my favorite outfit today: that nice white dress shirt underneath the blue asymmetric sweater I designed, my favorite black pants, and my teal high-tops. I even used that golden hair scarf with star embroidery to pull my hair into a half-up style. I wanted to look good, but not look like I was trying too hard. Who am I kidding, though? I always go all out with fashion, regardless of the circumstances; you can’t perform well if you look like shit, is something I always say!

Diary, he looked good today; I mean, he always looks amazing, but I can tell he stepped up his style-game today. Usually, he wears sweaters or a cardigan, but today, he was wearing this really nice black leather jacket; I didn’t even know he owned leather. He wore this loose-fitting, lilac t-shirt underneath, along with a pair of blue jeans and black boots. I was used to him having this quiet, nerdy aesthetic, but surprisingly, he could also pull off the hot, mysterious aesthetic equally as well!

I had to quickly gather myself before I talked to him; almost tripping down the stairs was already embarrassing enough. I didn’t need to add incomprehensible speech to the list. Even though I was internally panicking, I managed to tell him he looked nice, like a normal person!

I asked him where we were going, and he just lifted a finger to his lips and said, “It’s a secret.” AND THEN, he did the smoothest move ever, and slid the scarf out of my hair, tied it around my eyes, took my hands in his, and told me to trust him.

My legs felt like jelly during the whole walk. He led me around for maybe five minutes, and when he finally removed the scarf, I saw we were outside the greenhouse, and he had set up a picnic.

There was the softest-looking grey blanket laid out, little fairy lights in jars, snacks and pastries, a picnic basket, and a bottle of Moscato. He actually remembered my favorite wine! It’s the little things that matter, diary!

We sat together, ate, talked, and laughed. It felt so easy, like we were in our own little bubble separated from the rest of the world.

I brought my sketchbook with me so we could go through some of my designs together. I showed him some mock-up designs, and he asked me so many questions! He asked about the fabrics, the inspirations, and what pieces meant the most to me. It was so nice to explain my passion to someone genuinely interested! At one point, we both reached for the book at the same time, and our hands brushed against each other. We both froze, and I definitely saw him blush. It was like something straight out of one of those romantic novels Auri always reads.

Later, we were lying together on the blanket, looking up at the clouds and naming what we saw. I told him one looked like Whimsey, but he said he didn’t see it, and it looked like a loaf of bread. I guess Adrien might be slightly blind because I don’t know in what universe that cloud looked like bread…or maybe he was still hungry.

Halfway through our cloud-watching session, Adrien suddenly sat up and told me he'd almost forgotten something. Then he reached into the picnic basket and pulled out a book: The Celestial Trials, the same book he recommended to me a few days ago.

I smiled so wide when I took the book and thanked him that my mouth hurt. Then he told me he’d quiz me about it on our next date, which means he’s planning for there to be a second date!

He made me forget that anything else was around me, and in that brief moment, the only thing on my mind was him.

The kiss was so warm and intentional, and a gushing feeling I didn’t know how to describe rushed into me and lingered around, making my knees feel like jelly. He pulled back just as gently, his thumb still resting against my jaw, as he whispered, “Goodnight, Sol,” into my ear. Then, like he hadn’t just rewritten my entire brain chemistry, he let go and slipped away down the path.

Tonight it’s my turn to plan our date! I remember a few days ago, Adrien mentioned that he loved reading under the stars; he said there was some magic in getting lost in a story while a sky full of constellations stretched on for miles. That Diary, gave me the most brilliant idea.

I surrounded it with a bunch of fluffy pillows, which I may or may not have stolen from our living room (I will return them later, I swear).

I’ve also been paying attention to Adrien’s favorite foods so I can lay out a nice spread for him. His absolute favorite things to eat in the world are tarts, specifically raspberry and blackberry tarts. I’ll have to thank Vornakir later for his help in making them because I absolutely would have burned them into oblivion otherwise. I packed some other little snacks too: meat and cheese, a baguette, Earl Grey tea, things I remember him mentioning liking in passing; just enough to graze on while we read and talk.

Speaking of reading, I bought two copies of that new series he’s been raving about: The Secret Circle. It’s this novel about a coven of witches transported to a land without magic, trying to find their way home. It sounds fascinating and also terrifying; I can’t imagine a being in a world with no magic. How would I maintain my wardrobe?! Anyway, the idea is we can do what he likes and read under the stars together.

Kael helped me with the final touches, specifically an environmental enchantment. It dimmed all the ambient light around the rooftop and snuffed out any outside noise so it would just be me, Adrien, and the sky. I did bring a small lantern, though, just in case we can’t actually see the pages; as romantic as darkness can be, I would prefer that we can actually read the books.

The thing I’m most proud of is that I managed to get a telescope from Po. I have no idea what I agreed to in exchange; there was a lot of mumbling about “nonrefundable,” and something about “owing him a favor for a discount,” and I’m fairly certain I nodded when I shouldn’t have, but it’s fine. Adrien loves constellations and the myths, histories, and meanings behind them, and I just want to make sure he could see them up close.

I really hope he likes everything. Anyway, I have to get ready before I spiral into overthinking-mode and dismantle everything I’ve set up. I’ll update you later.

Wish me luck, Diary.

Glowing Star 28th, 1346 — Late Night

Dear Diary,

I just got back from my date, and tonight was…gods, I don’t even know if I have proper words for it besides everything I hoped it would be and more.

When we got up to the rooftop, and he saw everything, he stopped for a moment to take it all in. Then he looked at me with this soft, almost disbelieving smile and said it was perfect.

Diary, I think my heart and ego grew three sizes in that moment.

We sat together and ate first; he really liked the blackberry tarts, as I expected, so I will make sure to find a way to thank Vornakir again, because those might have single-handedly secured my romantic future.

We talked and laughed like we usually do, but it felt like we were connecting on a deeper level tonight, and like we weren’t just passing time.

Then we started reading under the stars together, and I don’t think I’ll ever forget what that looked like: him sitting beside me, the faint glow of the lantern catching the edges of his face, the endless, starry sky stretched above us. Now and then, one of us would comment on the plot or a questionable action the main character, Elio, made, and it felt so nice and easy to be around each other.

Diary, he lit up when I showed him the telescope. Actually, I don't think I’ve ever seen him look this openly excited before. He kept pointing things out: telling me the constellations' names, the myths behind them, showing me little details I never would have noticed on my own. He told me a forbidden romance tale behind two neighboring constellations: It’s about two lovers who were forcibly separated by their parents and never got to be with each other during their mortal lives, but they got reincarnated into these constellations that were said to always find each other in the sky, no matter how far apart they drifted.

I hope someday I get to love someone that much.

At some point, we stopped reading and talking and just sat close together watching the stars; it felt like the whole world had gone quiet, just like when he took me to the secret pond.

When it got late, we started slowly packing up together, neither of us in a rush to leave. When everything was put away, and we were standing there, not quite ready to say goodbye, we got close again. I don’t even remember how it happened, but one second we were talking, the next we were looking into each other’s eyes. And then, gently, Adrien leaned in, clearly giving me time to pull away if I wanted to. I didn’t. He lightly pressed his hand against my cheek before his lips met mine.

Everything disappeared: the rooftop, the lantern, the stars, even my own thoughts. All that was left was him: the softness of his lips, the warmth of his palm, the smell of his cologne.

When he pulled away, he smiled at me like I was a sacred treasure worth being careful with.

He walked me back home after that, like he always does, but I don’t remember most of it. I was still somewhere up in the clouds.

I’ve never kissed anyone before, and if that’s what it’s supposed to feel like, then by the gods, I hope he kisses me again.

Dear Diary,

Something’s changed, and not in a loud, dramatic, world-shattering way, but in a quiet way that you only notice after it’s already happened. Ever since we kissed on the rooftop, Adrien’s been different. He’s always been kind, gentle, and intentional, but after that night, it’s like whatever space was between us before disappeared.

He touches me more now than he did before. When we walk together, he always takes my hand into his, like it’s something he’s always done. His hands are always warm, and sometimes he brushes his thumb over my knuckles absentmindedly while we walk and talk, making me lose my train of thought every time.

He lets me lean on him, too. When we’re sitting together, I’ll rest my head on his shoulder, and he’ll shift his body so I’m more comfortable, like he’s preemptively anticipated what I was going to do. Once, I laid my head in his lap while he was reading, and he just kept reading, like it was the most normal thing in the world, while brushing his fingers through my hair.

And he’s started doing this thing when he’s standing behind me, especially when I’m working or talking to someone else, where he’ll rest his chin on the top of my head. Like he’s quietly saying “I’m here.” It makes a warm feeling bloom in my chest.

I’ve noticed I started doing things too, like hugging him from behind. I don’t even think about it anymore, I just wrap my arms around his waist and snuggle in close. Sometimes I’ll tug him down by his collar to place a kiss on his cheek. I’ve started to fix little things without asking, too, like straightening his collar, brushing lint off his jacket, and adjusting his sleeves when they sit wrong. I’ve even started playing with his hands when we’re together, tracing over his palms or lacing them with mine.

It’s not just the touching that’s changed; he looks at me differently, too. Like I’m a bright lantern in a dark place, or like I’m a precious piece of artwork.

Diary, I am in trouble. I didn’t think it was possible to fall any harder than I already had after that night, but I was so, so wrong. There was something about that kiss that changed everything. Before, it felt like I was standing on the outside of something I really wanted and wondering what it would be like to have it. Now I do, and there’s no going back.

When I think about him now, it’s not just butterflies in my stomach, excitement, or a racing heartbeat; it’s deeper. He feels woven into my days and the spaces between my thoughts. I catch myself thinking about what he would say about something, whether he’d like a design I’m working on, or what book I should read next, so that I can talk to him about it. I find things and immediately want to show him. I feel things and immediately want to tell him. When he’s near, I feel calmer, like no matter how loud everything else around me gets, he’ll always be a constant peace I can return to.

I think that’s what scares me the most, and it’s not a bad fear; it’s just that this matters to me more than anything I’ve ever done before. I don’t know if this is just a crush anymore. I don’t think it’s been one since the rooftop. I don't know how to describe what I’m feeling, but I don’t want it to stop.

Glowing Star 39th, 1346

Dear Diary,

I woke up next to him again this morning; I don’t think I’m ever going to get used to this feeling.

Dawn was starting to slip through my curtains, and everything felt peaceful, like nothing in the world could disturb the moment. I was tucked into the crook of his arm, and I stayed still because I didn’t want to wake him and end the moment. His arm was warm and steady around me, and I could feel the slow rise and fall of his breathing. At some point, while I was still half-asleep, I shifted closer, and he instinctively pulled me in tighter.

We stayed like that for a while after we both woke up, and I traced lazy patterns on his arm while he played with the ends of my hair. Then I remembered that I had made something for him. So I reluctantly sat up and told him I had a gift for him, and I think that woke him up more than anything else I could’ve said. I grabbed it from where I’d hidden it under the bed the night before and handed it to him.

Diary, I was so nervous, which is ridiculous, because I made it and knew it was good. But it wasn’t about what I thought, it was about whether he would like it or not.

I watched his face the whole time as he carefully unwrapped the box that contained an Ecto-themed jacket. I’ll never forget the way his expression shifted from curiosity to recognition to excitement. He ran his hands over the fabric, taking it all in piece by piece. Then he looked at me with such a warm smile and said he loved it.

Diary, I think that might’ve been the second-best thing that happened today. Because afterward, he got really quiet, clearly in deep thought as his fingers brushed over the stitching.

After a while, he said: “We’ve been doing this for a while: sending time together, going on dates, falling asleep in each other’s arms. I’ve been wanting to ask you something for a while now.”

Diary, my entire body went still when he then asked if I would be his boyfriend.

I don’t really remember what I said at first; I think I just stared at him for a couple of seconds, because my brain had completely shut down, but I know I definitely said yes very enthusiastically and possibly more than once after collecting myself! I might’ve laughed and cried a little while I hugged him, and I didn’t let go for a solid minute.

Diary, I have a boyfriend. My first boyfriend. The Adrien Thalson is my boyfriend. I didn't think that sentence would leave my lips for a while, but here we are!

We stayed there just holding each other a little closer than before, like something had shifted into place, and this had always been where we were heading.

I’ve never been happier.
