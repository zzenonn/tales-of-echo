The common room was quiet, dimly lit by the warm glow of the hanging lanterns. Auri lounged on the couch, her legs and tail tucked beneath her, dark shorts and sheer black tights dotted with tiny stars. A lace-lined shirt peeked out from under an open button-up, the fabric relaxed over her shoulders as she shifted slightly. Twiggle rustled contentedly in her lap, leaves nudging against her hand as she absently ran her fingers across the foliage while reading a book titled Shadows of Betrayal.

She took a sip from her tea. It was a black tea with a delightful mix of chocolate, cherry, and almond she’d found in one of the small shops on campus. While this particular blend was a little more expensive, there weren’t many things she wasted money on, or at least that’s how she justified the purchase.

The warmth of the room, the quiet of the academy beyond their door, the soft crackle of the fire, and the gentle rustling of leaves made it easy to relax. She sank a little deeper into the cushions, eyes scanning the page with the kind of focus that made the outside world feel far away.

Sol opened the door to the dorm and quietly slid into the common area. Three bags, respectively coated in gold, silver, and bronze colored magic, floated obediently behind him in an orderly line; they were stuffed to the brim with different colored fabrics, spools of threads, stencils, and what looked like a porcupine, but in reality was just an overcrowded pincushion that had been through the war that is designer Sol. A fourth bag bobbed lower than the rest; it was obvious that it was the heaviest of the lot, given its apparent attitude when asked to levitate itself rather than be carried.

Instead, Sol’s arms were occupied with the real prize: silky lengths of fabric that caught the light like water—soft, rich, and clearly expensive. One draped over his forearm in a pale, luminous, lavender shade; another gleamed darker, like it had stolen midnight from the night sky.

He paused when he saw Auri peacefully reading. Serenity seemed to have wrapped around her like a blanket: Twiggle nestled in her lap, her attention buried in the pages of her book, tea steaming faintly in her hand.

Sol didn’t move or say anything for a minute, not wanting to intrude and break the quiet moment. Then, with a warm smile, he greeted Auri.

“Hey Auri,” he said softly. His gaze flicked down to Twiggle, and his smile brightened a touch. “And hello to you too, Twig,” he added, tone affectionate and respectful, like he was greeting a tiny dignitary instead of a leaf-rustling companion.

He tilted his head towards his room down the hall, and the hovering bags drifted in that direction like a polite parade, treading carefully not to bump into anything important.

He re-adjusted the fabric in his arms as if it were more delicate than glass. “I’m just putting away supplies for something I’m working on, don’t mind me.”

That was all he offered for now. Usually, Sol loved to talk someone’s ear off about his special projects, but when he didn’t, it usually meant something else more important was running around in his head.

Sol crossed into the kitchen area and carefully set the silky fabric down on the counter, smoothing it so it wouldn’t crease. It spilled slightly over the edge, catching the light in soft ripples.

Only after he was sure the material was still pristine did his eyes flick back to Auri’s hands. The book’s title caught his attention, and he pointed toward it with a slight tilt of his chin.

“Okay,” he said, voice low with interest, “what are you reading? Shadows of Betrayal sounds like the kind of book where absolutely nobody is okay by chapter three.”
