When they had returned to their dorm and she was finally able to slip away to her room, Zašia fell back onto the bed, uncharacteristic of her, but her mind was playing back the events of the day and she was feeling crushed under the weight of her mistakes.

The social ones.

Firstly, she'd been so self-conscious. Sol had inadvertently hit on some of her weak points and Redd as well. Amidst the intense sensation of anxiety that had bile burning in the back of her throat, there was a moment of warmth. Both Vornakir and Caspian had at times spoken up loudly and steered the conversation away from her, her circumstance, and her shortcomings.

Vornakir in particular had done so by highlighting the shame and self-directed anger he felt over being suspended from the dueling league, among the other consequences he was given for his use of their highly irregular gifts. Spells that should have been well-beyond their capabilities, perhaps, but were almost as natural as breathing, somehow.

It made her consider her own power. She would try to dream walk once more tonight. She wasn't sure she should ask Vornakir to watch over her as she slept. While she had the utmost trust in Caspian to pull her away from any danger, he was entirely out of the question after how she had behaved.

She hadn't meant to, and that to her was the worst part. She'd spoken far too freely with him, and she took a moment to contemplate why. Her analytical mind went to work, the metaphorical gears turning. Readily, she felt she understood her actions, but could not and would not excuse herself just because she could puzzle out the why.

With all of that weighing heavily on her mind, making her chest feel tight and tying her stomach in knots, she dressed for bed. Pulling back the covers, she sat on the edge of the bed, reading to swing her feet up off the floor and onto the mattress to slip under the sheets and the quilt she'd brought from home.

Hesitation. Contemplation. Action.

Zašia stood up again, leaving the bed unmade and pulled her robe from the hook she left it on at the threshold of her bath. With a deep breath, she pulled open her door and headed down the hallway. For once, she did not step lightly, attempting not to be heard as she strode to Vornakir's door, her head held high in a pantomime of the confidence she did not feel, but hoped she might manifest. She knocked. Quietly, but with a rap of her knuckles, rather than the soft tapping of her manicured nails against the door, like a well-behaved dog waiting at the back door to be let in.

"It's me," she announced. She wasn't particularly loud about it, but nor was she speaking in a hush, instead maintaining a conversational volume that could be noted by others nearby, if indistinct through the walls that divide them in the halls. As the only woman in their dorm, it certainly made it obvious when she spoke.

Whilst Zašia wallowed beneath her feeling of social inadequacy, Vornakir was roiling with a similar issue but his took the form of physical and to some degree magical ineptitude. After the trial and everyone had returned to their own domains, he paced his floor. As the rest likely were, he was in his bed clothes - his favorite golden scale patterned pajamas and no shirt, the side of his torso sans an arm now fully protected with the layer of shimmering golden adult scales after the new-grown ones shed away.

Something beneath the scales could simmer no longer, though. Emotions that weren’t his own strangled him, his throat tightening and his jaw ticking in sourceless rage. It was like a fire being stoked against his will burning his insides, his neck craned and head turned to the side to make a crunching sound as he strained the bone enough to pop. His ochre eyes slitted and his nostrils flared heavily - a chuff of breath so frustrated that he could swear there was a taste of ash and smoke on his tongue.

He needed a release, something to snare his attention and focus. He wasn’t sure why these emotions grappled him, but he knew he needed to vent them out. He fell to the floor after a sparkling flick of his wrist conjured his trusty mage hand, and he began doing one armed push-ups.

It was a struggle, doing even two handed ones before was. But he needed to be better, he couldn’t stand to shame himself forever. He wasn’t as fast or efficient as Caspian could do them, he didn’t even bother counting them. He knew he wouldn’t stack up. But the effort was being made, several grueling seconds passing every-time he lowered himself almost nose to the floor and even more passed before he could struggle to lift himself upward. When he took the stance of holding himself aloft after each push up, his arm noticeably wobbled — the muscle screaming in protest as sweat beaded at his brow and fell from his skin to the ground below. It wasn’t enough.

”Gold holds value to everyone. It’s yearned for, it’s fought for, it’s universally lusted over and killed for. So what does that make me…? Polished bronze? Fool’s Gold?”

He gasped and suddenly his face made contact with the floor as his muscle gave out. A stained, choked nearly silent sob tore at his throat as he pounded a hand into the wooden floor and firmly planted his palm on it again.

“Not enough…I need more…” He muttered through heavy pants as his throat burned, his ragged breaths felt like glass tickling his lungs. The mage hand found its prey and brought a book over for him to wash over, propped open beneath him during his physical struggle.

For several long moments, he repeated the pitiful display of forcing himself to work out and read to distract his mind, metaphorically sweating out every insecurity and inadequacy he veiled behind his mask of golden confidence.

“To…find…A Fey….is unheard of…Outside of…mistakes…summoning…and their own will…having their..name…will make it—“ He paused his aloud reading of the tome on Fey at the sudden knock. He willed the hand over, having it unlock both doors for her to enter at will.

He just tried to manage getting himself off the ground before she was able to enter and see how he looked, and hopefully able to don the metaphoric mask to greet her in time.
