Ancient Star (Ancient Star 19th, 1346)

Mar 06, 2026 · 05:24 AM UTC

Today marks the official beginning of the Chroma Clash. A Banquet was held - I suppose you could call it that. I’m not used to them being attended in such low numbers. But I suppose the attendance makes sense for what it is.

Regardless, it is perhaps strange that I’m even committing these thoughts to a journal…speaking to myself in this manner. Yet to me, it feels necessary. Today marks not only the beginning of the Clash but the beginning of something else entirely. An attempt at a new era for myself. I am not the man I should be, and I’m not the man I wish to be. Perhaps in time this will become a record of growth, something I can look back upon in years to come. Something that may help me find myself again should I ever lose sight of who I am.

Again.

Something I said to Noxira sparked a realization in me that I have failed to grasp for far too long. Some people are born great, others must struggle to achieve greatness. Though I believe those who are born great are bound by a ceiling and their own hubris is their pride…And I was born great. But that doesn’t mean that I am great.

I was not born a great leader capable of inspiring all who hear my voice to follow me blindly. I was not born capable of reducing those who oppose me to cinders in sweeping displays of grand magic. I was born with scales, and I was born with a great last name. Because of that, I had potential and my story was written the moment I took my first breath.

But if I died today, how would I be remembered? Back home, almost everyone referred to me as Lord Vanori - or they didn’t include the Lord. Vanori, Vanori, Vanori. It borders on the obscene how often my surname becomes my given name in the mouths of others. But the words left my lips before I realized I even spoke them to Noxira.

I do not wish to be remembered as Vanori - but as Vornakir. That’s the truth that’s finally begun to settle in my mind.

Gods, how foolish I’ve been up until now. How could I have ever hoped to lead Ebonspire - To lead hundreds, or thousands of citizens - if I can’t even inspire admiration or confidence among a couple to a dozen students? How could I aspire to be a Sovereign when my own dormmates look upon me as though every choice I make is driven by Vanori Pride?

How could I hope to enact change in a culture that systematically raises people like me to become puppets to their ambitions - our lives penned by those who’ve already finished their own stories and chart the paths we must take…forcing us to slaughter one another for the sake of what? Family Pride?

Mar 06, 2026 · 05:24 AM UTC

What pride do I even truly possess if my life is not even my own? Pride in a family name, Pride in magic stolen from not one but two draconic bloodlines? My prestige is a surname and powers that aren’t even mine. Just for once, I want a reason to feel pride in simply being Vornakir.

If Tyravax or Nyx were to kill me, I wouldn’t be remembered as Vornakir Vanori. I’d be remembered as the Vanori whelp who was too weak to survive, too pitiful to grow his wings and roar in defiance.

I must come to terms with the truth; I am only blood of the Dragon, not the Dragon itself. Dragons are free.

And I am…simply not.

I am a slave to my name, a slave to my family, and a slave to their Pride. Before I can ever hope to break the chains that bind those who come after me, I must first break the chains that hold down my own wings.

I will win this Chroma Clash. I have to win it. And I will become the leader the Archmage saw in me.

If I succeed, perhaps it will be proof that I can become the man I need to be without relying on borrowed power any longer…but if I fail, then I will do what must be done - My pride be damned.

Hopefully in time, my friends will understand why I could not stand beside them. And perhaps one day they will finally see me not as the man I was born to be, but as the man I am striving to become.

Ancient Star, 20th, 1346

Mar 09, 2026 · 06:07 AM UTC

Today has been an atrocious showing and display, in my most humble opinion. It started quite fine, at least for the most part. A simple day, waking up and ensuring that my scales haven't spread any worse than they already have. The ache worsens now and then, and naturally it exists only below the exacerbated scales on my left side that are slowly consuming more of my flesh. The discomfort that breathes within my arcana hasn't worsened thankfully - In each incantation and sigil I weave, I feel that same sharpness in my blood. Only Auri has figured out so far that the ache beneath my scales exists, so for that I'm thankful. I do not want to worry them. Doubtless they wouldn't be able to handle it given the way they're all acting as of late.

I'm not sure what's more bothersome. The mounting responsibilities or the lack of true unity and direction we have in this group. Just when I thought, perhaps, we could make it a day without people barking and biting at one another of course I'd get contact from Ember that we have intruders in the Inverse. Cynthia and Cedric - because naturally it must be Cedric. Somewhere in his incoherent rambling, Cynthia made it apparent we needed to speak to our previous Headmaster. Although I would have very much enjoyed being able to pick Cynthia's brain a little more about the situation (I have very reasonable suspicion she's being forced to do Quill's bidding due to something Quill has a thumb on her forehead over, or she's the one pulling the strings because of something she won't tell us), for some unfathomable fucking reason another fight had to occur.

Redd I suppose thought it would be funny to Polymorph Cedric into a rat and Sunni played along and shoved him into his bag - Then Caspian decided he didn't want to be part of this anymore and walked away. Thankfully it seems Auri calmed him down. Another fight, another time when everyone acts without thinking - It's beginning to grate on my last fucking nerve. I know my homeland isn't perfect, my culture and society is gods awful but at least there's order. At least I had the mind to put Cedric's actions to our use in the future and secure his silence without leaving him to Cynthia or Quill's better judgement.

I don't understand what has changed but I feel as though almost everyone is somehow becoming enamored with the idea that what they think is the only route we must take or what they want to say is what must be said - And it seems its only leading to more and more conflict. I believe it's necessary for us to have a leader among us at this juncture. And as much as I came here to avoid the idea of putting that particular spotlight on myself, I don't think anyone else here is qualified or capable of it besides myself.

Mar 09, 2026 · 06:07 AM UTC

Although I adore my friends, truly, none of us are perfect. Sunni speaks his mind too much without regard for consequences and emotions weigh far too heavily on him which leads to irrational actions, Redd has a natural disposition to causing damage to bystanders with his actions without regard for the lasting effect it may have on our names, Auri has a level head but she's too soft spoken to tell hard truths or give definitive direction - Which are two things this group desperately needs to have done in most cases, and Caspian has a strong moral compass but seems to have this have this habit of impressing his concept of right and wrong onto everyone else then expressing disdain in how we carry ourselves when we don't meet that standard.

Doubtless there will be annoyances along the way and protests to the idea that we need strong leadership but the alternative is this complete mess that keeps happening anytime the others attempt to tunnel vision our group onto a black and white morality or a route based on disregard of navigating Spark Oath politically, reputably and cautiously. Whether we like it or not, we have more and more thrust upon us by the day, not to mention a severe uptick in inner strife - And whatever it is anyone else is attempting to do in order to keep our heads above water is failing miserably. Whatever I do no matter how good or bad it is must be an improvement on whatever this currently is.

My friends deserve someone to lean on that isn't Sunni with their life troubles whether they be relationship, friendship or personal trials so that he can experience his own feelings for a while and gather his thoughts. They deserve someone to bring warmth and comfort to them that isn't Redd so that he can finally focus on the troubles that's been plaguing him for some time now both back home and within his social life. They deserve someone to think rationally, formulate strategy and help ensure they aren't falling behind in their studies so that Auri can slim down her schedule and allow herself time to take care of herself for once because she pours from an empty glass far too often leaving herself parched. They deserve someone to offer guidance as individuals, and as a group, through navigating our Sparkian Responsibilities in a way that doesn't add even more strain to our lives but resolves these ever-increasing conflicts so that Caspian can take a breath and loosen his posture (and figure out what the fuck it is inside him).

Is it vain to say these things? Almost definitely. But surely - hopefully - at least one of my friends would agree that my stepping in to take a leadership role would benefit us. Even in some of Aurum's last words to us, he referred to each of us with a word - and mine was 'Leader'. Maybe that's the best way I can help everyone. Because this fighting is getting old, watching everyone bicker and have to make up once a week.

I am not perfect. I have my flaws. I'm not even saying I am the only clear answer. But I've changed far more than anyone else here knows from the man who arrived at Spark Oath so long ago. And as I've covered before, if I can't lead four other students how will I ever lead Ebonspire? So who better?
