Auri

The rooftop was quieter than she remembered. Just…still. No classes, no bells, no rush of students cutting across the stone paths below. The school felt like it was holding its breath, and maybe she was too.

Auri stepped out into the open air, the breeze tugging gently at her skirt as the sun peeked across the horizon low enough to paint the edges of the world in soft gold. She stood near the edge of the platform where she had met Vornakir before, and the skyline stretched before her. The last time they were here, they’d promised to meet again. He’d called it a “date," but Auri wasn’t sure if he meant the word literally or simply as a turn of phrase, and that uncertainty made her stomach flutter just a little. Either way…she wanted to be here, to see him.

Her hands found the edge of the stone ledge, her shoulders relaxing now that she was there. The past few days pressed at the edges of her mind, but she didn’t reach for any of it. Instead, she reached for the item in her bag. The one she’d promised to share with him. She felt the contours, remembered why it mattered to her, and let her mind drift for a moment.

She thought of the last time they had danced together: the gentle sway of the music, the warmth of his hand in hers, the soft press of his lips to her cheek after she had done the same. The memory made her chest tighten, a rush of warmth, a spark of longing that hadn’t dulled even in the aftermath of everything that had unfolded.

Auri glanced around the empty rooftop, noticing the way the early sunlight caught the edges of the stone. The world felt suspended in that quiet moment, and she let herself breathe into it, letting the anticipation hum through her like static.

And then she waited.

Eyes closed, he took on a sharp breath through his nostrils and filled his chest. No sooner he opened his eyes would his world narrow onto her — nothing else existed. Had he stopped breathing, stopped moving, stopped thinking? He couldn’t tell. Several long seconds of his life were stolen by the realization that all his dooming was for nothing.

She was here. And a brick relief slammed into his stomach. It took even longer for him to completely bring himself to composure once again but when he did, his lips curved into a smile.

“Arivae.” He called to her in a tone like satin. “What a lovely surprise. Honestly, I thought perhaps you’d have too much on your mind to remember our promise.” He admitted, sheepishly or not it was true.
