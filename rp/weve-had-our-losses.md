***FWHUMP!***

The sound was unceremonious, breaking the silence of the early morning. When Caspian turned, he saw her there, standing next to a bag on the ground, likely the source of the noise when it was dropped.

Chin up.

Long spine.

"I'm here to train."

Resolute.

The sun shone behind her, lighting up her pale hair and giving her the effect of the halo many aasimar had. Not her. There were so many things Zašia *knew* she lacked. She was going to make up for those things by acquiring new skills. "I want to join the spell dueling league and I am in no position for that as it stands."

When she shook her head, her ponytail swayed. As she was the last time she was in this place, she was fresh-faced, her golden freckles unimpeded by make-up powders.  She was serious about this. That determined expression softened some, though her conviction wavered not one bit. "Please."


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 14, 2025 &middot; 01:33 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian had been in the middle of his glaive drills when he stopped sharply at the sound of the impact. As he turned to see Zašia standing there was little surprise in his expression, she knew he came here every morning and he had invited her to come anytime. He planted the haft of his glaive in the ground and leaned against it listening to her.

When she announced her intent to train a huge smile broke out across Caspian's face. He let her finish and as she said more he only grew more excited. When the please was added at the end he couldn't help but laugh at the last minute nicety.

"Took you long enough Zašia. No need for a please, if you want to be here then you had it right the first time. I don't need to tell you that in life you have to reach out and take the things you truly want, niceties not required."

Caspian could sense her dedication to the task at hand. He immediately set to work in his mind planning and thinking of what best could help her achieve the spell dueling results she sought. 

"And what do we have here?"  He asked as he moved over and poked her heavy bag with the haft of his glaive. 

His eyes glimmered at the mystery. She wouldn't have brought something with her unless she had some ideas and he couldn't wait to see what she had in store for their morning.

"A notebook for taking notes on your guidance. Extra water. Wrist braces. My books and an extra set of clothes in case we don't finish up in time for me to go back to the dorm and get dressed..."

She looked up, that blush deepening. "I had to over-prepare in order to convince myself that I was ready for this. Please don't make fun of me for it."


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Sep 15, 2025 &middot; 03:50 AM UTC</sub>

That and her magic, which unfortunately was limited, but so was his own. He could make this work. 

"Make fun of you? Maybe I'd have teased a bit if you had come unprepared but there's nothing to mock about being serious about pursuing this. Speaking of, you want to spell duel and I assume your looking for ways to even the playing field? If that's the case then let's get a weapon in your hands."

He had the echo stroll back with a few options. A pair of daggers, a longbow, and a rapier. He went over grabbed a traditional longsword and spun it in his hands with a flourish. Unlike with Vornakir's training he didn't approach this with the harsh tones of a drill sergeant but more so a patient teacher. His excitement hadn't wavered. Fond memories played out in his head, another unexpected guest who had requested to join his training sessions, though that one was flanked by a cadre of royal guards. 

"First we'll get you comfortable with using the weapons and then we can work on you using magic to make the weapon dueling legal."
