The library's tall glass doors reflected Auri's outline: crimson skin flushed from the walk over, dark hair in a tight braid. Polished enough to look intentional. First impressions mattered. People needed to see a competent tutor, not a scholarship kid clawing her way up through borrowed books and sleepless nights. 

The scent of old parchment and binding glue stirred something warm in her chest. It smelled like home. Her mentor Artorius covered every surface of his tower with textbooks, the air holding candle smoke and powdery pages. Here, the prestige felt sharper, but the smell was the same. 

She stepped inside, tail wrapped neatly around her leg. *Professional*, she reminded herself. *Composed*. 

Sunlight filtered through the tall windows, casting pale gold stripes across the tables. Dust motes drifted lazily, catching light like tiny stars. 

She spotted Sol at the table before he noticed her approaching. Even from across the room, she could sense his magic, unbidden and effortless, reacting to his presence the way sparks hovered near an open flame. 

Auri crossed the room in careful strides, her boots barely making a sound. She set her books down softly and offered Sol a polite nod. 

She sat, smoothing her pants and then her notes: ecto-flow charts, resonance tables, color-coded in the margins. She was organized and prepared. 

"Today we're covering foundational magic theory," she said matter-of-factly. "Stable illusions require structure, not just instinct and scenic displays."

Her words were both an insult and a compliment. Sol's magic was undeniably impressive. 

As she lifted her gaze, her criticism was clear, but so was her admiration. 

"When you're ready," she said evenly, controlled, "start with the basics: the origin of all magical energy. If you know that, we can build from there."

She folded her hands, back straight. *Professional. Composed.*
