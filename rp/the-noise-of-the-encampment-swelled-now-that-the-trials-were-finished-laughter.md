### &#x270D; Calarel

The noise of the encampment swelled now that the trials were finished. Laughter, clattering mugs, and the shouts of victory carried on through smoke and torchlight. Calarel watched Ravye peel off toward the bar after she made it clear exactly how she planned to celebrate their victory. Calarel exhaled slowly. 

Ravye questioned her strength and was adorably nervous at times, yes, but she consistently proved that she could handle herself. Still, the instinct to keep her out of harm’s way stirred anyway, impossible to shut off. She stripped out of her armor in the tent with efficiency, the weight of it thudding onto the rug beside Alden and Talys. What remained were her close-fitting black leathers, long-sleeved and built for movement rather than display. The halberd stayed, but her dagger remained at her side.

By the time she reached the bar, Ravye had already claimed a space for them. Calarel stepped in beside her, leaning back against the counter, one boot hooked casually on the rung below. She caught the bartender’s eye and ordered a Blackfyre Whiskey, then flicked her gaze once around the room, taking note of exits and postures before settling on Ravye. Her voice stayed low, meant only for her to hear.

“You did well today,” Calarel said. “With handling your wyvern, and the manticore too.”

The bartender slipped her drink into her hand, and Calarel took it. She didn’t drink it yet, but held the glass loosely in her hand as she remained beside her friend, present without hovering and watching the room the way she always did.

Rayve’s hand landed on her shoulder before Calarel could retreat another step, and hesitation flashed across her face for a heartbeat. Then the music swelled, rhythmic and insistent, and she exhaled. 

“...Alright, I suppose,” she said, already letting herself be pulled. 

She followed the elven woman onto the open space, stuff for exactly two beats before the rhythm found her. Boots hit the ground in time as the other dancers stomped the floor. Hands came together on the clap. When it was time to spin her partner, Calarel took Ravye’s hand and lifted it, giving her space to turn before linking arms when the beat demanded it. 

The bard played on, the tune lively, and Calarel stayed with her friend every step. The air, once heavy with anxious anticipation, was now filled with drinking, laughter, and celebration. 

Just as the dance began the second round of motions, they reached the moment where partners were expected to change. An eager gentleman held out his arm, clearly intending to sweep Rayve into the next turn, but Calarel smoothly redirected her instead, placing herself between them just long enough to level the man with a look sharp enough to make him reconsider his life choices. He backed away without protest.

### &#x270D; Calarel

Calarel was already there when Ravye stumbled. One hand lifted, hovering near the elven woman’s elbow for a heartbeat before settling lightly at her back. She nudged Ravye upright with a gentle pressure between her shoulder blades, steadying her without pulling her away. 

“You’re fine,” she allowed, low and even. “I’ve got you.” 

The music shifted then, tempo changing, rhythm rolling into something faster and playful. The dancers adjusted instinctively, boots scraping and stomping against the floor as hands came together in time with the new beat. Laughter rippled through the crowd as a few people missed the step and recovered, spinning back into place with careless grace. 

Calarel eased her hand once Ravye was steady again, though she didn’t step far. Her eyes tracked the movement of the dance, the press of bodies, the way the floor seemed to pulse beneath them. She adjusted her stance to match the rhythm, ready to rejoin when the pattern called for it. The air was thick with the scent of ale and smoke, the sound of celebration washing over them like a tide. 
When the next sequence began, she watched Ravye dance off with Poppy and smiled at the sight of her friend enjoying herself. She stepped to the side and watched as the celebration continued right at the edge of the dance floor.

### &#x270D; Alden

Alden pushed through the tavern doors and into the bar proper, the music louder here - fiddle and drum rising over the clatter of boots and laughter. He didn’t look for anyone in particular. Instead, he slid onto an empty stool at the counter and signaled the barkeep.

“Whatever’s strong,” he said quietly.

The glass was set before him, and Alden wrapped his fingers around it, staring into the amber liquid as if it might offer answers. He took a slow drink, the burn grounding him, pulling him out of his own head inch by inch. At this point, Alden was ready to try and forget about what he encountered earlier in the night - whatever it was, he couldn't explain what he had saw, and even worse... He couldn't entirely understand what its words meant. Although he had faith in himself and his compatriots, he still couldn't help but feel nervous.

A burst of laughter cut through the haze. Alden glanced sideways - and there they were.

Ravye spinning Calarel beneath raised hands, both of them flushed and grinning, moving with the kind of careless joy that only came after surviving something you weren’t sure you would. Alden’s expression softened. A small smile crept onto his face, unbidden.

He looked back down at his glass, lifting it in a quiet, private toast before taking another sip, letting their laughter fill the space where heavier thoughts had been.
