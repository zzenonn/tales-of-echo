Auri sat cross-legged on the edge of Redd’s bed, a mug of something warm cradled between her hands. One of Redd’s blankets had been claimed and draped around her shoulders like a makeshift shawl, and a small pile of snacks (some sensible, some decidedly not) had already begun to dwindle between them. These weekly sleepovers of theirs had quietly become a weekly ritual.

She glanced over at him, eyes bright and knowing, the way she always did when she was about to pry just a little.

“So,” Auri began, casual as anything, “how are things with Zurr’ka after your mid-final-battle reunion?"

Her tone was light, letting the question hang there. She didn’t rush it, only added quietly, "Honestly, I’m really happy the two of you made up.”

Then her lips curved, slow and unmistakably teasing.

“And,” she continued, tilting her head, “you *have* been spending a lot of time together lately.” Her brows lifted. “Which I assume is just very *intense emotional reconciliation*.”

She shifted slightly closer, letting her shoulder brush his, her voice dropping conspiratorially. 

“Have you told him you love him yet?”

The question was gentle, curious, without pressure. 

“No need to answer if you’re not ready,” she added with a small shrug. “Just…wondering, since you were thinking about it.”

Auri leaned back against the pillows, expression softening into something fond. She tucked her knees up to her chest and wrapped her arms around them, waiting to hear what her friend had to say.
