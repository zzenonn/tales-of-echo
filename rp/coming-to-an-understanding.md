### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Nov 24, 2025 &middot; 07:54 PM UTC</sub>

The training grounds were empty at this time of night. A thin mist clung low to the grass, catching the lantern-light’s soft gold glow as Sol paced in a tight circle. The bruises from his spar with Vornakir and Caspian had faded, but the *memory* of their fight hadn’t. Every time he blinked, he still felt the echo of the moment he’d cast *Hold Person*on Vornakir, clean, precise, instinctual, before everything fell apart between them.

Yet now, standing before a simple practice dummy tied to a wooden post, he couldn’t even get the spell to hold for more than a second. His magic flickered weakly, gold and silver chains sparking to life and dying again as the spell fizzled out.

“Hold.”

Nothing. Sol tried focusing harder, thinking about the spell constricting around the dummy like a snake.

“C’mon!”

For half a second, the chains solidified, and the dummy stiffened, but a second later the chain shattered, and the dummy slumped forward again, limp and unaffected.

Sol groaned, grabbed his hair with both hands, and backed up a few paces. He tried again. And again. Each attempt felt worse than the last, magic slipping through his mental grip like sand through fingers. His chest was tight, his mind loud, his breathing uneven.
He wasn’t sleeping. He wasn’t eating enough. His Sponsorship Day…and the fight? That had left wounds he’d shoved deep under everything else. He didn’t *think* any of that had anything to do with his sputtering magic, but something was wrong.

When the spell sputtered out for the tenth time, Sol snapped.

“Ugh–fine! If you won’t *hold*, then just–”

He reeled back and kicked the dummy as hard as he could. Which, unfortunately, was *exactly* the wrong idea.
Pain shot up his foot like lightning.

 “GODS DAMMIT—!!”

He hopped on one leg in a furious, graceless circle, clutching his toes through his boot as the dummy wobbled and then toppled over with a pathetic whump. The whole scene made him look less like a fearsome caster and more like an angry kitten fighting furniture.

The humiliation broke something loose in his chest. Sol threw his head back and screamed into the night, raw frustration tearing out of him, echoing across the training grounds.

His magic surged in response, uncharacteristically wild and reactive. Flares of gold, silver, and bronze magic sparked violently, as a burst of overcharged energy snapped from his hand, slamming into the fallen dummy with a loud *BOOM*.

The training dummy exploded into scattered straw and splintered wood, raining down around him in a messy halo.
Sol stood in the middle of the settling debris, breathing hard, curls mussed, foot throbbing, eyes bright with leftover magic and pure, miserable fury.

He clenched his fist, hot tears of frustration streaming down his face.

Though he wore a crown of hope, a halo of gruesome reality darkened its luster. Vornakir’s life wasn’t lived with a presumed existence that he had to fight to keep; he was born to die. A true to his name dead man walking, the entity he met in the last clutches of his near death experience promised it to him. A few short years, he was destined to die. And since that knowledge overtook him, every second he wasn’t getting stronger was a second wasted and life expended.

But a few days of solitude away from Sol had Vornakir looking back on the duel. His entire culture and society was built on deceit, yet he yearned to rise above it. But should he? 

Every morning he found time to arrive at the training field to absolutely obliterate dozens of training dummies and every night he found time to return to the field to meditate on the day past. To think of how each decision he made could be changed, to rewrite destiny itself through sheer force of will. 

That’s when he saw the rather pitiable display. He stood back, watching with auric eyes locked firmly on the Aasimar. The frustration he knew his acquaintance tormented over was so familiar to him.

His better judgment told him to leave, let him wallow in his own anger and emotions. But the yearning for companionship ever remained tight in his chest. He wasn’t able to make friends as a child, and as an adult it was even more difficult. But that didn’t mean he shouldn’t try.

“*Hold Person* isn’t just a spell you cast that locks something down in place. It’s not just fueled by your weaving somatics or chants and spell work. It’s exerting the sheer force of your own will over theirs, and dominating it so throughly that they’re struck paralyzed by it.
