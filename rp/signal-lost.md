### &#x270D; Appo
<sub>Nov 01, 2025 &middot; 09:39 PM UTC</sub>

Appo crept through the eerie quiet of the now-cleared Isylinor hallways. The strike team had returned that evening, they all seemed so proud of themselves. They’d “overcome the obstacles” that Driad *thing* had put in front of them. How delightful. That wasn’t what had happened though, was it? No. They’d merely *accepted their failures*. It was pathetic. Those flaws were not meant to be embraced. Not meant to be accepted. Not meant to be expressed. 

You don’t accept the things that make others reject you, Faramore. You conform. You reshape. You erase the part that doesn’t fit.
You don’t beg for forgiveness, Eris. You accept punishment for your failure. You embrace the pain. You never make those mistakes again.
You don’t blame others for your pain, Vyntrix—Wren. The rules of the world are clear. We all know to obey them. It is your own fault for violating Truth. 

Appo hadn’t made that mistake. How any of them had survived this long in their lives was a mystery to him, they were so… soft. But he wasn’t so easily manipulated. No. 

The Driads had *dared* call him broken. Dared to say that the Cathedral *broke* him. No. It had *forged* him. To be a Resonant was to be the closest a person could be to perfect order. He was thankful for the Cathedral. For the truth it had given him. Even if he had questioned it, however briefly.

Appo stopped as he entered an empty chamber. He glanced around, checking that he was truly alone, and sank into a kneeling posture—legs tucked cleanly beneath him, hands resting on his thighs, spine taut as a wire about to snap, and head bowed at just the right angle.

He had resisted the Driads. He had faced them, their immense power, and held firm. Shown them the strength of Her Word, of his devotion to Her, and the power She gave him. Surely that had been Her test. Surely that was enough to show Her he was truely one of Her disciples—even if he’d failed Her once…

He took a deep breath, eyes closed softly. The air was colder here, nipping lightly at his skin. The floor bit colder still; small stones and remnants of Scurge needled at him through his robes. This wasn’t quite right, but he hadn’t found anywhere here with enough water for prayer. This cold would have to do. 

He bowed his head, hands pressing into his thighs as he drew a breath. The air wasn’t especially cold, yet it felt as though it clawed at his throat and lungs as he breathed it in.

He exhaled—continuing the steady rhythm of breath that he had long been taught, allowing his breathing to align him to the order of the world.

”Numeron,” he spoke, voice barely above a whisper as the name slipped past his teeth, “I resisted their temptation. I enforced Your Word. I kept rhythm. I obeyed. I have served.” The words echoed softly off the smooth walls. The words should have felt… different. *Right*. There should’ve been a presence there… *Her* presence. Where was She?

He tried again, louder.

”Your servant kneels before You. Your Resonant endures in Your name. I did not break. I did not bend. I held firm, even when they tried to name me ruined, broken. For I know that I am blessed to live in service to Your divinity.”

Appo waited, listening.

Silence.

Not even the drip of water, not the hum of distant life. Just… *Silence*.

Appo’s throat tightened. He’d done everything right! Hadn’t he? What… Why? *Where was She?* She’d always been there… a presence just beyond his perception. So where was she now—when he needed her most?

Appo bowed lower, hands slipping down his thighs and onto the cold floor, pressing flat against the stone. He longed for the water—that cold—to envelop his hands. Show his devotion. There was none here though, just the cold rocks.

*Please let that be enough*, he thought desperately.

“I stood before such power and spoke Your Word.” His voice cracked as he spoke into the bare stones. “I held firm. I showed them all the depth of my devotion. I showed them I *believed*. So why do You still—“ He stopped himself with a shudder, teeth grinding against each other. Questions. He’d always asked too many questions. Questions were why he was here. Why he had failed them. Why he had failed *Her*. *Questions*. 

The cold of the stones bit into his hands, creeping through his uniform and up into his knees, slowly crawling up his spine. His body asked to shiver but he held it still. 
He would not tremble.
He would not move.
To let the cold control you was to take power from Her. Devotion was to hold firm in Her presence.
He would not move.
He would not disgrace himself again.

”Forgive me.” He gasped, “Please. Forgive my impatience. Forgive my disrespect. It is not my place to question Your divine will. I merely ask for a sign. A word. A direction. *Anything.*”

His words echoed hollow off the walls, returning to him as empty as they had felt when he spoke them. She wasn’t here… 

***Why not?*** His mind shouted, an almost painful force against his skull, it made him wince.

He had done *everything* for Her. Vyntrix, Eris, Faramore, they were *weak*, discordant, unrefined, yet they were certain of themselves. Each in their own ways. They had *guidance*. How could it possibly be that ***he*** didn’t deserve that? He had failed, yes. But so had all of them!

*One. Stupid. Thing.* His mind echoed softly, remembering the words he’d spoken to Eris. He’d just wanted to see those lights. 

*The mountain wind hissed against his ears, the bitter cold nipping at his nose as he lied back on the grate, staring up at the sky. Four hadn’t understood, but that didn’t matter, in that single moment, before they’d been spotted, everything had felt* right.

But he wasn’t there. He was here—in this empty room—there were no stars here, none that he could reach, Vyntrix and Eris made sure of that… The silence stretched on. His throat tightening by the second, his breathing coming in shaky, discordant, gasps. His heartbeat pounded in his ears, the only sound in this brutal silence. Each thump reinforcing the knowledge that he was speaking only to stone. 

He dug his fingers into the floor, the small rocks and pieces of debris eating into his skin, cutting his hands. “She tests me…” he whispered into the dark, “She is measuring me. That is all this is. Another test. One more test…”

He lifted his head, eyes glazed over as they settled on the light of a dying torch across the room. He stared at it until his eyes burned, the cloudy image of its light danced through his vision as water filled his eyes.

”Then measure me! Break me again… Make me what You wish me to be. *Please*.” He shouted into the flickering light, desperate and pleading.

And the silence droned on.

His breath hitched—half sob, half laugh. “Was it all nothing? Were you ever there—?”

He bit his tongue, desperate to cut off the thought.

The question echoed once, then died away.

Silence.

Appo rose uneasily. His knees protested and his legs wobbled softly under him as he tried to correct his posture. 

”No… you were there. I know you were.” He muttered to himself as he took in the quiet around him, eyes tracing the shadows that lingered on the walls, as though he might find Her hiding amongst them. “I am a Resonant. That is all I will ever be. Resonants do not need affirmation to have faith. They need only to serve.”

Appo bowed deeply, back straight and arms clasped tight against his sides.

”Your order is my law. And law is divine.” 

He finished softly. The words left his mouth hollow, echoing back to him as little more than breath. They did nothing to quench the fire of doubt that burned hot in his chest—a flame he refused to acknowledge. 

He turned and walked from the room. 

But the silence walked with him.

And The Lady did not.
