Auri

The library had always been Auri’s refuge. When the world grew too loud, complicated, or sharp around the edges, she found herself drawn back between the tall shelves and quiet corners as if the place itself knew how to hold her together.

She had claimed her usual nook near the spiral staircase, the small wooden table positioned beside the tall arched windows where soft natural light still lingered in pale ribbons across the floor. An ornate rug lay beneath her boots, its deep patterns worn smooth by years of scholars who had come before her. It was quieter here. Removed. Safe…or as safe as anything felt lately.

Auri turned the page of the heavy tome before her, the motion assisted by the faint shimmer of her mage hand hovering dutifully at her side. Behind her, the soft, nearly imperceptible sounds of books being reshelved marked her unseen servant’s steady work. Efficient, tireless, and perfect for summoning into her study spirals.

One problem at a time. That’s what she kept telling herself. Amberwatch, Twiggle, The Chroma Clash, the cellar, helping Sol and Caspian, the trial. All of it weighed heavily in the back of her thoughts. Her quill paused mid-note. One problem at a time. With a small exhale, Auri reached for her tea, the porcelain cup warm between her fingers. The spoon within stirred lazily of its own accord, clinking softly against the rim before settling again as she took a careful sip.

Then, because she couldn’t help herself, she opened the new book beside her. Her eyes slipped closed for just a moment as she inhaled the familiar scent of new, fresh parchment and ink, something in her shoulders easing despite the weight she carried. When she finally looked back down, her focus sharpened once more, amber eyes bright with quiet determination as her quill resumed its steady scratch across the page.

If more answers existed, she would find them.
