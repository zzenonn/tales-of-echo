The celebration of three new squads of riders stretched into the early hours of ...

The Celstate Saga · Talys Calarel
February 05, 2026 – February 18, 2026
Talys

The celebration of three new squads of riders stretched into the early hours of the next morning. It was a major occasion, the Concord gained new riders only so often. As things wound to a close and almost everyone who remained had either passed out or was getting a head start on cleaning.

Talys was in neither group, his feet kicked up on a table he lounged and was lost in his own thoughts. Usually a rakish grin crossed his face but at this hour when no one was around he let the mask drop ever so slightly. Finally he finished his drink and tossed the empty mug onto the table. Putting his feet down he got up and began to make his way to the stables.

He made it to Biter’s stable and ensured the beast was asleep. After exciting days the wyvern could sometimes delay sleep and create a ruckus that would keep the entire keep awake. Those nights Talys would calm his partner for hours until they both passed out in the hay together. Just like back when he was a feather in the dark.

His check-in complete he made his was to the sleeping quarters. Other nights he might have slept in the hay with Biter instead of the soft bed in the keep. This night he needed that extra comfort, the next day would be the most important of his life. It wouldn’t do to smell like a barn.

As he walked through the twisting stone halls of the Eyrie he whistle to himself. It was late but everyone seems to be too deep asleep to pay him any attention. At an intersection of hallways he was surprised to find his path converged with Calarel.

With a look of surprise he said,

“Fancy running into you at this hour. Usually I’m the only one running around places this late. Can’t sleep before the big ceremony?”

His typical smirk returned to his face like a flash as his typical demeanor asserted itself.

Calarel

Calarel slowed when she reached the intersection, her steps soft against the stone. The Eyrie felt different at this hour. It was quiet in a way that pressed close, the torchlight low and golden, shadows stretching long along the walls. The distant sounds of celebration had faded entirely, leaving only the occasional crackle of flame and the muted hush of sleep. With the anticipation of the coming day weighing on her thoughts, she’d found rest unwilling to claim her. So she’d gone to the stables instead.

Other than the wyverns, she hadn’t expected company.

For a heartbeat, surprise crossed her face before her usual composure slid neatly back into place. She straightened, shoulders relaxed but alert, dark hair loose and spilling down her back in a way that caught the firelight when she moved. Unarmored and out of her fighting leathers, there was something about her like this that felt softer around the edges.

“Talys,” she said calmly as she passed him on her way toward Vaelor’s stall.

Her gaze flicked to his posture, the easy confidence. The way his presence filled the narrow corridor. She didn’t retreat from it. Instead, she shifted just enough to allow him to pass if he chose. When he spoke, she rolled her eyes lightly, the gesture more fond than dismissive.

“Sleep didn’t seem inclined to cooperate,” she replied. “I thought I’d take advantage of the quiet while it still belongs to us.”

Reaching Vaelor, she rested a hand against him, fingers threading through his mane as the massive wyvern lowered his head and settled his chin against her shoulder. Only then did she look back toward Talys.

“And you?” she asked of the tiefling, the corner of her mouth curving faintly. “What’s your excuse for wandering at odd hours?”

Talys

Talys didn’t skip a beat. He immediately redirected to Calarel and joined her by Vaelor’s stall. The intimidating wyvern had little effect on Talys other than the discomfort of his massive size clearly making him feel small. It was easy to deal with any kind of wyvern considering the one who slept not far away.

“My excuse? I don’t think you’d believe me even if I told the truth. Let’s just say I’m always exactly where I’m supposed to be.”

He gave a wink to add to his cryptic comment. Seeing an opportunity to push his luck he added,

“It seems you came to find some company. Do I qualify or should I leave you and Vaelor to yourselves? I promise to be the immaculate gentleman.”

To prove his point Talys crossed his heart with one hand in the childhood symbol of a promise.

In a fluid motion he threw his coat over Biter’s stall and grabbed a nearby stool. Thankfully the cranky wyvern remained as asleep.

Calarel

Calarel watched Talys redirect without hesitation, brows lifting just a fraction as he joined her at Vaelor’s stall. The wyvern shifted, massive head angling toward Talys, a low rumble rolling through his chest. At her touch, he remained settled, eyes half-lidded and unimpressed.

“Bold,” she observed evenly, fingers still buried in the wyvern’s mane. “Most people keep a healthier distance.”

Her gaze slid back to Talys as he spoke, the wink earning him a slow, assessing look. When he crossed his heart, her lips twitched despite herself.

“An immaculate gentleman?” she repeated. “That may be the least convincing promise you’ve made since I’ve known you.”

She shifted then, just enough to lean her hip against the stall rail, arms folding loosely.

“I didn’t come looking for company,” she said calmly. “But I’m not opposed to it.” Her eyes flicked briefly to the coat now draped over Biter’s stall, then back to him, unimpressed but not unamused. “And as for qualifications,” she added, “that depends. Are you capable of sitting still without testing every boundary in reach?”

She paused for a beat. “Because Vaelor and I have very little patience for nonsense,” she finished lightly, though the way her gaze lingered on Tayls suggested she, at least, might have more than she let on.
