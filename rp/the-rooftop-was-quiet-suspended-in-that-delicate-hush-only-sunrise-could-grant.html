Auri

The rooftop was quiet, suspended in that delicate hush only sunrise could grant. The sun hadn’t quite crested the horizon, but its soft gold already stretched over the rooftops and settled against Auri’s shoulders. She moved along the peak with practiced balance, a basket in one hand and a folded blanket under the other. The morning wind tugged at her dark hair as she crossed to the little platform where she’d told Vornakir to meet her.

He had insisted on planning his own birthday party, and as much as she knew he would love that, Auri wanted him to have something else, too.

She spread the blanket, then set the basket in one corner. A cluster of folded paper flowers she’d made herself served as a centerpiece. The waiting gave her mind room to wander.

Auri first noticed Vornakir because he was one of the few students who not only kept pace with her, but often stepped ahead. Their rivalry had become a mix of playful banter and helped push her forward, a rhythm she relied on.

And then, one day, he didn’t show up to class.

She heard that he was injured, but nothing more.
She kept working– and even began completing his assignments– not out of pity, but because she refused to win by default. Respect drove her more fiercely than competition ever had.

Later, he returned. Without an arm. She remembered the hush that fell over the room, how he walked with rigid steadiness, meeting every stare directly. Something shifted in her then, quietly and permanently.
