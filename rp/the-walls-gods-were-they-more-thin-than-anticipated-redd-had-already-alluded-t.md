### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 28, 2025 &middot; 05:30 AM UTC</sub>

The walls, gods were they more thin than anticipated. Redd had already alluded to that fact more than once. He didn't realize how thin until he was about to go grab a glass of water and overheard through his door a brief conversation - No exacts, but he heard voices. One was husky and smooth at the same time, sure enough to be Redd. The other, feminine and strong, intoxicating. Especially now. After the night they'd had, every word seemed even more thrilling to hear coming from her lips. Though it occurred to him, while he expected her to be more public and be more real with him that he had yet to give her the courtesy of the reward for that. And he knew she was still awake, this may be the perfect time. 

And so, he readied everything that was needed to be ready before he slipped out of his room. He closed his door quietly, for being considerate to those sleeping and walked down the hall - Not quite sneaking, but still courteous. He arrived at her door, and gave it his signature knock. Once, then twice and thrice in succession. *Tap, taptap*

When she answered the door, she likely could never have guessed what she'd open it up to. A finely stitched and crafted vest over a nice shirt, clean and well made pants and a freshly bathed Vornakir. He smelled of expensive soaps, his hair was brushed back groomed appropriately, and his demeanor was formal to a degree. A mage hand floated behind him, holding a small basket and his left arm was of course sewn shut on this outfit. 

"Sorry to bother you so late, doll. But I've come to the realization that I've not done you the proper honor of being your boyfriend the right way. I've had you, I've kissed you, I've told people my feelings to some degree. We've made it fairly obvious and fairly official but we haven't done the one that you deserve - A date. So would you, please if you're not so tired, accompany me to a nice spot just outside the dorm so we aren't breaking a curfew and have a meal under the Moon?" He asked in a voice like velvet with a smile that somehow cut the sin it usually portrayed into refined regality.

### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 28, 2025 &middot; 12:43 PM UTC</sub>

When she came to stand in the doorway, not a hair was out of place, even though she knew she would be receiving the one who was the deadliest enemy to her every attempt at composure and the ship that carried the myth of her poise had sailed long ago. She wore her night dress of lavender-hued silver and its matching robe cinched closed over the top. 

The pep talk from Redd (from which she took all the wrong lessons, just as she had from the one she received from Caspian days prior) had chased away the terror of what she'd done. Replaced it with confidence that whatever she might face later, it was worth it. The heartbreak of attempting to conjure sustenance — her *signature*, the very first gift of power Milady had given her — and coming up with nothing was mended well by the praise she received for being so radiant in this made-whole state.

Hand resting on the frame of the door, she leaned there with a casual confidence and an easy smile. Bemused slightly, but still sparkling with a delight rarely seen by anybody but him. "What's the matter? Do you need me to tuck you i—"

The smile fell away slowly when she received the explanation, her brows raising instead. "*Huh.*" Her eyes dropped to the basket held aloft behind him, then back up to him. "And here I thought perhaps romance was dead," she quipped, a lopsided smirk settling in. "I'll get dressed and meet you in the common room," she assured, shooing him away.

When she emerged, her hair was swept into an elegant updo, held in place by pins decorated with golden hurricane lilies. Her dress wasn't her usual style. The skirt was shorter than she normally sported without wearing jodhpurs underneath. 

The asymmetrical waves of the hem lapped just above her knees in front, cascading a little lower around the backs of them. Midnight blue gradually gave way to gold as it crept higher. Sheer sleeves started at her wrists and fastened around her throat, more shades of night sky worn over the gold bodice. The neckline of it plunged (tastefully) toward her waist, which was wrapped in a blacker shade of blue, adorned with gold embroidery to break up the transition of color and emphasize the narrowness of her waist.

She was always dressed stylishly, but not quite like *this*, and *oh*, but she was pleased. And it hadn't taken long at all to get changed. She hadn't had to dither and fret over what she thought might flatter her best or make her look most appealing to his eye. She had no *need* to.

"Sorry to keep you waiting," she murmured low, teasing him. "I had to make sure I looked better than you."

Zašia looked incredible and she knew it.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 28, 2025 &middot; 03:46 PM UTC</sub>

He fixed her with a deadpan stare at first when she began to insinuate he needed tucked in but offered a warm grin to assure her he wasn't upset. "Careful with that sass, Lady." He quipped with a wink before he took a few steps backward and moved back into the common room. 

A better look at him as she entered the room showed his attire wasn't all formal-casual. There was a certain nobility about it; The pressed shirt with what was clearly draconic wing cufflinks, polished buttons and the patterned vest fitted to his somewhat toned chest. Where fabric clung to his skin, the faint outline of scales could be seen pressing against the cloth; By all counts he played the part of a Lord exactly as he ought to. Sleek elegance paired with sophistication and *smolder*. He shifted to turn and look at her, offering nothing short of a smile heavy with a wandering mind. 

"Please, darling, the only way anyone looks better than me is when I'm not in the room." He teased back, in good nature of course, but strode forward to meet her with an extended hand. "But if anyone could eclipse this radiant Sun, it would be you *ilst isilme*. You look absolutely divine." He complimented and if she'd given her hand he would lean down to press a kiss to the top of it. 

"Perhaps on our next date, I'll take you out somewhere populated enough to show you off and make sure every man and woman knows true envy." He all but purred as he willed his mage hand toward the door and began backing his way to it to guide them to their short destination.


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 28, 2025 &middot; 03:57 PM UTC</sub>

Zašia offered her hand out freely when it was reached for, smirking like a cat with a canary. When he finished gracing her skin with a brush of his lips, she cupped his face and stepped in to lay a more forceful claim. Dark mirth shone in her eyes as they danced between his. She'd not gotten used to the new quality of them yet, but *he* was still beneath, no matter the shape of his pupil. Still that knife-sharpness of Vornakir. Her nose brushed against his as she grinned. "Do you promise?"

A scant two weeks ago, she'd all but begged him not to make their relationship public, worried over what people would say or think. Fear of becoming the target of gossip, to have accusations of unworthiness leveled at her, they absolutely paralyzed her.

But now?

"I'll be cross with you if you don't," she warned as they stepped outside.


### &#x270D; Vornakir
<sub>Sep 28, 2025 &middot; 04:06 PM UTC</sub>

His lips curved into a grin as she claimed them and he chased hers for a brief respite in the form of a third kiss, however short it may be. "Gods, you've went from a delightful Merlot to a deep,  Pinot Noir overnight. But no matter the vintage, no matter the label. You're still the finest of wines." He rumbled out to her as their noses rubbed alongside one another. 

"It's a date." He said, adding a little clip of his teeth together in a playful biting motion before he took to leading her outside. 

They were just outside the dorm, as to not get in trouble for breaking any curfew of any sorts - Or at the very least they could scramble inside if need be. The hand gently laid down the basket and set to laying out a nice quilt for them to lay on. The view from their spot was quite nice, an ever expanding sea of stars above with the waning crescent moon above casting a dull silver light all over the grounds. 

"It's short notice, so my apologies - I do have a few tart I've kept from everyone to share, some wine and some sandwiches but I don't have anything luxury. On our next, I'll prepare something far nicer for you."

Zašia let out a low hum, gratified by his small vow and his compliments. She sat herself down once their picnic had been laid out, smiling and pleased. "I don't need luxury," she reminded him. "I'm not dating you for access to that." She very nearly made a quip about simply thieving Caspian's wine stores, but it died on her tongue.

The negative thought that came with that barely qualified as fleeting, dismissed immediately in favor of the marvelous now. "This is lovely," she assured, dropping another little kiss on his cheek and her shoulders wiggled just a little, pleased at this offering he presented her with. "I've been wanting one of those tarts."


### &#x270D; Vornakir

He merely offered her a smile, and then gestured to the quilt once his mage hand had properly laid it out for them. "I don't doubt you in the slightest. I've become pretty decent at detecting lies in people, especially when it's people manipulating me." His nose crinkled a little at the kiss, clear delight apparent on his expression. He liked her displays of affection, no matter how minor they were. 

Once she settled on the quilt, he would join her at her left side so his arm was between them. With a flick of his fingers, the mage hand opened the basket and began pulling out small wrapped plates to lay out before them. Just a couple, it looked like one for each of them being the 'main course' of sandwiches and one being the dessert of tart. And of course, a pair of wine glasses. He produced a bottle of Obsidian Wine (from his homeland) and uncorked it. However, he leaned it against the basket and let it air for a few - He still had some decorum about him. 

"Would you like to start with the tart or save it to delight in after the fairly basic sandwiches I made?"


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Sep 29, 2025 &middot; 05:39 PM UTC</sub>

"I like to save my favorite thing for last," Zašia revealed, breathing in the moment and feeling satisfied. "I want it to be the last taste on my tongue."

She did not wait for some semblance of permission to reach for her sandwich and take a bite. It might seem a small thing, but her standard was to hold and wait for her companion to make that first move. To reach, to take the first bite. It was starting to fell as though perhaps she had confidence in all things.

"There's nothing wrong with basic," she pronounced after she'd indulged. "It's basic for a reason, and it allows us to appreciate things that are finer." That perhaps wasn't an attitude she held in her youth, but she'd never had to settle for basic. She'd learned not to settle for it, not to resent it, but to embrace and appreciate it.

If only that had extended to herself. No matter. There was plenty to love now.

He flashed her that easy grin of his and lifted a brow. "The last taste on your tongue? I'm afraid I'll have to disappoint you, I plan to be the last thing you taste tonight." He teased at her with a swell of confidence she was accustomed to by now from him. 

Nor did he rebuke her reaching first. In fact, it seemed to give him a moment of respite where he'd exhale a soft sigh, likely just happy she was confident enough to not need permission in anything at the moment. 

He took his own plate, unwrapping the simple sandwich he prepared - Hers was the same. Buttered sourdough bread, lightly toasted with honey glazed ham, some fresh very lightly salted tomatoes, and a thin layer of fresh wet lettuce. "Mmh, I guess it was more a commentary on how divine you look and how simple the date is. But I'm not complaining. If anything this is an indicator for me that we're both more than happy to enjoy the simple things." He added, taking a bite of his sandwich and politely chewing it slowly to savor the fruits of his labor.

### &#x270D; Vornakir

He offered a warm, light hearted laugh at her and leaned in to bump his shoulder against hers playfully. It was different seeing him not so strict and tense. “And you say *I’m* filled to the brim with an ego.” He playfully jested, turning to fix her with his stained glass ochre gaze. “Not that I’m complaining. This energy about you, I could get used to it. I love the confidence, and the pride that’s building. It just feels right.” He remarked, taking another bite of his sandwich. 

He leaned back a little and looked skyward to behold the moon, the shimmer of gold in his eyes clashing with the silver of the great star in the sky with no definitive winner. He didn’t turn to face her while he replied this time though, enamored with the pale lady above. “Please, darling, if I wished to have you share a bed again I wouldn’t do something as paltry as having a moonlit picnic. You know how direct I like to be. I would simply tell you to join me, or ask to join you.” He began, a thoughtful lilt of his head causing his black and gold hair flow down his shoulder like a river. Then he finally turned to settle on her again. “Not that I’d oppose it,” he added with that same damned charming grin of his. “But I did this to show you that I’m not just interested in you for sex or physicality or anything like that. I’m interested in *you*.”


### &#x270D; Zasia
<sub>Oct 04, 2025 &middot; 08:18 PM UTC</sub>

And it was different not seeing *her* sort of shrunk in on herself, looking and feeling meek, especially in the face of Vornakir's splendidness. Her shoulder bumped against his and she similarly smiled, a tight-lipped thing that wasn't sour, but like a secret barely kept contained.

"Thank you," she responded to his assessment and compliment. "It feels right to me as well." It felt *incredible.* After Redd spoke his assurances to her, and she finally felt she had his respect, that everything he had found wrong and her had been fixed, she'd been able to shove down the great terror of the *why* she suddenly had all this.

She oughtn't to.

The smile fell away from her face as he iterated his intentions. She blushed faintly, visible even in the moonlight. "That's... sweet," she murmured softly, a little embarrassed. "And unexpected." She hadn't expected this to be more about her beauty or the fact she shared his bed, somehow. Or at least that he wouldn't show it in such a soft manner. Maybe she'd half expected that this wasn't so much about the time they would spend together, this picnic, but more about flouting the curfew rules a little.
