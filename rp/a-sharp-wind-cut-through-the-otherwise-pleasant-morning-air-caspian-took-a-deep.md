### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 23, 2025 &middot; 07:33 PM UTC</sub>

A sharp wind cut through the otherwise pleasant morning air. Caspian took a deep breath as it passed over him. Relishing the chill that followed as it coursed through his body. 

He had gotten ready for training a little slower than usual. The events of sponsorship day still swirled in his head like a vortex. The prophecy, his own past, his argument with his parents, two high courts vying for his future. Even the last two days training hadn’t been able to soothe his racing mind. 

It was Auri’s scheduled morning to train with him so at least some company might prove a good distraction. She was a good student and a fast learner, she was already starting to match his own talents with a blade. Granted her own style was far different from his own. 

They hadn’t gotten along at first by nature of their first meeting. Before they knew they were roommates they had collided on campus while Caspian had turned a corner around the Quasi building and Auri had been hurrying to her next appointment. They both went down in a flurry of books and loose leaf notes. Auri quickly gave Caspian a dressing down on watching where he was walking. 

Caspian remembered rolling his eyes at her anger and continuing on with his day, not realizing that he had snagged her day planner and she had taken his investigation notes. He realized this shortly after and searched all over campus for her only to find her in the last place he looked, his own dorm

He strolled into the dueling practice grounds. Auri sat cross legged on one of the benches off to the side of the field. Her spell book seemed to be open in front of her. Caspian checked the position of the rising sun and realized he was a few minutes late. 

“Sorry I’m late Treble. Hope I didn’t throw off your schedule too much.”

He sat down next to her and peered over the cover of the spell book. He tried to read her notes upside down but it was difficult. 

“What are you working on? Still working on what did you call it? The blade song?”

### &#x270D; Auri

Auri sat cross-legged on the bench, her textbook open and levitating in front of her, a tangle of loose notes scattered around her like a miniature storm. A notebook was balanced on her upper thigh and knee. Every so often, she’d pause to furrow her brow, scribble a correction, and then gesture with her hands as if the spells themselves would follow her directions.

A half-finished granola bar rested beside her notebook, forgotten, and her hand signaled for the pages of the book to turn. The morning sun had just cleared the treetops, casting a warm light across the practice field. She was so absorbed in perfecting the rhythm that she didn’t hear the familiar footsteps approaching until a shadow fell over her notebook.

Auri looked up, a smirk forming as she closed her book with a flick of her fingers. “You think *I’m* trouble? You’re the one derailing my schedule every other day,” she said, tapping her planner with a finger, which now lay neatly beside her notes. “Remember the first time we met? You barreled around that corner, knocked me flat, *and* misplaced my planner. It took me hours to recover from that.”

She put her books into her bag, letting a small laugh escape. “But…I’ll admit, you might throw my schedule into chaos, but you also help me get better, and I can’t argue with the results.” Her smirk softened into something warmer as she picked up her practice blade and stood. “Just…try not to make a habit of being late, okay? We have work to do, and I have a journalism club meeting in an hour.”

With that, Auri moved to the center of the section of the field they used to train. “Alright, let’s see if I’ve improved since our last spar. I’ve been working really hard to be a *blade singer*.”


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 24, 2025 &middot; 07:39 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian smirked at her in return. He never got a good look at her book. It was a little game they had begun to play. He’d try to sneak a peak and she’d slam it in his face. Much like a dog chasing its tail he wouldn’t know what to do with it if he ever did see inside.

“Call you trouble I would never.” He said sarcastically, “You’re not trouble you’re *treble* like the musical notation. Cause you’re a blade singer…”

He paused gesturing at her like he expected her to laugh even though he knew one likely wouldn’t come. 

As she put away her things he shrugged. Not his best attempt at a joke but the nickname was fun so he’d keep it around unless she objected. 

The compliment that followed a few moments later was a surprise. Auri kept very high standards and meeting them had not been easy. It made her a worthy ally against the enemies they’d faced but difficult to impress.

He softened as well to show his appreciation. Caspian appreciated teasing the very serious natured tiefling but didn’t want to ruin a sincere moment. 

“Well thank you for the kind words. Thanks for putting up with me these early mornings. You really have improved dramatically. If you keep training with me and giving me a sparring partner I’ll be sure to improve my punctuality.”

Caspian grabbed his own practice blade from his pack. He swung it a few times to test the balance hadn’t changed. When he was standing across from Auri he gave a respectful salute with the blade and assumed his neutral stance. 

Standing across from her he noticed her focus, the determination. He knew that look, he wore it frequently himself. The others were excellent training partners but their expertise leaned on magic and trained with him more for the athletic benefits. Auri was his first opponent since Celstate who could keep pace with his martial prowess. 

Auri challenged him and he appreciated that. He was learning from their sparring sessions too. He hoped that they wouldn’t come to an end if she felt she had learned all she could from them.

Normally he would’ve waited for her to strike first but he’d worked hard in their previous sparring matches to condition her that he played defense first. This time he struck first with a blinding flurry of blows to take her off guard. 

“Alright so you’re a blade singer… let’s see how well you can dance!” He shouted over the rush of air as his blade sliced the space in between them.  

The blade was directed at her midsection attempting to threaten her with an early debilitating blow and put her on defense until she either lost or turned the tables against him.


### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Nov 25, 2025 &middot; 05:02 AM UTC</sub>

Auri barely had time to roll her eyes at the “treble” joke before Caspian moved. The flurry of strikes came faster than his usual openings. It was deliberate, meant to throw her off. Her eyes widened for half a heartbeat before her training snapped into place. 

“Oh, so *now* you’re early,” she shot back, stepping into motion.

Her foot slid back into a graceful sweep, her blade hovering next to her and humming through her like a plucked string. Instead of meeting his strike head-on, she pivoted sharply, the movement fluid as water.

Caspian’s blade sliced through the space where she’d been. She turned on her heel as she moved his momentum against him. Her blade flashed upward, catching his strike and guiding it aside with surprising finesse. 

After her blade returned to her hand, she shifted smoothly into the stance Sir Stahbd, the animated armor, had taught her in the tower, but it was Caspian who helped her fuse magic and martial prowess into one. 

“If you’re going to open with a dramatic line, make sure you’re ready to keep up,” she teased, eyes glittering with challenge.


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 25, 2025 &middot; 04:49 PM UTC</sub>

Fighting someone whose sword didn’t have to be wielded in hand was an interesting challenge. Caspian still hadn’t ironed out his defense against it. Auri could attack at angles that Caspian simply couldn’t manage at times. Now he was learning that it made her defense incredibly flexible. 

“When have I not been able to keep up with you Treble? You’re the one who has a schedule to keep, I could do this all day.” He finished with a smirk that showed he meant every word. 

Being able to guide the blade independent of her body would strain his mind to track but for someone as organized as her it seemed child’s play. He noticed a flaw though. 

He sent a few weak strikes to probe and confirm his theory. On the second he noticed the problem again. It was hard to notice, it wasn’t a weakness as much as something that seemed to be holding her back.

True to the name of the style the blade seemed to hum and vibrate creating melodic tones as if it sang. Especially when it moved independently he could notice the musical nature. 

Auri however was out of sync. Were it not for a tremendous amount of practice she wouldn’t have made up for the desynchronized movements. She was fighting but maybe she should’ve been dancing. 

He set up a trap to catch her in this strange pattern. He sent attacks exactly in time with the rhythm of the blade. Auri who was out synch wouldn’t be able to match the attacks with her footwork and counterstrikes. 

If it worked as planned he would be able to pierce her guard and land a blow to her shoulder. He made sure to give her a chance to find her own mistake first.

“For someone who cares a lot about time you seem to be missing something today. If you don’t figure it out I’m going to beat you.”

He sprung his trap changing his pace suddenly to match the sword and causing a stutter step in his movements. Auri would either find the rhythm and save herself or have quite the bruise.


### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Nov 26, 2025 &middot; 07:27 PM UTC</sub>

Auri felt the shift a heartbeat too late. 

Of course he’d noticed. Caspian *always* noticed. He was an excellent swordsman and teacher.

She’d been focused on the angles, the reach, the precision of her blade’s path…but not its song. The thin silver weapon shimmered like a tuning fork and struck mid-note, humming its own rhythm, and she’d been so intent on controlling it yet again that she’d forgotten to *dance* with it.

A rookie mistake, and absolutely the kind of thing he’d tease her about if he landed that hit. 

She exhaled, letting her weight drop just enough to catch the first strike–  and then his tempo changed. 

Her eyes widened. *Clever*. Infuriatingly so.

“I hate when you’re observant,” she muttered, breathless, boots scraping against the packed earth as she scrambled to realign her stance. Her tail lashed behind her– annoyance that he’d figured her out this quickly.

He pressed exactly where she was weakest, matching each swing to the hum of her blade. Her footwork faltered for half a second, and she knew:

*He’s going to land it.*

In that sliver of time, memory flooded her. Caspian’s reminders to *listen*. To *feel* the rhythm. Wispyr laughing with her in Artorius’ tower, spun around by a gust of spectral wind as music played in the background. Tiny Twiggle weaving between their ankles, rustling between their feet. 

If her blade was a song, she only had to move in time with the music. 

Auri pivoted sharply, letting the song pull her– even if she caught it a heartbeat late. Caspian’s strike turned from what would’ve been a clean blow to a lancing sting across her shoulder instead. Enough to bruise, but not enough to finish her off like the intended blow.

Still, the impact made her hiss through her teeth. She sent him a look with equal parts venom and admiration.

She rubbed her shoulder, then flicked her fingers.

The hovering blade responded instantly, its hum sharpening, the melody syncing perfectly with the rise of her breath and the placement of her feet. She eased into the stance Sir Stahbd once called “the opening step,” her silhouette poised, muscles coiled, tail arched in perfect balance. 

She smirked, sharp and challenging.

“Your move.”


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 27, 2025 &middot; 01:16 AM UTC</sub>

Caspian felt the thrill of success course through his body as his strike seemed as if it would land a crucial blow in their sparring match. To the fighter a sparring match felt more normal than sitting around. He could block out the complexities of real life and focus on one thing, landing a blow before his opponent did. For a brief moment he knew his victory was assured, it was too late for her to get away from him until... she did?

The humming and her last movement, they were perfectly synchronized. The elation was replaced by frustration for just a moment as he felt his attack glance off of her, still a solid strike but no longer a finishing blow. Then something clicked, the way she had glided away was synchronized. Every instinct he had told him that if she could remain synchronized then they would be evenly matched through and through. Many would be jealous or upset at the notion of their pupil catching up to them in skill. For Caspian however it was electric, so exciting he could feel himself vibrating at the prospect. 

"Now that was really great. You finally found the pulse of your magic. You've learned the steps, your stances. Now chain them together, feel the hum of your blade the rhythm of the battlefield. Remember you have to dance, you can lead but you still have a partner. Don't overpower them, work together, you have to *feel* it."

A wide grin spread across his face as she challenged him. He took the opportunity and began his own dance. He didn't often spar with this style, it was saved for real combat. If Auri was about to take this next step he couldn't hold back. He didn't have a bladesong but that didn't mean he couldn't find the rhythm. He could feel a different rhythm, the beating of his heart the blood rushing through his body. He followed that rhythm using all that he had learned from his teachers to **fight**.

"I'll be honest Auri, I've sparred with the others and they are tremendous individuals but they have other talents. Magic is a potent tool in their hands but they don't understand what it means to fight. You know what it means to struggle though. To fight for things, to get knocked down so many times you forget which way is up but you still push yourself when you don't think you have anything left. We have that in common I think. Let's see who will stand on top when this is over."

Slashes rained down on Auri from all directions. Caspian was a flurry of different stances and movement. He would not give her pause to think or react, only to react. She would sink or swim right here or right now. Auri needed to find her dance or she would be heading to class with a lot more bruises and a really bad headache.


### &#x270D; Auri

Auri’s mind raced as Caspian’s strikes came faster than ever, each one a blur of steel and motion. For a heartbeat, panic flared– her body remembered the old days, when she had hesitated to even attempt her blade song. She had been reluctant to try to combine dance and swordwork, but she had trusted him enough to try. To allow him to watch her fumble through plenty of mistakes. Slowly, painfully, the steps had begun to make sense. The hum of the blade, the rhythm in her feet, the subtle shift in her stance– it had clicked bit by bit. And now, here she was, standing against him on equal footing.

She inhaled sharply, centering herself. She let the flurry of Caspian’s strikes slide into the rhythm she already knew. She didn’t fight him with brute force. Instead, she danced, letting her blade sing and twist through the air in perfect harmony with her steps.

Each strike he threw came at her like waves, but she bent, pivoted, and slipped under them, her weapon humming in tune with hers. Her footwork was confident, precise, fluid. She remembered Caspian’s encouragement, remembered his grin as she had finally let herself flow with the music. *I can do this.*

And then she saw the opening: a narrow gap in his guard. With a swift, elegant step, she spun, letting her blade cut across his midsection in a controlled tap. Another pivot, and she slashed again with a final strike, each motion a note in a song only she could hear. The wind from her movements stirred her hair, and the blade sang in harmony with her heartbeat. She landed lightly, feet steady, tail flicking for balance, a triumphant smile tugging at her lips.

“Try to keep up, Maestro,” she said, voice teasing, letting the tip of her blade hover just out of his reach.


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 28, 2025 &middot; 06:07 PM UTC</sub>

Watching Auri move was truly impressive. The magic she wove through her steps gave her near supernatural speed and awareness. Caspian saw attack after attack that should have ended things parried and dodged in the nick of time. 

Across the training field they used every inch of space in their flurry of combat. They were locked in a strange pattern of offense and defense. Were anyone there to observe they would’ve sworn the two had planned the fight like choreography.

It felt like an eternity to Caspian, the blood rushing in his ears. There was no room for thought, only action. Eventually though the trance of battle shattered as he saw his mistake, his blade overcommitted to an attack. 

He couldn’t react by the time he felt her counter slash land hard on his ribs. It knocked the wind out of him and was already fumbling to recover when the second strike landed.

Caspian’s least favorite feeling in the world occurred a moment later. His back hitting the sand of a practice arena. He exhaled sharply and started laughing from the ground. 

It wasn’t a mocking laugh but one of someone who had a great time and was enjoying the moments after the thrill. After a minute he slowly pushed himself off the ground and to his feet.

“I yield, I yield. Now that was amazing Auri! I can’t remember the last time I had a match that exciting. You are a one in a million talent let me tell you.”

He approached her and tapped her on either shoulder with the flat of his blade.

“Hereby you have graduated from my tutelage. From here on out we are equals.”

He then promptly went and downed the contents of his water skin. He sat down and patted the spot next to him.

“Still got time for a chat before your next appointment?”

### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Nov 29, 2025 &middot; 09:58 PM UTC</sub>

He leaned back as she sat down. Making sure to stretch out and let his body cool down without getting sore. Though a few of Auri’s hits already had started to ache.

“I guess you got me there. There’s just been a lot going on since you moved in and it’s been a lot of business and training. We haven’t really gotten to know each other as well as maybe we should’ve.”

He stretched his arms and then rested them behind his head. Caspian wasn’t typically nervous but it was clear he’d given Auri some space over the last few weeks. He’d worried that maybe after their poor start to their living arrangement she’d held a bit of a grudge. 

“I know it’s irrational but I’ve kinda felt like maybe you weren’t a fan of me since you moved in. That’s why I jumped when you wanted to practice this stuff. I’m not great at reading people and you’re pretty challenging to get a bead on anyways.”

Not used to talking for so long Caspian kept fidgeting. A scratch at his temple, adjusting  the seam of his shirt. 

“The sparring matches helped a bit but, with everything going on I just wanted you to know that I consider you a friend. Someone I trust a lot. Not just cause you’re a great sparring partner but because I respect how hard you work for everything.”

He passed her another water skin so she could get a bit of water in her system. When she took it he wouldn’t let go initially made sure she met his eyes for the next part.

“I know my circumstances growing up were closer to the others but I had to fight hard to learn what I know and I feel like we have that in common. You’ll get twice as far as any of those rich assholes if you keep up what you’ve been developing.”


### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Dec 02, 2025 &middot; 08:43 PM UTC</sub>

Auri blinked, surprised by the sudden sincerity. Then her expression softened, her smile warming her whole face. She took the waterskin from him fully this time, took a sip, and handed it back. 

“Caspian…I wouldn’t be here if I didn’t trust you.”
She nudged his shoulder lightly with her own, but this time the touch had weight to it, grounding instead of teasing. 

“Did you *count* how many times I stepped on your feet, fell on my face, and otherwise made a fool out of myself during all this training?” She flopped backward into the dirt with a groan, hands covering her face and laughed. “Seriously! I knew I was bad.”
 
When she propped herself up on her elbows again. “But I let you watch me mess up and be vulnerable because I *trust* you. I wouldn’t do that with just anyone."

She sat up fully and tapped his chest, a gentle but deliberate insistence that he pay attention. “And about earlier…what you said?”

Her voice softened, and something honest settled over her features. “You’re wrong about me not being a fan of you.”

“I admire you, Cas. You’re loyal, friendly, surprisingly grounded despite how goofy you are sometimes, and you really care about people. You barely knew me, and you certainly didn’t have to help me train, but you did.”

Before the weight of that could settle too heavily between them, Auri leaned in and tapped her fingers lightly to the center of his forehead. Just a soft, playful little boop.

“Got it now?”

Then she stood, brushing dirt from her clothes, and offered him a small, warm smile.

 “I’m really glad you’re my friend, Caspian.”
