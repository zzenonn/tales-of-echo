### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 12, 2026 &middot; 02:24 AM UTC</sub>

Talys stands and stares at a war map he’s set up in the main chamber of Granveil Tower. Several large and small figurines represented the converging Saintillian forces. Four divisions of the Golden Shield contained more than enough soldiers and siege weaponry to crush the city thrice over. Over top of the city was a small division of figurines representing the Cobalt Guard, one a wyvern to represent the forces of the Concord. He drew up different arrangements and engagements with the figurines. Calculating the losses and results of each strategy each time Talys knocked over the blue pieces. Their odds did not look good.   

Talys had woken up quite early, sleep didn’t come easy when death loomed so heavily. After a year he still dressed well, ensuring that his clothes were well pressed and neat. The ribbon armband still tied on his forearm now had fourteen tally marks. In the last year he’d picked up smoking, a small hand rolled cigarette hung from his lips trailing a small stream of smoke. It was a bad habit but spend enough time gambling in your free time and you pick it up from the others at the table. 

He checked the time by the position of the sun and stepped away. It was time for the appointed lesson in sign language. A lesson that to him seemed to only solve half the problem. He'd exacerbated the tension between him and Aurelio for certain but that didn't excuse the insults he received from Aurelio regularly. This lesson would need to be a two way street. For now he’d see where this went, What Talys lacked in leadership qualities he made up for as a competent tactician. Without trust in his plans it made him more or less irrelevant. Worse, if only some of them trusted the plans and others didn't, that was where the real danger lay in wait. All it took for a squad acting in discord to get all of them killed or worse, captured. 

Either way he stepped away from the table, resetting it to the current state of things. He took a long drag on his smoke before dropping it and putting it out with the toe of his boot. Stopping only to grab a piece of fruit for breakfast in the kitchen, he made his way to the balcony Aurelio had told him to meet on. 

The sounds of the city greeted him outdoors. The sounds of life that would soon be interrupted and replaced by the silent terror of a people slowly evacuating. The longer the evacuation delayed the less would make it out of the city and far enough away to survive. Once again the poor and the underprivileged would be left behind to serve the benefit of the rich and the powerful. 

No time to focus on that, the tiefling pulled a notebook from his pocket, not his spell book but similar. Inside were some preliminary notes and observations about Aurelio’s type of sign language. Talys had scribbled these down last night before sleep to make sure he had somewhere to start. 

Leaning against the balcony railing he lit another cigarette even though he had just finished one. He waited silently, trying to lock into his memory Cindros as it was at this moment. For even if they did survive and save the city by some miracle it would never be the same.

### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 16, 2026 &middot; 12:12 AM UTC</sub>

Aurelio had been awake for hours. It had become unspoken knowledge within the group that rising with the sun was as natural to him as breathing. By the time dawn’s golden rays had burst over the horizon, he had already eaten a light breakfast, stretched, and centered his mind with some meditation. Now, he was perched on the thick branch of a tree that grew close to the balcony he’d designated for Talys’ lesson.

A gentle breeze brushed past him, carrying the warm scent of freshly baked pastries and blooming flowers from the city below. His gaze lifted as Pollux cut through the air overhead, wings spread wide, and eyes narrowed as he hunted. As he watched the world wake, for a moment, Aurelio pretended he was back home, high in the sky and wrapped in the clouds.

His eyes drifted back to the city, and heavy concern settled in. Innocent people moved through their morning routines, unaware of the dangerous, golden storm gathering around them. There were no mass evacuations, no panic, and no visible preparations. Someone had decided silence was preferable to chaos, and Aurelio wasn’t convinced that this choice wouldn’t cost thousands of lives.

A flicker of movement on the balcony below drew his attention. There was Talys, cigarette in hand. Aurelio’s nose wrinkled immediately as the sharp and acrid scent cut through the clean morning air like a blade.

With a soft rustle of leaves, Aurelio dropped from the branch and landed softly behind Talys without barely making a sound. A few seconds later, Castor padded out from inside and took a seat beside him, an attentive look on his face.

Aurelio lifted his hands, and with smooth, deliberate movements, his fingers curved and swept in arcs, wrists turning as if tracing the smoke in the air. Castor looked between them, then translated.

*“You know smoking is bad for you, right?”* Aurelio’s hands continued, expression tightening slightly. *“And not just physically, you’re damaging your ‘pneuma’.”* Castor tilted his head and added, “It’s an Ouranian term. The closest translation is ‘the essence of life,’ which they believe is primarily held in the lungs.”*

As if on cue, Pollux swooped down and landed on Aurelio’s shoulder, fixing Talys with a long, unimpressed stare. Aurelio glanced at Pollux, then back to Talys, with an unimpressed expression matching the bird’s. He did another series of signs, and Castor translated again.

*“Before we start, I have rules.”* Aurelio raised one finger. *“Rule one: no magic. Castor can translate for you in the beginning, but ‘Comprehend Languages’ is strictly off-limits.”

Aurelio raised a second finger. *“Rule two: leave your ego and whatever other baggage you’re carrying on the ground. Learning works best in a free and positive space.”*

He lifted his last finger. *“Rule three: No quitting, no matter how frustrated or exhausted you are; you’ll meet me here every morning for our lessons without complaining. Ouranian Sign Language is an invention of my people and hasn’t spread outside my homeland. As a result, many words don’t have a Common translation, and some concepts can’t be translated at all. It’s going to be confusing and extremely frustrating for someone not from the Domain, so I need you to promise me you won’t quit and waste my time.”*

Aurelio’s hands slowed. *“If you promise to try,”* Castor finished, *“Aurelio says he promises to guide you through it.”*

Aurelio studied Talys for a moment, then signed again.

*“And because I believe teaching goes both ways,”* Aurelio’s eyes narrowed slightly, *“do you have any rules for me?”*


### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 17, 2026 &middot; 01:35 AM UTC</sub>

Talys let the smoke fill his lungs for a moment. The comforting buzz of the tobacco filling his body. Aurelio’s warning via Castor passed through the air. Exhaling the smoke through his nose he turned to face his squad mate. 

“Well good morning to you too I guess… “

Talys grumbled at the harsh greeting. The harsh words unprompted but slung at him nonetheless.

“Maybe for most folks it’s bad. Maybe it’s bad for me but…”

He taps lightly at his horns and by extent his crimson complexion and tiefling heritage.

“A bit of brimstone seems to run in the family so I’m not sure your ‘pneuma’ works the same for folks like me. But maybe that’s just my ignorance on the matter. Either way let’s agree that judging my habits is not what we’re here to do is it?”

Leaning back against the railing Talys took the rules impassively. He waited patiently for the signs and translation to finish. 

Talys carefully memorized different signs and their corresponding translations. For him the lesson had begun already.

“If you’re offering rules I do have a few of my own. First is you need to follow your own second rule. You want a free and open space when you’ve already decided all the ways in which I’ll fail. That I’d quit, that I’d use magical comprehension.”

He took a bite of his breakfast and while he was chewing added,

“You’ve judged me based on what? My attitude? You’ve never bothered to learn a thing about me, you wrote me off as soon as I didn’t act exactly as you expect someone too. Remember your first words to me this morning were to judge my smokes.”

With that he stomped out the now stubby cigarette with his boot. He held up a finger.

“Which leads me to my other rule. My only other rule is that I’m not the only one learning something. This is not somehow my fault, we both antagonized each other. So I can teach you plenty but this has to be reciprocal, whatever you want to learn from me is your choice.”

Talys leaned back on the railing, his proposal hanging in the air. He waited patiently to see his would be tutor’s response to his concerns.


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 17, 2026 &middot; 05:31 PM UTC</sub>

Aurelio pinched the bridge of his nose and exhaled slowly, shoulders rising and falling steadily, clearly trying to hold his tongue.

Pollux lifted off his shoulder with a sharp flick of his wings and glided over to the railing, talons clicking softly as he perched a few inches away from Talys. The falcon tilted his head, studying the tiefling with assessing eyes.

Aurelio’s hands came up again, the movements now tighter and sharper. Castor shifted closer to Aurelio, ready to translate.

*“He says he hasn’t decided how you’ll fail; at all,”* Castor translated. *“He said he also never mentioned you would quit and use magic; he said it was very likely you may want to. He said he was simply setting expectations, and anything outside of that is your misunderstanding.”*

Aurelio rolled his eyes, and his fingers sliced through the air in a quick, irritated motion before flowing into the next sequence.

*“Getting to know each other is a two-way street, you’re right about that, but you haven’t tried to know me either. None of the squadmates have.”*

His hands slow, the change in pace visibly distinctive from how he’d been signing.

*“Since I’ve been here, nobody has cared enough to learn my language, and this is the most alone I’ve ever felt.”* Castor's voice dropped to a softer tone. *“I’m living in a foreign land, with cultures and traditions I’m not used to, and it’s been confusing and exhausting to deal with that on top of being unable to communicate. So the Concord assigned me a translator, but even Castor can’t always convey everything I’m saying correctly. Ouranian sign depends heavily on hand shape, speed, and motion to express tone, and unfortunately, some of that gets lost in translation.”*

Aurelio reached out and gave Castor’s head a few gentle scratches. *“He said he knows I try, and he appreciates my help, but he said it’s not the same as talking to him directly.”* Castor said as he happily wagged his tail.

Aurelio’s gaze drifted away for a few seconds, then returned to Talys as he continued. *“In that solitude, I had to learn about you all from a distance using my observation skills.”* 

Aurelio sharply flicked his wrist as he made the smoking sign again. *“He said he wasn’t judging you for smoking, he just made an observation and gave a warning because he’s seen what smoking does to lungs.”* Castor scratched behind his ear as he finished translating. *”He said if you’d rather he keep those warnings to himself, he will gladly stop talking.”*

Aurelio’s hands lowered as he dropped to sit on the floor, crossing his legs. Then his hands rose again, and Castor began speaking again.

*“I never said our dynamic is your fault, and I don’t understand why you keep putting words in my mouth or deciding how I feel about you. My feelings for you are not a simple matter, and they are more complex than you probably believe.”* 

There was a pause, and when Castor translated the next part, Aurelio wasn’t looking at Talys; his eyes were on the city.

*“He said he’s tired of arguing.”* Finally, Aurelio looked back up, and he made eye contact with Talys. *“If you want me to learn from you,”* Castor said, *“then you’ll teach me about who you really are, since apparently your perceived thoughts of my image of your character bothers you.”*

Aurelio’s final hand signs were swift and structured, clearly meaning he wanted to end the discussion and move on.

*“Are these terms to your satisfaction?”*


### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 17, 2026 &middot; 06:53 PM UTC</sub>

Talys sat with Aurelio’s response silently for a minute. He could see the man was trying to meet him in the middle. Good that was what he wanted. 

Talys attempted to illustrate the same by conveying his agreement in rudimentary sign language. He’d identified the sign for “I” rather easily. “Agree” had been more difficult but he had recalled the common sign Aurelio had typically given after a superior officer asked him a question. In a literal sense he signed something closer to,
“I understand”.

He paused afterwards to see if he had mangled the response. He was confident he mimicked the signs well, years of somatic components had made his hands precise and accurate. The vocabulary and grammar would be his biggest challenge. 

“Alright I can teach you about myself. That seems a fair trade, we both learn about each other in different senses of the concept.”

He sat down and took another bite of his breakfast and he caught Aurelio eyeing the city from their vantage point.

“First lesson about me, I lived here most of my life. Was born here, grew up here. Well not here exactly.”

He points off towards the city walls at a section of town that contained many trails of grey and black smoke into the air. No grand structures of Celian architecture stood there. Just a set of rundown slums filled with furnaces to process runesteel.

“Just me and my Ma. Barely a couple coppers to our name. It was a real shit hole, folks working day and night and barely feeding their families. Ma did her best but sometimes it just wasn’t enough.”

He looked over to his former home. There was no sense of nostalgia in his eyes, only a battle between sympathy and contempt. 

“Now everyone there is likely to die. No one there has the money to travel, ‘cept the people who own the furnaces and the ironworks. They’ll run off to the next town and wait it all out. The rest will just have to decide, die here in their homes or starve out on the road with no work and no food.”

He tore his eyes away from the city and looked at Aurelio. No smirk, no ego, just stating the facts and trying to not picture the faces and names of everyone he knew there.

“Anyways I don’t mean to delay. Your lesson should’ve come first. I apologize.”


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 18, 2026 &middot; 01:55 PM UTC</sub>

Talys’ attempt at signing brought an immediate reaction to Aurelio’s face; his lips twitched, it was somewhere between a quiet smile and a laugh. He shook his head and raised his hands to sign.

Castor glanced at Talys, then translated. *“Close, but you just told me that you don’t understand.”* Aurelio shifted closer, taking Talys’ hand into his; it was firm and deliberate and lacked any form of patronizing.

Aurelio curled Talys’ fingers into a loose fist, then lifted the index finger straight up, while slowly guiding Talys’ hand toward the crown of his own head. Then Aurelio mirrored the motion with his own hand, index finger rising like a small exclamation point.

Castor nodded, sitting up straight, and speaking with the cadence of a teacher. *“Hand direction matters; for ‘I understand,’ your curled fingers face towards you. If they face outward, it becomes ‘I don’t understand.’ Same shape, but much different meanings.”*

Aurelio released Talys’ hand and gave him a small approving nod anyway; he saw that he tried, and the effort meant something to him.

While Talys spoke about his home, Aurelio listened without any interruptions or commentary. There was only a gradual tightening around his eyes as Talys pointed toward the smoking district; Aurelio guessed it was the city's poorest district. Aurelio had assumed many things about Talys, but not that he hailed from the slums of a city. He had always assumed Talys was a noble due to his clothing and egotistical nature. 

The story settled into Aurelio like a slow, unwelcome weight; he had grown up in grand, open halls and sunlit courtyards, where hunger was a concept only taught in school lessons, not a sensation that gnawed at his belly. In the Domain, if someone lacked food, clothing, or shelter, his fathers intervened; they created programs and task forces whose sole purpose was to ensure that no one fell through the cracks.

Making others suffer for personal profit was a foreign, almost incomprehensible concept to him because he was raised to believe the suffering of any of your subjects was a failure of your leadership skills.

When Talys finished, Aurelio moved his hands more slowly and heavily than before. Castor’s voice softened.

*“How has your mother been doing, especially after you’ve been gone for so long?”*

Aurelio shifted his hands again, transitioning smoothly into a demonstration. He held up his dominant hand in an open five shape, then tapped his thumb gently against his chin twice.

Castor elaborated. *“Mother.”*

Then Aurelio repeated the same open hand shape, but this time tapped his thumb twice at his throat.

*“Father.”*

Aurelio watched Talys carefully to see if he understood what he said or if he needed to demonstrate again. Then he resumed signing in response to Talys’ story.

*“He says surely the city will find a way to evacuate people, and if they don’t, we will.”* Pollux gave a soft chirrup of agreement from the railing, feathers ruffling. *“Even if we have to guide them out one by one, we’ll get them to safety.”*

Aurelio demonstrated more words in sign. He made two fists crossed at the wrists in an X, palms facing inward, then he moved both hands outward.

*“Safe.”*

Then he formed a closed fist with one hand, palm sideways, and swept his other hand in a slow circle over it, fingers spread, settling atop the fist.

*“Whole.”*

Castor smiled, tongue draped out of his mouth.

*“Safe and sound.”*

Aurelio’s gaze returned to Talys, and he signed one last time.

*“You don’t need to apologize for sharing your background.”* A small, genuine smile tugged at the corner of his mouth. *“I learned something new about you today, which means our arrangement is working.”*

With those comments past he focused greatly on replicating each sign and teaching himself the syntax used by this particular style of sign language. He was still rough but he could convey rudimentary phrases or ideas at the level one might expect from a toddler. 

“Anyways maybe we focus on some combat related phrases Aurelio. We’ll need them for when Castor can’t just tag along. My spell to decipher languages will have to work in the field while I learn.”

Talys finished off his breakfast and used a mage hand to toss the remnants in a nearby trash bin. 

Overall the Tiefling was far more agreeable at this point. His cockiness and attitude had returned but no longer they so aggressively pushed to the forefront. Overall it was a more relaxed version of the same man.


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Mar 06, 2026 &middot; 07:09 PM UTC</sub>

Aurelio watched with a smile as Talys worked hard to learn and correct his signing mistakes; the tiefling’s precision was clear to Aurelio, and his years of somatic spellcasting undoubtedly gave him excellent hand control, but Aurelio could tell grammar was still fighting him.

Aurelio noticed Talys skip over the sign for father, and the sour expression spread across his face. He didn’t say anything about it, not wanting to start drama, so instead he tucked the observation away for a later conversation. Some things were better left untouched until someone offered the information willingly.

At the mention of Talys’ mother, however, Aurelio’s expression softened. He lifted his hands, and Castor watched and translated.

*“My fathers write to me weekly; they fill the pages to the brim with the latest updates about the Domain: court gossip, news about my siblings, the newest Skybond pairings.”* Castor flicked his tail. *“Apparently royal households run on gossip as much as politics.”*

Aurelio gave Castor an unimpressed look at the added commentary while Pollux shifted on the railing like the joke had personally offended him.

Aurelio lowered his hands as Talys finished speaking about the evacuation. He gazed once more toward the city below, and he agreed that time wasn’t exactly a luxury they could afford right now. Aurelio didn’t linger on that thought long as Talys shifted the lesson back on track.

At the suggestion of combat phrases, his posture straightened. Aerial combat, now *that* was a language Aurelio spoke fluently. 

His hands came up again, and he began demonstrating slowly, since combat signs were a bit more complex than common day signs. Aurelio began by drawing both hands upward; his right hand stayed close to his body with the forefinger extended outward, pointing away from him like a blade, and his left hand mirrored the shape but faced the opposite direction, its forefinger pointing toward the right. Then he slightly pulled both hands apart and held them there for a moment, fingers tense.

Castor nodded. *“Enemy.”*

Next, Aurelio moved on to demonstrating directional orientation. He raised one hand and traced a vertical circle in the air, like he was drawing a clock.

Castor clarified what Aurelio was doing. *“Aurelio uses a clock system during aerial combat, kind of like a compass.”* Aurelio pointed upward in the air. *“North is twelve o’clock.”* Then to the right. *“East. Three.”* Down. *“South. Six.”* Left. *“And west is nine.”*

Pollux hopped along the railing, lining his body up to wherever Aurelio pointed, trying to help Talys visualize everything.

Then Aurelio combined the two concepts, signing 'enemy' followed by pointing upward.

*“Enemy at twelve o’clock,”* Castor said while wagging his tail.

Next, Aurelio explained horizontal positioning. He formed both hands into “A” shapes (fists with thumbs up). He placed them in front of himself, one to each side. Then the right hand moved around the left, curving back toward himself while the left remained stationary.

Castor translated. *“Behind.”*

Then Aurelio demonstrated the exact reverse motion. His right hand moved from behind the left hand and forward past it.

*“In front.”*

Aurelio repeated the entire sequence from ‘enemy’ to ‘in front’ again as Pollux leaned forward on the railing as if he were supervising. When Aurelio finished, he lowered his hands and looked directly at Talys. His expression was calm but expectant, carrying the implication that he was signaling to Talys that it was his turn to replicate what he had taught him.
