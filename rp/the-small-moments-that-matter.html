Sol/Sunni

The dorm was quiet, save for the soft shuffle of paper and the faint clink of glass jars. Late afternoon sunlight slanted through the dorm windows, painting long bars of gold across the floor. Dust motes drifted lazily in the air, catching in the glow. Sol knelt by his bed, sleeves rolled and hair tied loosely back as he worked through what looked like the most serious mission of the week: packing.

Sol was surrounded by half-open drawers, unrolled ribbons, and a precarious pile of paints, brushes, and food that looked like it might stage a rebellion if left unattended. His hands shimmered faintly with golden light as objects floated in slow orbit around him.

A loaf of bread bobbed toward a wicker picnic basket lined in red-checked cloth, followed by neatly wrapped sandwiches, slices of fruit, and jars of jam. A wedge of cheese drifted uncertainly before he frowned at it. “No… Caspian’ll eat half the basket anyway, better double that.”

With another wave of his hand, two more sandwiches and an additional cheese wheel obediently floated in.

Across from him, a larger satchel waited, already stuffed with what looked like the supply closet of an entire art school. Brushes, palettes, watercolor tins, and neatly labeled acrylic paints nestled beside rolls of parchment, sketchbooks, ribbons, yarn, and a small container of glitter that shimmered faintly, as if it knew it was trouble.

“Okay…” Sol muttered, ticking off items on his fingers. “Food, art supplies, possible chaos. Perfect.”

He snapped his fingers, sending the last few items drifting neatly into their bags, except for a pear, which bonked him on the head mid-flight.

He blinked, rubbing the spot with a mock glare. “Excuse you. That’s assault.” Then, with a laugh, he plucked the offending fruit from the air and tucked it safely into the basket.

He started to rise, brushing off his pants, and then he froze. “…The coffee and tea.”

Spinning on his heel, he nearly tripped as he lunged for tins of black tea and espresso on the nightstand. He caught it with a triumphant little noise, holding it aloft like a prize. “You thought you could escape, didn’t you?”

He set it carefully on top of the packed basket, surveying his work with the sort of proud smile that came from pure, hard-earned relief. Everything was ready now: sandwiches, fruit, drinks, a blanket folded neatly at the bottom, and an artist’s worth of supplies in the satchel. Enough for all five of them, plus seconds for anyone who was still hungry.

“Alright,” Sol murmured, brushing a lock of hair from his eyes. “Snacks for five, art for ten, and hopefully no explosions.”

He lifted the basket into the crook of his arm, slung the satchel over his shoulder, and took one last look at the golden light flooding his room. For a fleeting moment, it felt peaceful, almost normal.

He stepped into the hall, calling brightly toward the common room,
“Alright, everyone! I’m declaring a mandatory break before any of us lose what’s left of our sanity! I’ve got food, drinks, paints, and glitter, and I’m not afraid to use them!

His voice carried a laugh at the end, half challenge, half invitation, before he took a seat on the couch.

Vornakir

As was tradition when in regard to the draconic dynasty of the Vanori, Vornakir was more often than not the first to present himself when summons were given or presence was requested. Today was no different as the sound of two locks clicking open sounded off from the first door on the right down the hall, and out walked the sovereign himself. Much like his companion doing the summons, his hair was uncharacteristically messily tied back in a lazy half bun. On average, he presented himself with full pride and groomed effects such as pressed clothing pristine of soiling, and his hair brushed and combed out, perfectly lush for the sassiest of hair flips when he needed to punctuate a statement. It was custom, according to him, to always be seen at his best.

However as the days dragged on and on, he began to tame the metaphoric beast of his pride and allowed his friends to see a more real side of him. Such as today. A simple black shirt trimmed with golden lines that helped shape and accentuate the figure beneath the top, of course with a surgically removed left sleeve sewn shut as a harrowing reminder of his lack.

Strolling into the room like the entire campus belonged to him, and everyone attending it his dutiful citizens as he had done each time he entered literally any room, he turned those glass petals of auric hues onto Sol with a raised brow cocked in his regard.

“And a fine morning to you as well, Sol. Pardon my lack of decorum but I’d be remiss if I didn’t ask for clarity — did you mean to imply you would use food, drinks, paints and glitter on us all? You’re quite a charmer and your particular brand of masculine beauty is of course only second to mine but I must remind you that I duel for a different league.” He quipped in clear and obvious jest judging by the easy smile he managed to offer in turn. “But I’ll let you know if my alignment changes. However if you’re offering to share these things with us, I may indulge you.”

Despite the banter and light ribbing, he felt no shame nor concern with approaching and sitting at Sol’s side in the couch. He always opted for sitting in the couch, it was the only seat in the commons that had a decent view of every possible avenue a potential threat could enter.

“May I ask what made you desire an arts, crafts and treats day?” He inquired, leaning more comfortably back into the couch’s cushion and of course since Sol sat to his left, he didn’t have to worry about elbows bumping uncomfortably.
