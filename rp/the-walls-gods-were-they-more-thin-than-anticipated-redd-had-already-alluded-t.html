Vornakir

The walls, gods were they more thin than anticipated. Redd had already alluded to that fact more than once. He didn't realize how thin until he was about to go grab a glass of water and overheard through his door a brief conversation - No exacts, but he heard voices. One was husky and smooth at the same time, sure enough to be Redd. The other, feminine and strong, intoxicating. Especially now. After the night they'd had, every word seemed even more thrilling to hear coming from her lips. Though it occurred to him, while he expected her to be more public and be more real with him that he had yet to give her the courtesy of the reward for that. And he knew she was still awake, this may be the perfect time.

And so, he readied everything that was needed to be ready before he slipped out of his room. He closed his door quietly, for being considerate to those sleeping and walked down the hall - Not quite sneaking, but still courteous. He arrived at her door, and gave it his signature knock. Once, then twice and thrice in succession. Tap, taptap

When she answered the door, she likely could never have guessed what she'd open it up to. A finely stitched and crafted vest over a nice shirt, clean and well made pants and a freshly bathed Vornakir. He smelled of expensive soaps, his hair was brushed back groomed appropriately, and his demeanor was formal to a degree. A mage hand floated behind him, holding a small basket and his left arm was of course sewn shut on this outfit.

"Sorry to bother you so late, doll. But I've come to the realization that I've not done you the proper honor of being your boyfriend the right way. I've had you, I've kissed you, I've told people my feelings to some degree. We've made it fairly obvious and fairly official but we haven't done the one that you deserve - A date. So would you, please if you're not so tired, accompany me to a nice spot just outside the dorm so we aren't breaking a curfew and have a meal under the Moon?" He asked in a voice like velvet with a smile that somehow cut the sin it usually portrayed into refined regality.

When she came to stand in the doorway, not a hair was out of place, even though she knew she would be receiving the one who was the deadliest enemy to her every attempt at composure and the ship that carried the myth of her poise had sailed long ago. She wore her night dress of lavender-hued silver and its matching robe cinched closed over the top.

The pep talk from Redd (from which she took all the wrong lessons, just as she had from the one she received from Caspian days prior) had chased away the terror of what she'd done. Replaced it with confidence that whatever she might face later, it was worth it. The heartbreak of attempting to conjure sustenance — her signature, the very first gift of power Milady had given her — and coming up with nothing was mended well by the praise she received for being so radiant in this made-whole state.

Hand resting on the frame of the door, she leaned there with a casual confidence and an easy smile. Bemused slightly, but still sparkling with a delight rarely seen by anybody but him. "What's the matter? Do you need me to tuck you i—"

The smile fell away slowly when she received the explanation, her brows raising instead. "Huh." Her eyes dropped to the basket held aloft behind him, then back up to him. "And here I thought perhaps romance was dead," she quipped, a lopsided smirk settling in. "I'll get dressed and meet you in the common room," she assured, shooing him away.

When she emerged, her hair was swept into an elegant updo, held in place by pins decorated with golden hurricane lilies. Her dress wasn't her usual style. The skirt was shorter than she normally sported without wearing jodhpurs underneath.

The asymmetrical waves of the hem lapped just above her knees in front, cascading a little lower around the backs of them. Midnight blue gradually gave way to gold as it crept higher. Sheer sleeves started at her wrists and fastened around her throat, more shades of night sky worn over the gold bodice. The neckline of it plunged (tastefully) toward her waist, which was wrapped in a blacker shade of blue, adorned with gold embroidery to break up the transition of color and emphasize the narrowness of her waist.

She was always dressed stylishly, but not quite like this, and oh, but she was pleased. And it hadn't taken long at all to get changed. She hadn't had to dither and fret over what she thought might flatter her best or make her look most appealing to his eye. She had no need to.

"Sorry to keep you waiting," she murmured low, teasing him. "I had to make sure I looked better than you."

Zašia looked incredible and she knew it.
