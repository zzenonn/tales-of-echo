### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni
<sub>Feb 25, 2026 &middot; 03:55 AM UTC</sub>

Dear Father,

Before I say anything else in this letter, there’s something you should know: I met a divination archmage. Headmaster Unelle, well, I guess *ex-headmaster*, set up a meeting for us after we uncovered the culprit behind the murder of a professor and the Quasi incident.

He spoke of numerous uncertain outcomes and warnings that hung over our heads, one being about the fate of the academy itself. 

Unelle is being forced to resign, and a replacement has yet to be determined, which brings me to the Chroma-Clash. Three students are chosen by each High Court to compete in a spell-duel tournament, and the court with the winning student will select the next headmaster.

The archmage said that the Court of the Axiom choosing the next headmaster is in the best interest of the academy, but the only one in our group chosen to represent them was Caspian. If any of us are matched against him, we would have to lose to get the most favorable outcome.

Vornakir won’t throw a match; he’s too prideful and too conscious of his legacy to do that. Redd won’t throw either; one court has offered him the power to help save his home, and the other buying for his participation is the one his boyfriend belongs to. They’re in dangerous waters right now, and he’ll want to prove himself a worthy partner to his boyfriend and their family. Auri has big, long-term dreams that depend on her visibility and success, so I know she won’t sacrifice that. So that leaves me. 

It feels like Caspian and I are the only ones considering the consequences this tournament will have on the academy if the wrong court wins. Caspian said he didn't want anyone to sabotage their own match, and everyone else in the group agreed, but there’s a part of me screaming that they’re making the wrong decision.

I’m at a crossroads, Father. If I participate and try my hardest, I risk standing opposite Caspian and knocking him out of the clash; if I don’t participate, I risk abandoning the chance to gain prestige within the courts and leaving Caspian to fight alone; and if I intentionally lose, I could lose the trust of my friend. So I keep asking myself: *Should I even bother?*

I know you would want me to enter; you always believed I should sharpen every weapon at my disposal, especially magic, but the truth is, my heart isn’t in dueling. I don’t hate it, and it can even be fun, but it isn’t who I am. Dueling is a hobby to me; design is my passion. So I keep circling the same question: *If I don’t crave the arena, should I bother to step into it at all?*

The archmage told me something else. He told me that Lucien is suffering from *Arcane Aphasia*, and his body and soul can’t communicate with the magic around him. He also told me that Lucien knew the cure for his condition, and that he managed to hide the answer in my bracer.

I don’t even know where to begin uncoding the bracer’s secrets, and I don’t know why Lucien had to hide the answers to his condition, but I do know that if there is even a slim chance to save him, I have to pursue it, and that I’ll need your help.

There’s one last thing I wanted to tell you. I have a boyfriend now; his name is Adrien. We recently went through a difficult patch of adjustment in our relationship, but we’ve resolved it now and are doing better. I know you told me not to entertain a relationship with him, but obviously, I didn’t listen. But he makes me really happy, Father, in a way that pulls me back to the ground and surrounds me with warmth and security. He’s intelligent, emotionally mature, ambitious, and most importantly of all, he’s kind. In the middle of everything happening here, he has been one of the only constant sources of peace I’ve found. I hope you’ll give him the chance to show you who he is, instead of making up your mind before you ever meet him.


<sub>Feb 25, 2026 &middot; 03:55 AM UTC</sub>

All of the drama with the courts, Lucien, romances, and friendships has made me tired, Father. People’s emotions have been overwhelming me since the Quasi imploded: fears linger like mist in the air, anger pulses like waves through the halls, and frustration is building like a volcano about to erupt. What makes it worse is that I can feel it all at once.

They’ve started calling me a Sparkian Protector; I never asked for that title, and I certainly don’t know how to live up to it. I just want my brother to be healthy, my friends to be safe, and to create things that make the world feel more colorful.

Right now, I just need someone steady to talk to. You told me in your last letter that you were willing to try to understand me better; this is me trying to meet you halfway. I’m inviting you into my life again, and not just the parts of me that look good on paper, but the parts that actually make me, *me*. I don’t want to fight and defend my life choices to you anymore; I just want my dad back. 

If you’re willing, I would like to come home or meet you here at the academy to talk.

Please.

Love,
Sunni
