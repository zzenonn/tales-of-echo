### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 06:57 AM UTC</sub>

The door creaked as Alden pushed it open, a rush of warm air and smoke washing over him. Laughter still lingered in the rafters, though the crowd had thinned; empty mugs abandoned where celebration had burned hottest. He stepped inside alone.

He crossed the room and lowered himself beside the central fire, the flames reflecting faintly in his eyes. The crackle of burning logs should have been comforting. *Should have been.*

Instead, his thoughts were still outside, caught beneath the stars and that impossible glow.

Golden wings. Not quite feathered, not quite radiant, but refined like sunlight sharpened into a blade. An empty, blank face, impossible to trust... And the words...

"It is your destiny to fall. We have already seen fit."

Alden exhaled slowly, fingers curling around the symbol of Lathander at his chest. “Lies,” he murmured under his breath, though the word felt thin in the open air. The Morninglord taught that every dawn was a promise - that no night, however cruel, could last forever.

Still… divine things did not usually waste breath on empty threats.

He stared into the fire, watching embers rise like dying stars. 

"We passed..." 
he reminded himself. The Winged Concord did not hand out victories. He had earned his place. His squad had earned theirs; through blood, bruises, and long nights in the saddle, trusting their wyverns with their lives.

His jaw tightened.

“If doom is coming,” Alden whispered to the flames, “then it will find us facing the dawn, not hiding from it.”

The fire popped, sending a brief flare of light upward. For just a heartbeat, the glow reminded him of that golden figure - and then it was gone, reduced to ash and warmth once more.

Alden sat straighter, resolve settling into his bones. Whatever that creature was... angel, herald, or something wearing borrowed light... it did not speak for Lathander.

Alden whispered to himself, 

"You do not determine my destiny..."

"But you may live long enough for it to defy you."

Tomorrow, the sun would rise.
And Alden Ashdawn would rise with it.

*Alden continues to stare into the fire, clutching his mug, although with a much more hopeful look on his face.*


### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 04:28 PM UTC</sub>

Talys strolled across the room. In his hands he counted out silver pieces he had just won in a game of cards with other newly inaugurated riders. They grumbled behind him as he denied them the opportunity to turn their fortunes. As if their luck had anything to do with the results.

Talys moved about the party with precision as if each step was calculated and planned. As he travelled he chatted lightly with those he passed.

With a cocky grin he flipped the last silver piece high into the air with ease before catching it and rolling it across his knuckles. A practiced maneuver that he’d done hundreds of times around his fellow Talons, now Riders. 

Talys then adjusted the sleeves of his silk shirt. He grabbed another drink before passing by Alden at the fire. He paused a couple of steps having heard the man whispering to himself of destiny and doom. 

He swung around to face Alden with a puzzled expression. 

“What are you talking to yourself about? Destiny or something?” 

Talys planted himself across the fire from Alden. He leaned towards Alden. With an unexpected amount of passion he spoke clearly. 

“No such thing as destiny. It’s a load of garbage made up to trick folks into thinking they gotta be happy with the hand they’re dealt. Destiny, the gods, whatever it is I don’t need ‘em. Never done a thing for me and look where I am now.” 

He held his hands out wide indicating the celebration around. His casual dismissal of the gods would be nothing new to Alden. In the last two years Talys would’ve made very clear he was not a man of faith in anything much other than himself and the coin in his pocket.


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 07:38 PM UTC</sub>

Alden watched the fire for a long moment before answering, letting Talys’ words settle like ash.

“I think you’re half right,” he said at last.

He lifted his gaze, calm and steady, not offended - just certain. “Destiny isn’t a cage. Anyone who says it is wants you small, quiet, and obedient.” He reached down, moving a glowing ember with the tip of a poker, shaping its path before nudging it back into the flames. “But it isn’t nothing either.”

Alden gestured toward the hearth. “Fire always rises. That’s its nature. Doesn’t tell you where it’ll burn, or what it’ll light - but it will rise.” His eyes flicked back to Talys. “That’s how I see destiny. A direction, not a script.”

He tapped his chest once, over Lathander’s symbol. “The Morninglord doesn’t hand us endings. He gives us mornings, a beginning. What we do with them... that’s on us.”

A faint smile tugged at his mouth. “You didn’t get here because the gods dragged you along. You got here because every time you were given a choice, you chose *forward*.” He inclined his head slightly. “*That* is destiny. The one you keep carving, step by stubborn step.”

The smile faded, firelight hardening his expression.

“But when something claims your end is already written?” Alden said quietly. “That’s not destiny talking. That’s fear dressed up as certainty.”

He leaned back, eyes returning to the flames.

“And I don’t intend to let fear decide how my story ends.”


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 07:56 PM UTC</sub>

From the shadows near the ceiling, Aurelio watched the events below. He had slipped away from the noise of the party some time ago, climbing into the rafters with quiet, practiced ease. 

Up here, above the clatter of mugs and overlapping voices, the air felt more open; closer to the open sky he’d grown up beneath, closer to home.

Aurelio sat along one of the thick wooden beams, one knee drawn up, the other leg dangling loosely over open space. His fingers toyed with the thick blue ribbon wrapped around his wrist, the party's prize for slaying the manticore.

His jaw tightened. The image of the manticore still lingered vividly in his mind; the way it was pushed into its own attack by Talys, diving with Orion to claim the ribbon, the harsh, crushing sound he heard as the creature collided with the waters below.

He knew manticore’s weren’t native to the region, which meant it had been dragged from its home, shipped across borders, and set loose into the open sky, but not as a living creature, but as a spectacle for sport and target practice.

*It wasn’t evil*, he thought. *It was just afraid.* He knew from many years as a scout what fear looked like when something felt cornered. 

Aurelio exhaled deeply as his gaze drifted toward the roofline; it was as though he were trying to see through it and into the open air. His people were taught from a young age that no creature was meant to be owned, and no life was meant to be caged. Freedom was not something granted or earned; it was something inherent.

The Winged Concord offered honor, purpose, and protection, but recently, he couldn’t shake the question gripping in his chest: *If I must abandon my morals to stand here, then what am I really becoming?*

Below, familiar voices cut through his thoughts; first Alden, then Talys. Aurelio leaned forward to listen in. He caught fragments of the conversation at first: destiny, garbage, gods never did a thing for me.

The full weight of the words settled into Aurelio, and his lips pressed into a thin line. He didn’t agree with Talys about everything, but he did on this.

Slowly, Aurelio slid off the beam and dropped down, boots touching down softly near the edge of the fire. At Aurelio’s heels, Castor stirred, and his ears twitched as he lifted his head from where he’d been curled near a support post. The blink dog gave a soft, sleepy huff, stretched his front legs, then sat at Aurelio’s side, tail thumping against the floor.

Aurelio’s gaze flicked between Alden and Talys, then he raised his hands, fingers moving expressively. As he signed in smooth motions, Castor tilted his head, and a faint glow of blue magic briefly surrounded his body.

Then, in a warm, playful voice, the dog spoke:

*“He says he agrees with both of you.”*

Castor glanced up at Aurelio, then back to the two men.

*“He says destiny isn’t something written for you, it’s what you build with the freedom you’re born with.”

Aurelio’s hands moved again, slower this time, and Castor translated.

*“He said no one, not gods, not spirits, not angels, not monsters, gets to decide who you become.”*

Aurelio’s eyes drifted briefly to Alden, and Castor continued.

*“He says he understands believing in something bigger than yourself, and that believing in light can give people strength.”

Aurelio signed once more, this time with a small, sharp motion at the end. Castor’s tail gave a soft wag.

*“But where he’s from, they don’t follow gods.”*

Castor finished the thought.

*“They follow the winds because the wind can’t be owned, and it doesn’t obey crowns or commands. It moves where it chooses.”*

Aurelio lowered his hands, and a calm expression spread across his features. Castor looked between the three of them and spoke again.

“So he's saying he respects your faith and thought, Alden, but he  also understands Talys’.”


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 08:46 PM UTC</sub>

“That’s fair,” Alden said quietly.

He rested a hand over his holy symbol, not in challenge, but in acknowledgment. “I don’t follow Lathander because he commands me. I follow him because he reminds me to choose the light - every day.” A faint smile touched his lips. “Sounds to me like your winds and my dawn aren’t so different.”

His gaze lingered on Aurelio for a moment longer. “Both free. Both moving forward.”


### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 09:17 PM UTC</sub>

The sudden arrival of Aurelio caused Talys to tense before he realized who had joined them. His hand that had darted to the dagger strapped to his ankle slowly retracted. A moment later it was as if the tension hadn’t even occurred. 

Talys listened to the two different opinions. He raised his eyebrows at a few points but never interrupted or made a rude reaction. After they both concluded he lightly shook his head.

“It does seem we agree to some degree but I must insist that you both have one thing mistaken. Not all are born with freedom.”

He let the words hang for a moment before continuing.

“Your suggestions are noble but naïve. The world is cruel and many would snuff the flame instead of seeing what it burns.”

Talys rolled his sleeve up before conjuring a small flame in his palm. He slowly moves it towards the larger fire, his infernal heritage and magic protecting him from the heat.

“Choices only matter if you have the will, the strength, to make them matter. Each of us may be a flame but how easy it is…”

His hand enters the fire in the center and his conjured flame is indistinguishable from the larger blaze. 

“…to be consumed.”

Then a small blue flicker appears in Talys’s’ palm. It grows until the entire fire between the three of them becomes blue. It ends suddenly with a snap of his fingers. Fixing his sleeve he finishes.

“Your new dawn, and your winds are not guaranteed. You will only receive them as long as you are strong enough to earn them.”


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 05, 2026 &middot; 09:57 PM UTC</sub>

Aurelio listened attentively as Alden spoke. At the mention of dawn and light, his expression softened, and he smiled, giving Alden a nod of agreement.

When Talys responded, though, Aurelio’s shoulders stiffened. The words about strength and being consumed rubbed him the wrong way, and his jaw tightened. He let Talys finish and waited for the fire return to normal before responding.

As he lifted his hands, Castor scooted closer to Aurelio’s leg and promptly flopped half his body onto his foot, tail giving a lazy wag.

His signing was sharper than before: less flowy and more clipped, as if he were trying to emphasize his point.

Castor glanced up at him, ears flicking before he translated.

*“He says you have a very negative view of the world.”*

Castor winced slightly, as if apologizing in advance.

*“He says you think with a narrow and jaded mind, which is probably why you feel like nothing good is guaranteed.”*

Aurelio’s fingers moved again; this time, he made a very distinct, almost exaggerated flipping motion with both hands.

Castor huffed out a quiet sigh.

“He says you should expand your worldview and look at things from different angles.”

A beat of silence followed before Castor added on his own accord:

*“No offense.”*

Aurelio’s hands didn’t slow as he kept signing.

*“It may be naïve to believe the world is only kind, but it is just as childish to believe it’s only cruel.”*

Castor glanced between Talys and Alden as he continued.

*“The world exists in a state of neutrality. It just is.”*

Aurelio’s signing grew more animated.

*“It becomes benevolent or malevolent based on how people choose to act within it.”*

Aurelio’s eyes flicked sharply back to Talys, and his hands snapped through the next set of signs with slight traces of irritation.

Castor’s ears drooped a little before he spoke.

*“He says your comment about the winds is just wrong.”*

Aurelio looked at Castor before quickly moving his hands again. The dog sighed before he translated again.

*“Very wrong.”*

Aurelio signed more slowly now, deliberate to a point that he wanted to make sure Talys was paying attention.

*“He says the winds have been guiding people since the beginning of time, and that they’ll keep guiding others until the world’s last breath.”*

Aurelio’s fingers closed into a firm fist over his chest, and Castor swallowed.

*“Your path in life isn’t determined by whether or not you have the strength to decide.”* A beat of quiet followed. *“It’s determined by having the wisdom and will to listen to the guidance around you.”*


### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 06, 2026 &middot; 11:27 PM UTC</sub>

Talys doesn’t waver under the dressing down from Aurelio except for a small twitch of his eyebrow. The tiefling finishes buttoning the sleeve he adjusted. 

Arms crossed, Talys made direct eye contact with Aurelio. Sizing up the man in his entirety. The way he sat, the manner in which he signed. While they had trained together for years that rigorous schedule hadn’t always left time for conversations such as these.

“You are correct that I think nothing good is guaranteed. It’s not, nothing is ever guaranteed good or bad. Therein lies the opportunity, to play the game of chance that is life. Winners can be kings and heroes, losers will be dead and long forgotten.”

The rakish grin spreads wider as if he’s reached the culmination of his philosophy.

“You both might think me a braggart too focused on his own ego. But I’ve worked for every single accomplishment I’ve ever had. Nothing in my life has been handed to me on a silver platter. Even the kindness I’ve received has come with strings attached. I’ve played the game, risked my life to do it more times than I can count. I will take what I want from this life and when I finally die it will be on my own terms having accomplished everything I possible could.”

A well timed cantrip caused the fire to flare in dramatic effect. No ill will came across in his words to Aurelio and Alden. Talys was many things but he ultimately respected his squad mates. While he wasn’t afraid to challenge their beliefs he wouldn’t lash out over hard words aimed at him either.

Then as if none of the debate had happened he lazily flagged a new drink from a server. He sipped and sighed with refreshment.

“Our lives have been very different paths, I don’t seek to change yours nor my own. If you intend to chart your course by wisdom and mine by strength then our squad will only be the stronger for it.”

Talys relaxed and waited to see what effect his words would have on his allies. While he wouldn’t mind their friendship it wasn’t a requirement. It was better to know now where they stood than when on a blood soaked battlefield.


### &#x270D; Aurelio
<sub>Feb 09, 2026 &middot; 06:49 PM UTC</sub>

Aurelio didn’t immediately fire back as he listened to Talys speak; his parents had raised him with the decency of not interrupting others when they were talking. So, he respectfully sat there with loosely folded arms, waiting for his turn to speak. 

He jotted down mental notes about his squadmate as he listened: his devilish grin, his flair for the dramatic, his easy dismissal of the conversation; none of which were traits Aurelio was a big fan of. 

When Talys finished talking, Aurelio exhaled a slow, tired breath as he rolled his eyes and lifted his hands. 

Castor, sensing what was coming, shuffled a little closer to Aurelio’s leg and lay his head on his lap. Aurelio gave him a brief scratch behind the ear before he started signing; this time, it wasn’t fast or explosive, it was just precise.

Castor’s voice came out calm but with an edge of tension.

*“He said…you just disproved your own argument.”* Castor glanced at Aurelio, then back to Talys as he continued to translate. *“You said nothing in life is guaranteed, right?”* 

Aurelio’s fingers moved in a small circular motion, then pointed subtly at Talys.

*“If that’s true, then by your own reasoning, you are not guaranteed to die by your own terms.”*

Castor snickered to himself before he finished translating. *“He said that he’s no mathematician, but the odds of you dying on your own terms are slim to none.”* 

Aurelio made a sign with his hands that looked draconic in nature, and Castor laughed even louder. *”He said that according to your words, you’re more likely to die by your wyvern eating you whole than by you getting a death you desire.”*

Aurelio tilted his head, obviously unimpressed, and his hands moved again.

*“He doesn’t think you’re a braggart with a big ego.”* Castor winced and tucked his ears back. *“He knows you’re a braggart with a big ego.”*

Aurelio flicked two fingers toward his own eyes, then outward, the universal sign for *he’s been watching you*.

*“He said he’s been watching everyone, especially you, from a distance for years.”*

Aurelio’s hands slowed, and he signed more deliberately.

*“And he says he’s rarely seen anything of substance from you, other than your love of gambling.” A beat of silence filled the space; Castor clearly didn’t want to translate what Aurelio had just said, but he did anyway. *“And of yourself.”*

Castor swallowed as Aurelio’s gaze didn’t leave Talys.

*“He says respect isn’t automatic where he’s from. It’s earned,”* Aurelio signed again, but very sharply, *“and so far, you’re sitting dead last in the running for his.”*

The fire cracked softly, and Aurelio let his hands fall to his lap. After another brief pause, he added one last set of signs. Castor translated these words very quietly.

*“He said his fathers also taught him to know when to stop beating a dead horse.”*

With that, Aurelio turned his head away from Talys and looked into the flames in front of him.


### &#x270D; Alden
<sub>Feb 09, 2026 &middot; 09:36 PM UTC</sub>

Alden lifted a hand gently, palm open toward both of them - a quiet, grounding gesture meant to slow the moment rather than stop it outright.

“Hey,” he said, voice calm but carrying just enough weight to cut through the edge in the air. “This one’s on me.” He followed with a slight chuckle.

He glanced between Talys and Aurelio, expression apologetic, a touch weary. “I stirred the waters talking about all this in the first place. Didn’t mean to turn a celebration into a debate around the fire.”

Alden rose to his feet, brushing ash from his knees. “Both of you see the world through paths that make sense for where you’ve been, and what you’ve had to do to survive.” A small, genuine smile followed. “Doesn’t mean one of you has to be wrong tonight.”

He stepped back from the fire, giving them space. “I’m going to get some air... and maybe a drink that doesn’t taste like smoke.” His eyes softened as he added, “No hard feelings. I’d rather remember tonight for what we earned, not what we argued about.”

He looks towards Castor, and gives him a kind smile.

With a respectful nod, Alden turned away and walked out the door, letting the crackle of the fire reclaim the space he left behind.

### &#x270D; Aurelio

Aurelio watched as Alden got up to leave. He gently nodded and offered a polite smile that didn’t quite reach his eyes, as Castor ran over and nuzzled against Alden’s leg after he smiled at him. As the door closed behind Alden, Aurelio’s smile slipped, and was replaced by guilt. He hadn’t meant for the conversation to become a debate and make Alden leave.

Castor noticed Aurelio’s mood change, and he leaned against his leg, offering him some quiet comfort.

His guilt didn’t last long due to Talys speaking again; the words *sulking* and *accomplishments* hit him like a spark in dry brush, and Aurelio’s shoulders went rigid. Color rushed up his neck and face, turning his cheeks a vibrant crimson, his jaw clenched so tightly it ached, and his breath came in sharp, ragged pulls.

Castor whimpered softly. *“Oh no,”* the blink dog muttered under his breath. *“Here it comes,”* he said while hiding his face under his paws.

Aurelio’s hands flew through a series of signs at a breakneck pace; the signing was sharp, angry, and cut the air with force. Castor was clearly having a hard time keeping up with Aurelio’s increased speed.

*“Uhhhh…he said maybe if you cared about something other than yourself,”* Castor began, voice strained, *“your fame, your accomplishments, your image,”* he swallowed, *“you might actually notice what’s happening around you.”*

Aurelio’s hands flew, and Castor was forced to take on a harsher tone to get Aurelio’s point across.

*“Here’s a fun ecosystem lesson I know you didn’t know: manticores aren’t native to this region!”*

Aurelio jabbed a finger downward, outward, then made a sharp, tearing motion.

*“That means the Concord had it *imported* and ripped it from its home.”*

Aurelio’s hands trembled with fury.

*“So what did we do?”* Castor said in a demanding voice. *“We hunted a terrified creature that didn’t understand where it was or why it was being attacked.”*

A pause filled the air, and Castor’s ears drooped.

*“For no reason.”*

Aurelio’s signing slowed, every movement carrying a heavy weight.

*“Back home,”* Castor continued, *“creatures of the sky are sacred, and their life is treated with reverence.”*

Aurelio’s hands pressed against his heart.

*“We only attack them when we’re defending ourselves, and we do it non-lethally.”* Castor’s voice cracked just a little. *“And today I hurt something sacred, and it wasn’t to protect anyone or to survive.”*

Aurelio’s breathing was audible now, and tears streamed down his face.

*“It was just for sport.”*

Castor winced.

*“So yeah,” he finished bitterly, “please excuse my sulking.”*

Aurelio reached for his wrists and forcibly ripped the blue ribbon free, the soft fabric tearing unevenly. Then he stepped forward and shoved it hard into Talys’ chest; the impact wasn’t violent, but it was angry.

Aurelio signed again, glaring daggers into Talys’ eyes. Castor resumed translating.

*“Here, since you care so much about your accomplishments, you keep it.”* Castor’s voice dropped low. *“Keep it to remember how our promotion was earned by murdering an innocent creature.”*

Aurelio turned and stormed toward the exit, shoulder bumping against Talys’ as he passed. Just before he crossed the threshold, Aurelio stopped and turned back only long enough to sign a few final words before leaving.

Castor stood frozen, then he looked up at Talys, eyes sad and tail low. *“I’m sorry,”* he said softly. Then, reluctantly, he translated what his partner had said.

*“He said he wouldn’t want to be your friend even if you were the last living thing left alive,”* Castor swallowed, *“and that he wouldn’t trust you to watch a pet rock, much less his back.”* A soft, broken whimper escaped him. *“He said he’ll do what needs to be done to finish missions,”* there was a small beat of silence, *“but trust?”* Castor shook his head. “He has none for you.”*

### &#x270D; Talys
<sub>Feb 10, 2026 &middot; 05:44 PM UTC</sub>

Talys never broke eye contact with Aurelio the entire time he signed. He’d been respectful to let Castor translate the answers but he’d understood the signs the entire time.  A well timed spell could help with things like that. Not that he wanted to let Aurelio know that. Talys never disclosed an advantage unless he was sure he couldn’t use it. 

As Castor departed he didn’t deny the dog a light pat on the head. He opened his mouth to call something after the departing figure before he closed it without speaking. That was enough, any more might result in a brawl and Talys had no interest in that form of debate.

Unlike Aurelio’s opinion of him, Talys had a newfound respect for the man. He didn’t flinch at a challenge, that was important. Though this trial wouldn’t be the last time his ideals and reality would conflict. Next time Aurelio would need to decide to do what was demanded of him in the moment or walk away. There would be no time to sulk moving forward.

The tiefling remained alone by the fire. The snaps and crackles mingling with the part going on around them. He looked down at the ribbon shoved at him now lying in his hand. 

He examined it for a moment before nodding appreciatively. A fine memento to mark the occasion. He tied the ribbon around the bicep of his right arm. With a subtle application of magic a set of six tally marks appeared on the band in black thread. Carefully he added a seventh. A good a place as any to keep track.

Talys reentered the party and began to dance, drink, and celebrate one step further on his path. Soon he would be at war, with the Saintillac most likely. With war came chaos, and all wise men knew chaos was a ladder and Talys would be ready to climb.
