The Spark Saga · Auri Caspian
December 14, 2025 – January 13, 2026
Auri

By late morning, Auri had already reviewed her notes out of habit, realized there was nothing left to revise, reorganized them anyway, and then stopped halfway through a second pass when it finally occurred to her that she was doing this purely on instinct. After that came the book she’d been “saving for when things slowed down,” which she finished in one sitting, only to stare at the last page in silent accusation when it ended and offered no further tasks.

The puzzle on the coffee table took longer than expected, mostly because she kept pausing to straighten the pieces into neat piles before reminding herself that this was, allegedly, a leisure activity. She placed the final piece, adjusted it so it aligned perfectly with the image, and gave it a satisfied nod.

Then she froze.

“...Now what?”

That question followed her to the couch, where she collapsed flat on her back, arms folded neatly over her stomach, staring up at the ceiling like it might reveal a hidden syllabus or at least a suggestion. The room was unnervingly calm. No bells. No deadlines. No low, constant pressure humming in the back of her mind telling her she was behind on something.

Her tail began a slow, restless swish against the cushions, tapping an irregular rhythm that did not match the supposed goal of “relaxing.” She flexed her fingers. Folded her hands. Unfolded them. She had studied, trained, finished her reading, and even completed a puzzle. Her friends were all out doing other things.

Auri had, to her knowledge, exhausted all acceptable productivity. The silence did not applaud her.

She turned her head, squinting at the finished puzzle, then at the tidy stack of already-read books, then back to the ceiling.

“...Am I supposed to just sit here?” she wondered aloud.
