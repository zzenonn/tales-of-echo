Spark Oath Academy
The Present…

It was late afternoon when Caspian emerged from studying in his room. Typically around this time he would find at least one dorm mate around to hang out with. However as he strode into the common area he remembered the day before. 

Each of his roommates had let him know they had plans. Vornakir and Zašia were off together. Redd was with Zurr’ka. Sunni trying to find Adrien. It struck him how accurate his friend's prophecy about him being the only single roommate had become.

He really didn't mind being single but being surrounded by couples had a way of making one feel a bit lonelier. He shrugged the feeling off and set about polishing and sharpening his most prized weapons. First came the rapier, it always came first despite it not getting much use. He was glad it wasn’t worse for wear after Zašia’s trip into the woods with it. 

His gaze fell on the inscription…

CV, May you dance with this as we once did - TR

The memories came back to him as they always did…

The Palace of King Galio
One Year Ago…

Caspian was a good dancer - in fact he was a great dancer, but he’d never admit as much. He glided around the ballroom elegantly and gracefully, his partner held close to him. If Caspian was a great dancer then she was a prodigy. Not once did she even appear to notice she was dancing. It was as natural to her as breathing. 

As they twirled Caspian’s ivory and gold uniform flowed with air rushing past it. The skirts of her emerald dress flared and rippled with her steps. Her green eyes locked in on his, their cheeks both flush from the relief of finally sharing this dance..

His dance partner was none other than Terra Redwynne, the daughter of Lord Anders Redwynne, a well respected member of the king’s court and friend of the king. Terra was a popular member of the court's cadre of young nobility. Several other young men stared at the pair with open envy and even rage in their eyes. 

Those that weren’t envious of Caspian either stared in fascination or agape in horror. For their dance was already quite the scandal. The next day’s gossip would surely include plenty about the romantic dance shared between the Saintillian and Lord Ander’s daughter. 

They didn’t notice or care. This was their dance together and they didn’t worry about the future in a moment like this. So around the ballroom they moved, time stretching as it does for important events. To make them feel as if it might go on forever. Eventually, as all moments do, it came to an end as the song slowed and eventually faded to silence.

They stepped off the dance floor exactly as etiquette dictated and Caspian carefully led Terra to an empty standing table where he politely excused himself to fetch her a drink

Prince Galio spoke a mile a minute at Caspian who now held a pair of glasses of white wine. A Spark Alliance vintage by the looks of it. Galio quickly finished his glass of brandy and another was immediately replaced in his hand. There were perks to being a prince. 

“How long has this been going on? You two seemed pretty close for this to be a first date.” He added.

“For about six months, we’ve been meeting outside the palace to keep it secret.” Caspian replied smoothly

Galio then opened his mouth to speak before stalling out before a single word left his mouth. Caspian for his part simply took a sip of his wine and began his walk back to his table. Galio found his tongue soon after and gave words to his realization. This time the tone was serious and had a note of concern. 

“Her father definitely doesn’t know, does he?”

“Must’ve slipped our minds.”

The Palace
Six Months Before the Ball…

Caspian winced as he was caught red handed. He was standing on a counter in the palace in the middle of digging through the cabinets for the stash of whiskey one of the cooks hid there.

“Lord Veilbright! What a surprise to find you in the kitchens at this hour! How polite of you to assist Chef Andros in cleaning his cabinets.”

Terra stood with her arms crossed and eyebrows raised in amusement. She was wearing what appeared to be a patterned cloak over colorful clothes. She had an instrument case set down by her feet. 

Returning from a performance he thought. However, a half hour past sundown was quite early to be returning from a performance. It didn’t quite add up, now that he looked at her longer he noticed a growing discomfort. 

“Going somewhere Lady Redwynne? Last I believe I had heard your father had forbidden you from performing at common taverns any longer.” He retorted, still holding stock still.

“My plans and family are none of your business Caspian. Let’s just cut to the chase, if you didn’t see me I didn’t see you.” She gritted her teeth a little frustrated that her bluff hadn’t played out as she had hoped.

Caspian chuckled and finally returned himself to the floor. With the bottle of whiskey in hand, he cracked it and took a swig. He offered it to her as a courtesy. She rolled her eyes at the offer.

The young man smiled pleased with himself at her reaction. He had always enjoyed teasing her and Cordelia when he found them spending time together. It was also how he had known about her father’s restriction. 

As with many of the young women at court he felt that they had looked down on him from time to time as a bit of a lout and wrote him off because of his Saintillian heritage. He worried she saw him the same way especially with her father’s distaste for him. Seeing her standing resolute in front of him against her father’s instructions gave him pause on that thought. Maybe he had judged her too quickly.

He backed down a bit, curiosity winning out.”Sorry Terra, just a bit of teasing. Where are you playing tonight? If you’ll allow it I’d be happy to walk you there, I wouldn’t mind a bit of music to accompany my drinking tonight.”

“Won’t Galio be expecting you back with that bottle?” She asked unconvinced and unenthused.

To Terra, she had always tolerated Caspian and his antics towards Cordelia. He could be a bit of a brute but it could be quite amusing in her experience. “Not tonight, he’s in a meeting with his father’s council. So I’m just doing this for my own gratification. You’ll barely notice I’m there, plus it's been a while since I’ve heard you play and sing. I’d much rather listen to you than some minstrel at a random bar.” 

The sentiment was genuine, he had always made sure to pay attention when Terra was performing. It was a rare opportunity nowadays as her responsibilities as Lady Redwynne demanded more of her time. 

“Fine, but only because if I don’t leave now I’ll be late.” She conceded.

The pair excused themselves from the palace by the kitchen exit with cloaks drawn over their heads.

The Quilted Canary was a well known establishment in the upper city. It housed a large stage that could hold a full band and a few extras. The rooms were regularly booked up and each weekend it was packed with travelers and locals alike to see one of their performers. 

The revue included everything from musicians to mummers. Only the best ever played there, it was a kind of recognition that could only be earned through skill and practice. This was all the more reason why Terra so enjoyed her secret midweek performances. While not as well attended as the weekends it was better for her to remain incognito and the crowd was still plenty large. 

Terra and Caspian entered through the stage door. Caspian had taken her instrument case and been carrying it for her throughout town. The bouncer at the door merely nodded to Terra and let her through but gave Caspian a hard stare as he walked in behind her. 

“Go find a seat Caspian, come find me after my performance and we’ll return to the palace together.” she instructed him after he had gotten her settled backstage.

He obliged and went to get a drink from the bar and a prime seat. He managed to get a seat in the front by center stage by bribing a drunk gentleman with a little silver. He relaxed in the comfortable seat and took a sip of his drink while he waited for her to go on.

Their evening stroll had been pleasant. They even managed a little small talk about the goings on at court. He found that his original assessment of her had been quite wrong. She was thoughtful and while she wouldn’t hesitate to tease him she didn’t seem to mind his rougher corners. 

For a girl who had the attention of every gentleman in the capital she was remarkably down to earth. Of all the things about her she never once spoke to him as if he was a foreigner and it made him warm up to her quickly.

He had made her even laugh a few times. Usually the girls he dated just politely giggled. She really laughed when she found something funny. He found himself growing equally excited for their walk back as her upcoming performance.

Terra sat backstage oddly nervous. A little shakiness before a performance was normal but this audience felt different. Her unexpected guest had been a very pleasant escort. He had been a gentleman in every way, carrying her case and helping her avoid puddles and other obstacles. 

The magical lights dimmed slightly and the crowd quieted. Caspian set his drink down and joined the crowd in silent anticipation of the performer’s arrival.

Terra strode on stage, her boots tapping softly against the hard lacquered stage. Her posture was softer than Caspian had seen before. No longer forming to the rigid standards of her everyday. She held a lute in her hands and she took her seat at a stool prearranged at center stage. 

Caspian gave a soft clap and managed to catch her attention. She looked surprised to see him sitting so close to the front but the surprise slowly transfigured into a smile. After that she composed herself and began to strum the first chords of her song.

Her playing was crisp and clear; it felt like a brisk autumn day against bare skin. The notes chimed and overlapped to form a compelling melody that could not be ignored. It wasn’t melancholy or sad but neither was it happy or bubbly. Just when it seemed the entire audience was beginning to fall under her spell she began to sing.

To say her voice was beautiful was an understatement. Terra sang and anyone who had yet to pay attention turned sharply in their seats. Her voice harmonized with her strumming as she played her first ballad. The combination was serene, a ballad that dug deep into the core of anyone listening.

Caspian leaned back savoring the music. He’d known her talents and heard them before but he could tell there was an element that those performances had been missing. Freedom. No longer feeling chained it was clear that Terra’s muse was allowed to flourish. The young man didn’t understand entirely why but he just knew that he would do anything to hear her sing more in the future.

Terra sang eyes closed, as awash in her music as anyone listening. Her fingers slid up and down the neck of the guitar as if moved by an unseen force. She longed for these nights, the nights in which she could sing what her heart desired to the people in attendance. To her own surprise she felt a warm feeling in her chest to think that Caspian had made sure to sit so close to the stage.

He was promptly stopped by a private guard at the doorway separating the front and back of the house. The man didn’t speak, just held up his hand and wouldn’t let Caspian pass.

“Sir, I know the last performer. I came out from behind you to get a seat, didn’t you see me? Please, she's waiting for me.” 

There was a growing desperation in his tone. The guard didn’t react but just said with a deep voice,

“Sure you do bub. No groupies or stalkers backstage.”

Caspian whipped his head back and groaned. Thankfully about this time he saw Terra’s head peak around a corner behind the guard and see him. She quickly came around and called out.

“He’s with me Terrance, please let him through.” 

Caspian was begrudgingly let past the guard and he quickly caught up with Terra.

“Thanks for the save. Your performance was amazing Terra, the best one I’ve seen. Your voice is really beautiful.” 

He gave a smile ear to ear, hoping it came off naturally. He kept having to ignore the butterflies in his stomach. Where had they come from?

She inhaled sharply at his compliment and squeaked out a, “Thank you.” She took a deep breath before continuing. “I was happy with it too. I didn’t expect you to be right in front. I thought you just wanted an excuse to go out somewhere to drink.” 

She was hesitant, he probably just wanted to spend time with her to brag to his friends. He just sat in front so others would see him. Why was she so worried about why he came with her? Why had he bothered to pay attention to her show, he was usually so aloof.

“Well it started that way, but…well I really wanted to hear you sing. It’s been a year or two. Plus you seemed nervous on the way here and I figured you’d appreciate a friend in the crowd.”

Back at the palace they climbed a set of old and weathered steps in the oldest and tallest tower. Caspian led the way up the abandoned tower until they reached the top. 

The tower was the tallest point for a league in any direction and all around them spread the mirrored twinkling landscapes of the city and the sky. 

Terra brushed past him and walked to the edge and carefully sat and let her legs hang off the edge of the tower. She patted the ground next to her, an inviting smile stretched across her face. Caspian approached cautiously and sat. 

“You were right, the view from up here is beautiful.” She said looking out on the glowing dots beneath them.

“I agree” he said but she didn’t notice that he was more focused on her than the city around them. 

He placed his hand on top of hers. It was immediately grasped tightly and he smiled when she turned her head towards him. Her green eyes seemed to shine in the starlight.

They had fallen for each other that night. It wasn’t that they hadn’t thought of each other romantically before but their logic had dictated their actions. Now having spent time together and come to know each other beyond the most shallow facts, logic had given way to emotion. Now they sat inches apart feeling something new for the first time, feeling that this was something worth chasing. 

They both leaned in for their first kiss. Their lips met and time froze for them. Lost in each other for just a serene few moments. As they drew apart they turned back to the city and she rested her head on his shoulder. They shared no more words until it came time to bid farewell when the sun crested the horizon.
