Dear Lio,

The Spark Saga · Caspian
February 04, 2026
Caspian

Dear Lio,

I’m sorry I have not written you sooner. I need to start with an apology. I’ve been a bad friend to you these past months. I won’t make excuses for the fact that I haven’t sent a letter before now when I’ve been able to for months. I won’t make excuses that I wasn’t there on the anniversary of Gwen’s passing. I won’t make excuses for passing you false information.

All I can say is that I’m sorry. That I should have done things differently and now I regret my choices deeply. You have been and will be my closest friend and I only hope that you will forgive me.

Since I left the capital months ago much has transpired and it would not be possible to explain to you what has occurred in a reasonable page count. I will give you the simplest explanation with the promise that I can explain everything in depth at your request.

My parents actions to use me to spread fake intelligence to you caused a breakdown. We had it out both verbally and escalating to physically. I was nearly locked up if not for Cordelia’s intervention to convince my parents to send me to Spark Oath with her. That is where I’ve been since then.

To keep it brief I was then recruited by the headmaster to investigate a faculty death. The deceased faculty member had been planning to sell school secrets to my parents in return for resources. I agreed, the investigation spiraled into a larger conspiracy involving missing students and the Quasi building.

We managed to foil the actions of a disguised undead warlock posing as a professor, she was the culprit behind the murders. Not without consequences, the Quasi was destroyed and an ally of ours, a being named Aurum who lived inside, perished in the explosion.

Notably shortly before this consequential battle happened we had Sponsorship day. An event for the students to attempt to earn the attention of the High Courts of the Spark Alliance. My parents attended, while things were icy they had prepared an exhibition for myself and Cordelia.

I’m still reeling from the revelations that occurred during that spell duel. I dueled a man named High Registrar Saga, an enigmatic figure of the Saintillac. What I discovered during that duel is not easily or safely explained in a letter.

Before my parents departed we argued, more than before. They gave me an ultimatum, invite you to the school, lure you to hold court here. Or they would find a way to forcibly return Cordelia and I to the Saintillac. They claim the purpose would be to negotiate terms of surrender for the city of Cindros.

I’m not so foolish as to believe that is their only goal. However I cannot risk Cordelia’s position at Spark Oath. I’ve taken too much from her to take this from her as well. I’ve made too many decisions without considering the consequences. I won’t make that mistake again.

So I am writing to invite you to Spark Oath. It is assuredly a trap, politically or literally I don’t know. But I will state it plain, springing their trap intentionally is safer than allowing them to resort to their plan B.

I think together we can find a way to beat whatever they have planned. Alone I wouldn’t be able to make a claim, especially given how my parents have always been able to stay one step ahead of me. However now I have allies, friends who have supported me through everything. Where I may fail they won’t.

So please if only selfishly, come to Spark Oath. It will be dangerous but I miss you my friend and I’ll help you take this situation and turn it into a win.

Your friend,
Caspian Veilbright

P.S. I remember you telling me about Tempest Skreecher once. It’s a long story but I think something about it is tied to the school. You may think I’m crazy but I saw it in a vision. This school has vaults of ancient artifacts, maybe it somehow ended up here? With your status maybe we could take a look? That would be a way to turn the tide against the Saintillac right?
