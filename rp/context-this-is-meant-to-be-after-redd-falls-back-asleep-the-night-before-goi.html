The night was alive with monsters. Magma-born beasts stalked the streets of Luminar, their hulking bodies split with glowing seams, dripping fire that hissed as it scorched the cobblestones. Every step they took left molten pits in the earth; every roar sent sparks scattering like arrows loosed from the dark. Villagers fled in blind terror, their cries ripped away by the heat, but the creatures pursued without mercy, tearing through homes and flesh with the same burning hunger.

Redd ran. He could feel the suffocating heat claw at his lungs, every breath of smoke cutting deeper, but his legs carried him faster, faster, though never fast enough. His title thundered in his head with every step: lord, lord, lord. He should have been leading, defending, saving. Instead, he was fleeing through streets he once claimed as his own. His hands were empty; no spell rising to his lips, no wild strength at his back; just sweat and ash slipping down his skin.

One of the magma beasts lurched past him, a living inferno dragging its claw through a stone wall as if it were parchment. Another clambered onto a roof, the weight collapsing the beams in a spray of embers. Neighbors’ faces blurred in the chaos: a guard he once had a dalliance with, engulfed in molten claws; a mother clutching her child as fire swallowed them whole. Their screams rose, then vanished, leaving only the hunt.

And then he saw them.

His father’s form loomed ahead, face lit in ghastly glow, features carved with disappointment. The voice came without lips moving, deep and thunderous: “You were never enough. You shame this family. You shame your name.”

Beside him, Bluemond—Blue—stood small and fragile in the inferno’s glow. Barely twelve, his eyes were wide with terror, his hand outstretched. “Redd!” he cried, voice cracking. “Why did you leave me?”

Redd’s chest clenched. He reached, desperate, his arm trembling toward that hand. “I didn’t want to!” His voice broke, swallowed by the roar of flame and beasts. The magma creatures prowled between them, each step collapsing the ground into pits of molten stone, their burning eyes fixed on him. His father’s shadow grew taller, darker, stretching across the fire, while Blue’s hand slipped further from reach.

And still the fire closed in, the roar of the beasts swallowing his voice before he could speak again.

The spell ignited as it touched him, a vertical column of pale radiance that sliced through the creature’s molten chest. The beast screamed, body hardening to obsidian before collapsing into shards.

Another came. Redd moved like a storm—half man, half serpent, half something older than both. His movements blurred, driven by instinct more than form. When he swung, his claws left streaks of radiant light; when he breathed, smoke parted in obedience. For the first time in the dream, the fire yielded.

He stood tall in the glow, chest heaving, the Moonbeam’s silver flame still burning faintly around his form. Zašia’s voice echoed through the chaos, steady, unrelenting: You are better than this.

It pressed inward—focused, deliberate, as though something greater than the beasts had begun to watch. The ground trembled. The air thickened with the taste of metal and ash. His own.

He looked good, and that was the worst part. The flames sculpted him in impossible perfection—the smirk that had once charmed half of Luminar, the glint in the eye that always meant trouble was coming, the careless tilt of the head that said he knew he’d win before the game even started. Every inch of him radiated heat and ego.

Redd’s breath came sharp, but he didn’t move. The silver light from his spell flickered against the creature’s molten skin, reflected back like mock applause.

“Tell me,” Molten Redd continued, the smirk widening, “does it feel good? Pretending you’ve changed? You just swapped the audience.”

He leaned closer, ember breath curling against Redd’s cheek, voice dropping to a hiss. “You burned everything you touched long before the fire came.”
