### &#x270D; Sol/Sunni

Sol’s room felt like stepping into the night sky itself, as it usually did. Constellations drifted lazily across the ceiling, but tonight it was dimmer and quieter, as if it were trying to give him space. The stars still danced around, but they were slower and more spaced out; the auroras softened into faint silver and pale gold wisps that curled lazily along the ceiling; even the breeze that always threaded through the room had died down to a frail whisper. Sol’s room was known to match his mood, and it was clear he wasn’t sad or overwhelmed anymore based on his surroundings, but he wasn’t back one hundred percent.

He slightly straightened his posture from where he was standing at his drafting stand, easing the tension in his shoulders by rolling them back once. He wore an oversized light grey hoodie that hung loosely off his frame, sleeves pushed up just enough to keep them out of his way, the crimson school insignia faintly catching the soft ambient light of the room. Black joggers sat comfortably on his waist, and his gold-rimmed glasses had slipped slightly down the bridge of his nose, which he hadn’t bothered fixing yet.

His hair was different than usual; it was still pulled half up into the messy bun style he favored, but the upkeep had clearly lapsed. The blonde had faded downward, leaving most of his hair a deep raven black, with only the lower half carrying that pale gold color.

Shadows still lurked under his eyes, but they had lightened, and he looked less like exhaustion had taken root in him and more like it had just passed through.

Sol reached up, pulling a silver pencil from his hair. As he did, the sleeve of his hoodie shifted just enough to reveal a golden bracelet at his wrist. An opal charm caught the light first, flickering with soft iridescence, but right beside it was the heart-shaped ochre charm Vornakir had given him.

He was working with his sketchbook open beside him and propped carefully on a stand, pages filled with layered designs and notes. A tuxedo concept for Vornakir was sprawled across the pages; the base silhouette was sleek, but the detailing was where Sol had clearly lost himself in thought. Silky, black fabric was planned as the foundation, with textures noted in the margins for using matte fabrics to contrast with the silk’s sheen. He sketched golden embroidery designs along the lapels in delicate, almost celestial patterns with silver mapped along the seams to catch the light at certain angles. Small fabric swatches were pinned to the page: black, gold, silver, and a few others that he clearly hadn’t committed to yet. Deep charcoal, muted ivory, even an almost iridescent dark blue. In the margins, Sol had scribbled: *“Maybe break away from his usual color palette?”*

His pencil swiped across the page as he adjusted the lining of the jacket, before he paused, lost in thought. That was when he looked up and noticed Vornakir. He tapped his pencil against the edge of his sketchbook, fully looking him over.

“Hey,” he said, voice quiet and a little rough at the edges, but no longer strained to the point of breaking. It was his usual warmth again, but it was noticeably fainter. “I need to remeasure you,” he said, softly. “You’ve changed a bit since I last made something for you, and I want to make sure the shirt and jacket fit snugly against you without constricting your scales.

With a slight tilt of his head, he gestured toward a raised platform in the corner of the room. “Can you hop up there for me?” He paused just long enough for his gaze to linger for a second longer than necessary on Vornakir. “And don’t stand so rigid the second you get up there like you usually do,” he added, with the faintest hint of his usual humor threading in, the corner of his mouth lifting slightly. “It’s just measurements, not a royal inspection.”
