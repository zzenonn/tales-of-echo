Sol/Sunni

For once in his academic career, Sol was actually studying. He sat criss-cross on the living room couch, posture deceptively relaxed, like he wasn’t fighting against the concept of academic responsibility.

A thick textbook floated in front of him, held up by lazy threads of gold and silver magic that shimmered faintly whenever he focused. The title, The Art of Transmutation, was scrawled across the cover in sharp, elegant lettering.

Sol’s eyes tracked the pages, brows furrowed in concentration as he took notes in the notebook resting on his lap. His handwriting was surprisingly neat today; he even underlined important details twice, as if he were a real academic weapon.

On the table in front of him sat a cup of dark tea, steaming and half-forgotten. It rested on a single sheet of paper, with absent-minded sketches scattered across it. Tea stains bloomed at the paper’s corners like little bruises. Near the mug, other textbooks had been stacked in a chaotic pile: one had paint smears along the spine and was titled Chromatic Expression & Advanced Brushwork, another, a thicker book, had metallic lettering that read Enchantment Theory.

Nestled against Sol’s thigh, like an ancient cat who refused to be moved, was Bookie, snoozing so deeply his pages softly ruffled with each slow exhale.

“Mhm,” he murmured as he kept reading, tapping his pencil against the notebook. “Okay, Slow isn’t that complicated in theory. It just takes an annoying amount of concentration to maintain.

The floating textbook turned a page with a flick of his hand, the crinkle of the page crisp against the room's quiet. Sol leaned back into the couch cushions and narrowed his eyes when he read something he didn’t think was important. He scribbled it down anyway because, as much as he liked to pretend he didn’t care about his grades and academic performance, he did care a lot. Especially when he cast a spell in front of his peers, and it refused to bend to his will on the first try.

His gaze slid toward the open space in front of him, imagining how it would look if the spell manifested. A gentle frost lulling in the air, the faint sound of a slowing clock tick in his ears, and the slow motion of his opponents.

He returned his attention to the textbook and continued studying, determined to master the spell’s theory and execute it flawlessly.
