After the party returns to their dorm, Redd slumped hard into the dorm's common ...

The Spark Saga · Redd Zasia Caspian
July 06, 2025 – July 09, 2025
Redd

After the party returns to their dorm, Redd slumped hard into the dorm's common room couch, the weight of the night pressing heavier than any hangover. The door had barely shut behind him before he was muttering to himself, hands clawing slightly at his hair, breath hitching unevenly in his chest.

"Stupid, stupid. gods, what the hell did I do." The words came out ragged, more breath than voice.

He hunched forward, elbows on knees, eyes fixed on nothing.

"Should’ve kept my damn mouth shut. should’ve known she's wasn't going to walk away peacefully."

For a moment, he looked like he might keep unraveling quietly, alone. But his voice cracked the silence again.

"They’re all gonna hate me. Sorjin, Zurr'ka. I didn't mean for anyone to get hurt. "

He looked up at his party members; his face showing a rare moment of vulnerability.

He swallowed.
“Do . . . do either of you still trust me?”

Zasia

Zašia had been watching the way Redd moved as soon as Sorjin had told them what happened to him. He was rattled and people are unpredictable when they're rattled. That's why it's such an effective tactic against an opponent or rival.

When he put voice to his regret, she shifted her posture, arms crossing loosely under her chest and head tilting to one side. She meant to deliver a crisp who cares if they hate you? Not out of cold-heartedness, but as a prelude to an assurance of a path forward. Redemption, if one wanted to be flowery about it.

Except, he looked at them like that. Zašia's posture eased, a small bit of confusion filtered its way to her expression. She hadn't expected this sort of reaction from Redd, who always seemed so confident in himself. She was learning new things about her dormmates today, wasn't she?

"Of course. What happened isn't a reason not to trust you," she asserted with a slight furrow of her brow. "You had no way of knowing how she would react. It isn't as though you said 'good, now go beat that nerd up.'"

Redd

Redd let out a shaky breath that almost passed for a laugh. He wiped a hand down his face, like he could smear off the guilt if he just pressed hard enough.

“Well . . . glad I haven’t been voted off the couch yet,” he muttered, the corners of his mouth twitching into something that might’ve been a small hint of a smile of relief.

He leaned back into the cushions, eyes on the ceiling. “I didn’t think she’d… do that. I mean, Velvyn’s sharp, yeah, but this?” He shook his head slowly. “I didn’t see it coming. That’s what gets me.”

“I know I didn’t tell Velvyn to hurt him,” he said slowly, words heavy like he had to test each one before speaking it. “But I handed her the damn ammo, didn’t I? Wrapped it up in a nice little box.

“She made a choice. What happened to Sorjin? That’s on her. But I’m not pretending I didn’t help set the board.”

His gaze flicked to Caspian, then Zasia; tired, but steadier now. Not asking for forgiveness. Just anchoring himself.

“She fooled me."

He exhaled through his nose, more bitter than angry. Then, quieter:

“But she’s not getting away with it."
