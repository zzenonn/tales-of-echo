Dear Mother and Father,

I hope this letter finds you well. Life at Spark-Oath has been interesting. The academy is as impressive as its reputation promised, and the professors waste no time in testing both patience and skill. I’ve kept pace, of course, though the pace itself leaves little room for idleness.

What has surprised me most is how quickly I’ve come to value the company I keep. Redd, for instance, may be the quickest tongue I’ve ever encountered. Every remark of his comes with a grin, and somehow, he always manages to turn the dullest evening into something memorable. Zašia carries herself with the kind of grace that would turn heads in court, though here she pairs it with a mind as sharp as her poise. Caspian is a presence in every sense of the word; he eats, laughs, and fights with the same boundless energy, and I’ve never doubted his loyalty or heart. And Vornakir, well, he reminds me of home. There’s a nobility in him that he wears like a mantle, and though it could be overbearing, he tempers it with discipline that makes it difficult not to respect him.

Together, they’ve made Spark-Oath more than tolerable; they’ve made it enjoyable. Every day feels like a challenge worth meeting, and I dare say I’m the better for their company. You may rest assured that I am keeping pace with my studies and fulfilling every expectation, but I will admit that it is my peers who have made this place feel almost like something worth belonging to.

I’ll continue to write weekly, as required, so that you needn’t worry. You may consider this proof that all is well, that I am safe, and that the Aelia name is carried with proper regard here.

Until next week.

Love,
Sunni

And Vornakir, yes, here is a name I might be willing to hear again. Discipline, nobility, presence. He is the sort of companion I would expect from an Aelia. Learn from him. Keep him close. If you must waste your hours on camaraderie, then waste them wisely.

Your talk of Spark-Oath feeling “like something worth belonging to” is both childish and disappointing. You do not belong there, Sol’Régem. You belong above it. Belonging is for those content to kneel. You were not born for comfort, you were born for ascendancy. Remember this: every moment not spent sharpening yourself dishonors the blood that sustains your brother’s life and the legacy of our house.

And yet, for all your words, I have heard nothing of you doing anything to bring glory to our name. That, above all, is embarrassing. If you would prove yourself worthy, then do so where it matters. I expect you to enter the Spell Duels. There, at least, you may show the academy, and the world, that the blood of Aelia does not waste itself on mediocrity.

Your following letter will contain your academic results, particularly in political science, with sufficient detail to assure me you have not squandered your time. Do not fail me again with this drivel about friendship.

Patriarch Sirius Erebus Aelia

Rather than attempt brute force, I leaned on subtlety: weaving enchantments into the arena until he yielded of his own accord. It was a clean victory, one that required both patience and precision. I imagine you’ll approve of the result, if not the method.

As for political science, this week’s lecture circled around the nature of power. Power as influence, as perception, as the art of controlling how others see you. Nothing I haven’t already witnessed in court, but useful to frame in academic terms.

You may rest assured: my studies continue on pace, my name is carried with care, and I remain above reproach in the eyes of both professors and peers. This should satisfy your expectations until my next letter.

Love,
