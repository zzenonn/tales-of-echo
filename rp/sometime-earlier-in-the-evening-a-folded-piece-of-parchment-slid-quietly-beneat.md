### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Mar 08, 2026 &middot; 04:15 PM UTC</sub>

Sometime earlier in the evening, a folded piece of parchment slid quietly beneath Caspian’s door. The handwriting on the outside was unmistakably Redd’s — a little uneven, like he’d written parts of it, paused, and then come back to it again.

Inside:


<sub>Mar 08, 2026 &middot; 04:15 PM UTC</sub>

Caspian,

~~Dear Caspian, I hope this letter finds you well~~  ~~no, that sounds terrible.~~

~~Hi~~  
~~Hello~~  
~~Right. Fine.~~

I’m not entirely sure how one is supposed to start a letter to someone who lives in the same dorm as you. This feels a bit ridiculous, if I’m honest. 

But Vornakir wrote me one recently, and apparently that is a thing people do when they’re trying to talk like responsible adults. So, uh, here I am, attempting the same.

Anyway.

You’re important to me. ~~More than I probably say out loud~~ I hope you know that already, but it felt wrong not to say it outright before anything else.

I know we haven’t exactly been seeing eye to eye lately. I also know I’ve been… sharper than usual. ~~And you didn't deserve any of it~~ Maybe more than I meant to be.

That edge isn’t meant for you.

~~You’re one of the only people here I actually trust~~  
~~That sounded dramatic~~  

I’ve been pushing back harder at people lately, yes. But the people I care about aren’t the ones I want to push away in the process. And you’re very much in that category, whether you like it or not.

~~You’re one of my closest friends here~~  
~~I hate writing things like that~~  

So.

If you’re willing, I’d like for us to actually talk.

~~Without everyone else there~~  

~~Before this turns into something worse~~  

~~Before before I lose you like I did everyone else in my fucking life~~ 

Properly.

You can come find me in the common room when you get back tonight, or whenever you have the time. I’ll be around.

~~I’ll probably still be awake anyway~~  

— Redd

~~P.S. If Vornakir asks, the letter was perfectly clean.~~


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Mar 09, 2026 &middot; 02:30 AM UTC</sub>

Caspian entered his room and noticed the note almost immediately. His bag was haphazardly tossed onto the bed and he picked up the note with confusion and apprehension. Once unfolded the apprehension faded as he realized it was from Redd and noticed the intruder from the other night. 

Carefully he read the note a few times. Making sure he didn’t just skim its contents but to read the whole thing both considering and not considering the clear editorial process taking place in it. 

By the end a smile had crept it way onto Caspian’s face. No one except Redd could quite capture their own train of thought in process like him. 

Overall the letter was reassuring that maybe things weren’t as bad as they had appeared the day prior. He’d hoped so, but in the heat of the moment it wasn’t so clear.

Remembering the invitation to talk Caspian exited his room, note still in hand. It was late, Caspian had a training session with the other Axiom participants. The others resting in their rooms he found Redd in the common area. 

Caspian’s smile still present he greeted his friend, 

“Hey there. Got your note when I came in. I want to make sure I start with that I feel the same way about a lot of what you said. You have my trust, you are important to me too. I want you to know that a couple of fights doesn’t just change that. I don’t get scared off that easily.”

Caspian found a seat across from Redd and fell into it with a soft thump. 

“Let me also follow that up by saying I think I owe you an apology for something. You’ve been feeling pushed around and it’s clear that I could be doing more to make sure you know that I care even if it’s not in that exact moment.”

There was genuine remorse in his eyes. For as much as they might not agree about how to handle things he still could have made sure his friend felt cared for and supported. 

There was much more to talk about but this is where Caspian wanted to start.


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Mar 09, 2026 &middot; 04:41 PM UTC</sub>

Redd was in the common room when Caspian returned — though if anyone asked, he absolutely had not been waiting there.

At the moment Caspian stepped in, Redd was halfway through a spoonful of something from a bowl balanced on his knee — some kind of greenish grain-and-vegetable mix he’d clearly thrown together from the kitchen.

He froze mid-bite when he noticed the letter still in Caspian’s hand.

“…You actually read the whole thing.”

The spoon hovered awkwardly in the air for a second before Redd finally committed to the bite, chewing quickly like he suddenly remembered food was a thing people were supposed to finish.

“Please tell me you skipped the crossed-out parts,” he added after swallowing.

Caspian’s apology, however, clearly hadn’t been part of the expected script.

Redd blinked once, caught off guard. For a moment he didn’t say anything.

Part of him immediately wanted to push back. Stand his ground. Point out that Cedric had started it, that someone had to say something, that he wasn’t the villain here.

Another part of him remembered that he needed to try a little harder to be a better person. 

"Imsorryok?"

The apology came out before he could think too hard about it.

“I’m sorry too.”

The words came out more slowly this time; a little rough, like he wasn’t entirely used to saying them.

“I’ve been on edge lately,” Redd admitted. “Getting hit by a commet from the sky after being targeted by my maybe-still-boyfriend's spurned lover does that.”

He rubbed the back of his neck, glancing down at the bowl for a moment before looking back up.

“So… I’m sorry for that. For making it feel like we were the bad guys in that moment. Like we were the ones doing the bullying.”

A small pause followed as he searched for the right words.

“I know the way you handle things isn’t weakness,” he said after a moment. “You try to do right by people. Even the ones who probably don’t deserve it yet.”

His expression softened slightly.

“That’s one of the reasons I trust you.”

Another breath.

“But lately it feels like I’ve been doing the opposite,” he continued. “Like every time someone takes a swing at us we turn the other cheek too quickly.”

Redd leaned back a little, fingers drumming once against the bowl before he set it aside.

“And I’m starting to feel like if nobody ever pushes back, people just keep escalating and getting worse and worse until it's too late.”

His gaze returned to Caspian.

“So yeah… sometimes that ends with me doing some stupid stuff somtimes.”

There was no defensiveness in the way he said it, just quiet honesty.

“But that doesn’t mean I want it to turn into us becoming the thing you’re worried about.”

A small breath.

“That’s not what I want this to be.”

He nodded once toward the letter still in Caspian’s hand.

“I meant what I wrote. I want us to figure out how to talk about this… without it turning into a fight every time. I don't know where to draw the line.”


### &#x270D; Caspian
<sub>Mar 10, 2026 &middot; 03:17 AM UTC</sub>

Caspian nodded after Redd asked about the letter. He even chuckled a bit as he asked about the stricken out sections. 

“Yes of course I read the whole letter. To be quite honest I actually appreciated the crossed out sections. More people should include their revisions actually. It felt more genuine.” 

Caspian was equally surprised by Redd’s apology and he held up his hands as if to stop him. 

“You don’t owe me any apology. You have every right to feel how you do even before what happened to you during and after the duel.”

His expression changed from minor amusement at the quirks of the letter to thoughtful and contemplative. He listened carefully to Redd’s words and was careful to not interrupt even when a response came to his mind immediately. 

Caspian did start to shake his head to disagree when Redd labelled his actions as stupid. When Redd finished he started there. 

“It wasn’t stupid. I’m not here to judge what you did but I’m also not here to pretend I agreed with it. I trust you enough that your reasons are your own and they justified what you did.”

He seemed to mull over what he said next for nearly a minute before he spoke.

“I guess to explain why what you did got a visceral reaction I should give some context. Growing up Cordelia and I got bullied, almost constantly. In Celstate we were the evil Saintillian children and in the Saintillac we were the outsiders. I could usually give as good as I got against some of those bullies. So I don’t disagree, if someone’s causing pain you can’t just let them get away with it.”

A pause before he continued,

“…but Cordelia struggled. She wasn’t someone to hit back. That is until she sent a kid to the infirmary after her magic surged the first time. The rest I taught them their lesson later.”

There was the implication of violence but no darkness from Caspian. This particular act didn’t carry any regret. He stood by the choice to this day.

“So all that to say my problem isn’t with protecting each other. Bullies don’t go away by asking nicely. But I just don’t think Cedric has been ever more than a nuisance.”

He sighed, never one to be comfortable talking about himself for too long this was pushing his limits. 

“So I guess I just hope the next time we’re across from someone who’s antagonistic you take a moment to consider who has the power.”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Mar 15, 2026 &middot; 02:29 PM UTC</sub>

Redd listened quietly as Caspian spoke, the earlier tension in his shoulders easing a little as the story unfolded.

When Caspian mentioned Cordelia, his expression softened.

“…That must have been rough,” he said after a moment. “For both of you.”

He turned the spoon in his hand absently before setting it down beside the bowl.

“I can’t pretend I grew up like that,” he admitted. “Most of my time growing up was spent in the woods with my mom. After she died, growing up involved more etiquette tutors and taking care of Blue. Not bullies.”

A faint, self-aware smile flickered across his face.

“But I think that might be part of the problem too.”

He leaned back slightly, thinking through the thought as he spoke it.

“When you asked who had the power… I realized I’m not actually sure I know how to judge that very well.”

His gaze drifted briefly toward the table before returning to Caspian.

“I’m a lot more used to knocking powerful people down a peg or two,” he said, a wry hint of humor touching the corner of his mouth. “Turns out charming arrogant men into bad decisions is a surprisingly effective hobby.”

The humor faded into something more thoughtful.

“But that’s a different kind of fight.”

Redd exhaled quietly.

“So maybe that’s where I’m still figuring things out.”

A small pause.

“I just know I don’t want us to end up being the kind of people who hurt others for no reason.”

He nodded once toward Caspian.

“And I trust you to call me out if I get close to that line.”

A short pause followed. His fingers tapped once against the table before he spoke again.

“…Speaking of lines.”

His gaze drifted briefly away before returning to Caspian.

“Where does Sorjin fall in all of that?”

The question came out slower, more careful this time.

“Because that’s where the line gets blurry for me.”

Another pause.

“I don’t know if you believe me or not,” Redd continued quietly, “but I’m pretty sure he was the one who sent that comet after me.”

He leaned back slightly in his chair.

“And if I’m right… then I genuinely don’t know how I’m supposed to read that situation. I understand he's still hurt, but he almost killed me.”
