### &#x270D; Redd

> *OOC: likely weeks back*


Redd had been staring at the same paragraph for so long that the ink might as well have been rearranging itself just to mock him. A diagram of "avian mana-redistribution pathways" blurred again, and he pressed the heel of his hand to his brow with a quiet groan. Handling magical creatures in person? Fine. Easy. Instinctive. Put him in a paddock with a snorting thunder-ram or a venom-backed basilisk and he could calm it, read it, coax cooperation out of it like he was born for it. He can even convince Whimsy to drop food from the charcuterie board (most times)!

But ask him to memorize the twelve theoretical frameworks behind why the creatures behaved as they did? That was apparently where his brain short-circuited.

He slumped deeper into the sagging dormitory armchair, textbook open across his lap, quill stuck behind one ear, lips tightening in frustration. Every few seconds his eyes tugged—not toward the text, but across the room.

Auri was at the common table, surrounded by neatly arranged notes and a half-finished cup of tea. Focused. Efficient. Brilliant, in that easy way that made Redd’s stomach twist with equal parts admiration and dread.

Because, of course, of all the people he needed help from… it had to be her.

Redd dragged a hand through his curls, jaw flexing. He still felt the heat of embarrassment crawl into his ears whenever he remembered. He hadn’t meant to sleep with Auri’s boyfriend. He genuinely thought they were broken up. But then he never actually bothered to check, so he kept doing it anyway because he was stupid, and hurting, and not his proudest version of himself.

Auri had every right to hate him. Except she didn’t. The tiefling had been… civil. Calm. Even kind sometimes, in that unnervingly collected way of hers. Which somehow made everything worse.

Redd shifted again, the chair creaking. With midterms coming up, he needed help with the theory *desperately*. 

Finally, he exhaled, shut his eyes for three seconds, and muttered under his breath, “Ok ok. You can do this Redd. New me and all of that.”

When he opened them, he looked toward Auri—soft-voiced, hesitant, almost apologetic—as he cleared his throat.

“Auri… got a moment?”

The words hovered there, uncertain, carrying just a hint of the shame he still hadn’t quite managed to swallow.

### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Nov 26, 2025 &middot; 10:47 PM UTC</sub>

Auri had been carefully arranging her notes, spreading out sketches of magical flow diagrams in neat, overlapping layers on the common table. Steam curled from the half-finished cup of tea beside her, the spoon turning lazy circles on its own, keeping the brew warm. Her quill hovered above a particularly tricky diagram, poised to make a tiny correction, when she heard the creak of a chair behind her. 

She looked up, mildly surprised, to find Redd shifting uneasily in the armchair across the room. They had never spoken, but she knew exactly who he was. Her mind flicked to earlier in the week. Her boyfriend– correction, *ex* boyfriend– had planned a lovely outing for her birthday, something small and sweet. And she’d let herself get excited, cautiously, but sincerely. A lady didn’t have much luck in the romance department when she lived in a tower full of books and undead, after all. 

And then she’d opened his door.

She had stumbled across a scene she hadn’t expected on the day of her birth– her boyfriend tangled up with a man she didn’t know. She’d slowly closed the door, walked away, and that had been the end of all of it. Fury had simmered in her ribs, yes, but no harsh words or violent reactions would help her keep her jobs…or her scholarship. It simply wasn't worth it to her.

And now, here he was. Redd, looking sheepish and unsure. But, he didn’t look ready to run or make excuses. At least...he *better* not be about to make excuses. 

She closed her notebook, brushing her hands together, the faint scent of ink and tea rising around her. 

“You’ve got my attention,” she said, her voice calm and collected. She leaned back slightly, keeping the conversation neutral without shutting him out. 

“I’m…surprised, I suppose, to see you here. The last time we saw each other was…my *birthday*, wasn’t it?”


<sub>Nov 26, 2025 &middot; 10:47 PM UTC</sub>

Her lips curved into the faintest hint of a smile, enough to reassure him she wasn’t about to set him on fire. If he was worried about that. Which, to be fair, he should be. It’s not as if he knew her well enough to know she wouldn’t. Only that she *could*, should she be so inclined. 

“How can I help you, Redd?”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Nov 26, 2025 &middot; 11:24 PM UTC</sub>

Redd’s shoulders tightened the moment Auri said the word birthday. He tried to hide the flinch, but the memory hit him with the force of a thrown brick.

>>> He remembered the soft click of the door behind them.

A breath sucked in through teeth.
Then the quiet, final thud of it closing again.

Redd had blinked, frozen mid-movement, still pulling air into his lungs.
The boyfriend muttered something sharp under his breath, already irritated.

Redd’s voice had cut through the silence. “Who was that?”

The boyfriend didn’t answer right away. Instead, he gave a nervous little laugh and raked a hand through his hair like he thought charm would fix this.

“Redd, relax. It’s not what you think. We were basically done.”

Redd stared. “Basically?”

The boyfriend shrugged, slipping into that blend of arrogance and insecurity he mistook for confidence.

“I mean… look, she’s never around. Always buried in her research, or at the archives, or tutoring some undead thing. Half the time she forgets what day it is, or that I exist. We haven’t been… connected in ages.”

He said it with a dismissive wave, as if Auri’s dedication made her unworthy of consideration.

Redd’s jaw had tightened. “That doesn’t mean you weren’t still together.”

“She wouldn’t have shown up today,” the boyfriend shot back defensively. “She never does on days that actually matter. I figured she’d be holed up in her tower again—”

Redd had gone still. Very still.

“So you didn’t tell me,” he said slowly, “because you assumed she’d be too busy studying to catch you cheating?”

The boyfriend bristled. “Will you stop being dramatic? We weren’t working. It was ending. I just… didn’t want to deal with the argument.”

Redd felt the shame and anger flood him at once.
“Unbelievable,” he breathed. “You lied to me. And you walked me straight into this.”

“You’re overreacting,” the boyfriend insisted, rolling his eyes. “Trust me, she won’t care that much. She’ll be annoyed for a day or two, then she’ll bury herself in work again and—”

Redd didn’t let him finish.
Something in him snapped clean through.

“I’m done,” he said, voice sharp and final. “We’re finished. Don’t contact me again.”

He’d grabbed his shirt, stormed out, and slammed the door—feeling filthy, furious, and genuinely sick to his stomach.


<sub>Nov 26, 2025 &middot; 11:24 PM UTC</sub>

The memory dissolved, leaving him raw around the edges.

Redd drew in a slow breath, meeting Auri’s eyes with a quiet, uneasy steadiness.

“I… don’t think I ever apologized to you,” he said. His voice was low, stripped of bravado. “Not properly. Not… at all, really.”

He looked down at his hands. They didn’t quite stop fidgeting.

“I honestly thought you two were done. I really did. And when I realized—when I figured out who you were…” A soft, humorless huff escaped him. “It was a spectacularly stupid chapter of my life. And I’m sorry. Especially given… everything around that day.”

He swallowed, bracing himself, because this was the part where she might walk away—or worse, tell him exactly what he deserved to hear.

“But I didn’t come over here to make you relive all that,” he added quietly. “I was actually… kind of hoping for help.”

He lifted the textbook slightly, as if it were incriminating evidence.

“I’m good with creatures. Instincts, fieldwork, calming them, handling them—I can do all of that blindfolded. But the theory?”
His mouth twisted. “I swear I'm going to feed half these diagrams to Whimsy soon.”

He finally looked at her again, earnest in a way he rarely let himself be.

“I’m asking because I trust your judgment with this. And I could really use someone who actually knows what they’re doing. Since we’re going to be sharing a dorm now, I really don’t want this to start off on the wrong foot. If you’re not comfortable with this, just say so. I don’t want to make things awkward between us.


### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Nov 27, 2025 &middot; 03:09 AM UTC</sub>

Auri listened without interrupting, posture composed. Her expression settled into that careful, unreadable calm she used whenever she wasn’t sure how she was supposed to feel yet, and her tail curled around her the way it always did when she was thinking too hard. She studied him the way she might study a spell diagram– cautious, thorough, picking apart every angle for clues about his motivation. Was Redd telling her this because they’d be dorm mates soon? Because he needed help with studying? Or…because he genuinely meant it?

But then she saw it– the guilt, the shame, the blunt honesty of someone who wasn’t dodging responsibility or making excuses. He just felt awful. Honestly, plainly awful. Far more awful than the person who *should* have felt it in the first place.

Her ex... gods, what an embarrassing lapse of judgement, never to be duplicated. The way he acted as if her ambition was a flaw he had to tolerate? Auri knew she could be intense, so she’d put up with far more than she should have. But she didn’t have the time to waste– not then, and certainly not in the future.

Auri exhaled through her nose, a tiny huff of laughter slipping out despite herself. “Sure, and I forgive you, but before we start studying…be honest with me.” Her gaze lifted to meet Redd’s, curious and wry. “Was he…spectacularly mediocre with you, too?” She shrugged, a conspiratorial smile tugging at the corner of her mouth. “I keep wondering if I’m being too harsh, but…well, there was a point where I started choosing extra time to study and picking up extra shifts at work over…having fun…with him.”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Nov 27, 2025 &middot; 03:59 AM UTC</sub>

Redd blinked at her question, then let out a breath that was half a laugh, half a groan. “If it helps… I did all the work,” he said dryly, lifting a brow to make sure she caught the implication without pushing it too far. “I don’t know why I kept him around, to be honest.”

Auri’s conspiratorial smile loosened something tight in his chest, and a reluctant grin tugged at his own mouth.

“'Spectacularly mediocre' sounds about right,” he went on, settling deeper into the armchair. “At the time, I think I was just… stupidly hungry for attention. Validation. Anything that made me feel wanted for longer than five minutes.” His voice lowered, softer, more honest than he meant it to be. “Not my proudest era.”

Whimsy gave a tiny, judgmental hoot, and Redd muttered, “Yeah, yeah, I know,” under his breath.

He looked back at Auri, feeling the tension in the room shift into something lighter—still cautious, but no longer sharp-edged.

“But you weren’t being harsh,” he added with a small shrug. “He was… aggressively committed to underperforming.”

He cracked a faint smile, then lifted the battered textbook in mock despair.

“Anyway—thank you. For forgiving me. And for not, you know… telling me to fuck off the moment I walked over.” He cleared his throat. “Since we’re going to be living together, I’d rather not start things off feeling tense.”

He tapped the page full of swirling diagrams. “And I really would appreciate the help, if you’re still willing.”

A breath passed. Redd glanced at her notes—neat, layered, meticulous in a way his brain could never manage—and curiosity tugged at him.

“You… always study like that?” he asked gently, nodding toward her diagrams. “All this structure, color-coding, the… floating spoon situation?”

He didn’t sound mocking—more intrigued than anything, like he was trying to understand the person who would be sharing his space.

His tone stayed light, but there was genuine interest behind it—an opening for her to talk about herself, not just the disaster they’d survived.

“You always been that organized,” he asked, “or is that just… a tiefling thing?”

A wry glint flashed in his eyes.
“I wouldn’t know. You’re my first one I’m living with.”


### &#x270D; Auri
<sub>Nov 28, 2025 &middot; 05:43 AM UTC</sub>

Auri blinked at him, then gave a small, incredulous laugh. “A tiefling thing?” she echoed. “No. It’s definitely an *Auri* thing,” she clarified, tone wry but not defensive. “I have a goal in mind, and I learned early on that if I didn’t organize everything, it would drive me insane. So now everything has a system. And if it doesn’t have a system…” She gestured toward her spread of color-coded diagrams and the textbook marked with an entire constellation of tabs. “It gets one.”

Her gaze returned to him, softer now. “You’re welcome,” she added quietly. “For the forgiveness part. I don’t really see the point in holding a grudge, especially when someone is already trying to do better. Besides…” A faint smirk tugged at the corner of her mouth, “...it sounds like we both escaped something exceptionally underwhelming.”

“And yes,” she said, shifting one of her notebooks aside and gesturing for him to take a seat at the table, “I’m willing to help, and not just with studying.”

Auri hesitated for a breath, then her expression shifted– less guarded now, a spark of dry humor warming her voice. 

“It seems we have…a similar taste,” she went on. “So if either of us needs relationship advice, or a warning sign spotted early, I’d say we’re…uniquely qualified.”


### &#x270D; Redd
<sub>Dec 05, 2025 &middot; 02:51 AM UTC</sub>

Redd couldn’t help it—Auri’s comment pulled a real laugh out of him. Not sharp or mocking this time, but warm, relieved, like something finally unclenched in his chest.

“Yeah,” he said, shaking his head, the grin lingering. “Underwhelming feels generous.”

He pushed himself up from the armchair and crossed to the table when she motioned him over, settling into the seat beside her with a cautious, appreciative kind of ease. It was strange—nice, even—that she wanted the company.

When she mentioned relationship advice, he huffed out another small laugh, rubbing the back of his neck. “I mean… sure. Couldn’t hurt. I’m not exactly winning awards for relationship stability yet.” The self-deprecation was gentle, honest, not self-loathing this time. “But I’m trying. And Zurr’ka…” His voice softened at the name, the way it always did now. “He’s different. Better for me than I deserve some days.”

He glanced at her again, eyes warm with gratitude. “Still doesn’t mean I won’t need a warning sign pointed out occasionally. My track record speaks for itself.”

Whimsy fluttered down to the table with a soft thump, clearly deciding he was part of this study session, and Redd shot the owl a helpless look before turning to Auri’s notes.

“Thanks,” he murmured. “For this. All of this. It’s… nice. Getting to know my dormmate without it being a disaster.”

He leaned in a little to study her color-coded diagrams, genuinely curious now. “So,” he said, tilting his head, “which part of this beautiful chaos is supposed to explain why avian mana pathways refuse to obey basic logic? Because if you can get that into my skull, I’ll owe you a miracle.”

His tone stayed warm, open—two students settling into something that looked suspiciously like the beginning of a real friendship.
