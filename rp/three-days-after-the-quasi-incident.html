Vornakir

~Three Days after the Quasi Incident™~

It felt like months had passed since Vornakir's birthday celebration, and weeks since the effective implosion of the Quasi. They had no classes to attend, no High Courts to impress, and most importantly no investigation to continue with. For the first time since most of them arrived on campus, they finally had free time that was genuinely free. There was worry about their friends, and foes to some degree in the case of Velvyn at least on Vornakir's side of things. Nimbus. Aurum. But other than that, they all had the run of campus.

And to Vornakir, that felt like high time to pamper himself. All this stress and combat was doing numbers on his personal health. The few friends he'd made that noticed the bags under his eyes would realize how much weight he had on his shoulders not only from the investigation but all the other underlying factors he dealt with on a day to day basis. Only Redd however would truly know how different that was to the day they first met. He was so composed back then and so well groomed. He was a disheveled facsimile of the man he used to be.

If there was one other person in his dorm that understood how important things like this were, it was the only other person that cared as much about their looks as Vornakir himself did. It was clear what he needed to do with some of this free time and he didn't want to do it alone.

That's how he found himself standing outside of Sol's bedroom door. He composed himself, taking a steady breath and lifting his hand to rap the knuckles against the wooden door thrice.

Knock Kn-Knock

"Sorry for the sudden visit, Sol. But I think it's time we stop letting our hair down and getting tangled in everyone's mess for a change. It's time to put our hair up and wash away all this fucking mess. Do you fancy a bit of an impromptu spa day?" He asked the door, turning to lean his back against the wall beside Sol's door.

One leg crossed over the other and his only arm rested over his chest while he waited for Sol to decide if that was something he wanted to do. He called over his shoulder again, "Hair, nails, mage hand massages, face masks. Fuck it I'll even slice up some cucumbers for our eyes if you want." He mused.

Sol was half-draped across the couch: one leg tucked under himself, the other dangling uselessly off the side as auroral light slowly danced across the walls of his room. The events at the Quasi still echoed in his mind: the pressure, the sounds of implosions, the static of the unstable magic that refused to dissipate. He’d meant to meditate, sketch, or do literally anything productive, but instead he’d been blankly staring at the ceiling.

Knock. Kn-Knock. The sound cut through the quiet, pulling Sol out of the half-daze state he’d been drifting in.

Vornakir’s voice carried through the door, and Sol exhaled a tired laugh. He pushed himself upright with a quiet groan and rubbed at one eye as he padded barefoot across the room.

“Give me a second,” he called back, voice muffled and rough.

The door opened a moment later, and Vornakir was greeted first by the room itself. The room felt like stepping into a constellation. A night sky stretched across the ceiling, auroras of violet, sapphire, and soft emerald drifted slowly as stars rearranged themselves in subtle patterns that reflected Sol’s exhaustion. The warmth humming through the space was from a gentle, scented, magical breeze, faintly of vanilla and honey. The breeze stirred through the sheer celestial fabrics on the lofted canopy bed dressed in navy and gold silks. Beneath it sat a plush white couch and a low table atop a deep black rug. On the far side of the room was a meticulously ordered design station: mannequin, half-finished fabric, spools sorted by shimmer, tools aligned with care. Near it was a newly added lectern where a sleeping Bookie rested atop it.

The sentient book looked peaceful, with beady eyes shut and unbothered by the state of the world. His cover page rose and fell with each soft Hrrrk—shff of his muffled snore.

Then Sol stepped fully into view, revealing him in a state very few people ever saw him in: unguarded, unpolished, and clearly running on fumes.

He was wearing a black, off-the-shoulder crop top, loose enough to slip down one arm; elvish music lyrics arched across the back in silver thread, and a golden band logo was emblazoned on the front. Loose, black joggers hung low and comfortably on his hips. He wore no jewelry, his hair was yanked into a messy high bun, with stray curls escaping to frame his face, and his square gold-framed glasses sat slightly crooked on his nose.

He looked exhausted, but when he saw Vornakir, his expression softened into a sleepy smile.

“Yeah,” Sol said, leaning against the doorframe. “I could definitely use some pampering.”

As if on cue, Bookie let out another tiny snore behind him. Sol glanced behind him, amusement tugging at his mouth.

“I think his adventure in the Quasi absolutely tuckered the little guy out,” he adds with a quiet laugh. “He’s been knocked out for days now. Pretty sure he’ll still be asleep by the time we get back.”

He reached up, absently touching his hair; he winced as his fingers brushed through one of the curls, the feeling of oil coating it.

“Oh Gods,” he cringed. “Okay. Yeah. I don’t even remember the last time I washed my hair properly. This is unacceptable.”

He stepped aside, grabbing some sandals, clearly past the point of caring how he looked today. He gestured toward the hallway, a smile returning.

“Please,” he said, tone warm and grateful. “Lead the way. Save me from whatever unholy state I’m currently in.”
